White Wine and Caper Sauce


INGREDIENTS

1 x 30g of Capers

1 x 30g Cold butter

1 x 300ml Double cream

1 x 300ml Dry white wine

1 x Chopped onion

Black pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Pour white wine into a saucepan with onion and bring it to boil.

Simmer until reduced by half.

2) Add cream, seasoning and simmer again, until reduced by half.

3) Pass through a fine sieve and add capers to the sauce.

4) Simmer again for 5 minutes and add cold butter to it.

5) Serve hot with your choice of meat.

Ragu Sauce for Lasagne

Serves 4-6


INGREDIENTS

1 x 500g Beef mince

1 x 50g Smoked pancetta, roughly chopped (or use thick bacon)

1 x Red onion, peeled and finely chopped

1 x Carrot, peeled and finely chopped

1 x Stick of celery, finely chopped

3 x Cloves of garlic, peeled and crushed

1/2 x Wine glass of red wine

2 x Tablespoons tomato purée

1 x 400g tin Good-quality chopped tomatoes

1 x Beef stock cube

2 x Dried bay leaf

1 x Teaspoon of dried oregano (Optional)

1 x Teaspoon of dried marjoram (Optional)

Freshly ground black pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Heat a large pan over a medium heat, when hot, lower the heat and fry the pancetta for 2 to 3 minutes, until it starts to crisp up.

2) Stir in the onion, carrot and celery and leave to sweat with the lid on for 10 minutes, until soft and translucent.

3) Add the garlic to the pan and stir for 1 minute.

4) Add the beef to the pan, stirring occasionally until browned all over.

5) Pour in the wine and keep stirring for 2 minutes. The aim is to burn off the alcohol, leaving just a hint of the lovely wine flavour.

6) Stir in the tomato purée and chopped tomatoes and then add 200ml of boiling water.

7) Crumble in the stock cube, add the bay leaf and stir together.

8) Leave the to simmer with the lid on for 15 minutes.

9) Remove the lid and continue to simmer for a further 15 minutes.

10 Season with pepper and serve.


Tip1 Use with Lasagne

Tip2 Use with Spaghetti Bolognese

Tip3 Use with meatballs

Tip4 Try replacing the dried herbs with a sprig of fresh thyme

Tip5 Add ½ teaspoon of chilli powder for a spicier version

Easy Supreme Sauce

Serves 2 to 4


INGREDIENTS

5 x Tablespoons butter

3 x Tablespoons plain flour

1 x 300ml chicken stock (2 Knorr cubes)

1 x 150ml of double cream

1 x Tablespoons of sherry (or a squeeze of lemon)

1 x Small clove garlic, crushed

1 x Teaspoon fresh parsley (optionally use ½ teaspoon of dried)

Salt to taste

Black pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Melt butter in a saucepan over medium to low heat.

2) Add the flour and whisk for 2 to three minutes keeping the roux a light “blond” colour.

3) Add the stock in small additions, about 1/2 cup at a time, and whisk until smooth.

4) Bring the mixture to a boil then reduce the heat to low and cook for about 10 minutes until sauce is thick enough to leave a trail when the whisk is run through it.

5) Whisk in the cream and sherry (or lemon juice) and continue to cook for 2 minutes, stirring.

6) Add a little hot water if too thick.

6) Season with salt and black pepper (sprinkle with parsley if used).

7) Serve warm.


Tip 1: Add Mushroom slices to sauce for the last 2 minutes

Tip2: Works well with Chicken and pasta
