Pork Shoulder Roast


INGREDIENTS

2kg x Shoulder of pork, bone-in, skin on

1 x Sea salt to taste

1 x Feshly ground black pepper to taste

2 x Red onions, halved

2 x Carrots, peeled and halved lengthways

2 x Sticks celery, halved

1 bulb garlic, skin on, broken into cloves

6-8  x Bay leaves

600ml x Water or organic vegetable stock


METHOD

1) Preheat your oven to 220°C/425°F/gas 7.

2) Place your pork on a clean work surface, skin-side up.

3) Get yourself a small sharp knife and make scores about a centimetre apart through the skin into the fat, but not so deep that you cut into the meat.

4) If the joint is tied, try not to cut through the string. Rub salt right into all the scores you've just made, pulling the skin apart a little if you need to.

5) Brush any excess salt off the surface then turn it over.

6) Season the underside of the meat with a few pinches of salt and pepper.

7) Place your pork, skin-side up, in a roasting tray and pop in the preheated oven.

8) Roast for 30 minutes, until the skin of the pork has started to puff up and you can see it turning into crackling.

9) At this point, turn the heat down to 170°C/325°F/gas 3, cover the pork snugly with a double layer of tinfoil, pop back in the oven and roast for a further 4 and a half hours.

10) Take out of the oven, take the foil off, and baste the meat with the fat in the bottom of the tray.

11) Carefully lift the pork up and transfer to a chopping board. Spoon all but a couple of tablespoons of fat out (save it for roast potatoes!).

12) Add all the veg, garlic and bay leaves to the tray and stir them into the fat.

13) Place the pork back on top of everything and return to the stove without the foil to roast for another hour.

14) By this time the meat should be meltingly soft and tender.

15) Carefully move the meat to a serving dish, cover again with tinfoil and leave to rest while you make your gravy.

16) Spoon away any fat in the tray, then add the water or stock and place the tray on the hob.

17) Bring to the boil and simmer for a few minutes, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon to scrape up all those lovely sticky tasty bits on the bottom of the tray.

18) When you've got a nice, dark gravy, pour it through a sieve into a bowl or gravy boat, using your spoon to really push all the goodness of the veg through the sieve.

19) Add a little more salt and pepper if it needs it.

20) Serve the pork and crackling with your jug of gravy and some lovely roast potatoes.


Tip1 Roast the potatoes in the fat you spooned out of your roasting tray.

Tip2 Some stewed red cabbage and apple sauce will finish this off perfectly.

Leg of Pork Roast

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

1 x 3kg higher-welfare leg of pork

6 x Onions, peeled

2 x Tablespoon coriander seeds

1 x Tablespoon white peppercorns

6 x Bay leaves

1 x 50ml Extra virgin olive oil

1 x Bunch of sage, leaves picked

1 x Bunch of rosemary, leaves picked

30g x Plain flour

1 x Large bunch of parsley, chopped

1–2 x Tablespoon mustard


METHOD

1) Preheat your oven to full whack.

2) Score the leg with a sharp knife in a zig-zag pattern.

3) Halve the onions and spread out in a large roasting pan and place the scored pork on top.

4) Using a pestle and mortar, bash the coriander seeds, peppercorns and 2 teaspoons of sea salt until fine.

5) Add the bay leaves and bash again, followed by a good glug of olive oil to form a loose paste.

6) Rub the paste into the pork, making sure you get it into all the nooks and crannies.

7)Roast the pork for 35–40 minutes, until you have a lovely crisp crackling, then turn the oven down to 170°C/gas 3.

8) Slow-cook the leg for 3.5 hours, until the meat is easily pulled apart.

9) Dress the sage and rosemary leaves with olive oil and scatter them over the pork.

10) Pop it back in the oven and cook until the herbs are crisp.

11) Remove the leg from the oven and place on a wooden board.

12) Cover with foil and allow to rest while you make your gravy.

13) Place the roasting pan on the hob over a medium heat, add the flour and stir it into the juices so you get a sticky paste.

14) Slowly add a little water until you have a lovely rich sauce. Add the onions, then pour into a plastic jug or bowl and blitz with a hand blender.

15) Return the sauce to the pan along with the parsley and mustard. 16) Season to perfection and serve with the roast pork.

Pork Chop with Mustard Caper Sauce


INGREDIENTS

1 x Tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil

4 x 250-260g Pork loin chops (30mm plus thick)

1 x Salt and freshly ground pepper

1 1/2 x Cups chicken stock or canned low-sodium broth

1/3 x Cup drained capers

1/2 x Teaspoon finely chopped rosemary

2 x Tablespoons Dijon mustard

2 x Tablespoons unsalted butter


METHOD

1) In a large skillet, heat the olive oil until shimmering.

2) Season the pork chops with salt and pepper, add them to the skillet and cook over moderately high heat until well browned, about 5 minutes per side.

3) Transfer the pork chops to a large plate and cover loosely with foil.

4) Add the stock, capers and chopped rosemary to the skillet and boil until reduced to 3/4 cup, about 5 minutes.

5) Return the pork chops to the pan and simmer until cooked through, about 3 minutes.

6) Transfer the pork chops to 4 plates. Whisk the mustard and butter into the sauce and season with salt and pepper.

7) Pour the mustard-caper sauce over the chops and serve.
