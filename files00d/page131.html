Perfect Roast Beef

Serves 6-8


INGREDIENTS

1.5 kg x Topside of beef (or use Rib)

2 x Medium onions

2 x Carrots

2 x Sticks celery

1 x Bulb garlic

1  Small bunch fresh thyme, rosemary, bay or sage, or a mixture

1 x 50ml Olive oil

1 x Sea salt to taste

1 x Freshly ground black pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Take your beef out of the fridge 30 minutes before it goes into the oven.

2) Preheat your oven to 240°C/475°F/ gas 9.

3) There's no need to peel the vegetables – just give them a wash and roughly chop them.

3) Break the garlic bulb into cloves, leaving them unpeeled.

4) Pile all the veg, garlic and herbs into the middle of a large roasting tray and drizzle with olive oil.

5) Drizzle the beef with olive oil and season well with salt and pepper, rubbing it all over the meat.

6) Place the beef on top of the vegetables.

7) Place the roasting tray in the preheated oven.

8) Turn the heat down immediately to 200°C/400°F/gas 6 and cook for 1 hour for medium beef.

9) If you prefer it medium-rare, take it out 5 to 10 minutes earlier.

10) For well done, leave it in for another 10 to 15 minutes.

11) If you're doing roast potatoes and veggies, this is the time to crack on with them – get them into the oven for the last 45 minutes of cooking.

12)Baste the beef halfway through cooking and if the veg look dry, add a splash of water to the tray to stop them burning.

13) When the beef is cooked to your liking, take the tray out of the oven and transfer the beef to a board to rest for 15 minutes or so.

14) Cover it with a layer of tinfoil and a tea towel and put aside while you make your gravy, horseradish sauce and Yorkshire puddings.

15) Slice and serve.


Tip1 Replace all of the olive oil with beef dripping.

Basic Steak Pie


INGREDIENTS

25g x Beef dripping

25g x Plain flour

1 x Black pepper to taste

700g x Braising steak cut into small pieces

1 x Onion finely chopped

225g x Button mushrooms halved (optional)

3 x Beef cubes (Oxo or similar)

600ml x Boiling water

1 x Tablespoon Worcestershire sauce

225g x Plain flour

100g x Salted butter

3-4 x Tablespoons of cold water


METHOD

1) Heat the oil in a large frying pan.

2) Sift the flour into a small bowl and season with salt and pepper.

3) Toss the meat pieces in the flour to lightly coat and then add the meat to the frying pan (keep the remaining seasoned flour for later).

4) Fry the meat in the oil until brown and then add the onion and optional mushrooms.

5) Sprinkle the remaining flour into the pan and cook for a further 2 to 3 minutes.

6) Add the Worcestershire sauce and add more seasoning.

7) Add the stock cubes to the boiling water and stir until dissolved.

8) Add to the pan so that the meat is covered.

9) Leave this to simmer for about 30 minutes or until the sauce 10) thickens.

10) Preheat the oven to 150C.

11) Sift the flour and salt into a mixing bowl.

12) Add the butter and cut into the flour using a knife and then rub in with fingers.

13) The mixture should look like fine breadcrumbs.

14) Slowly add cold water to the crumbs and mix to a stiff crumbly looking paste.

15) Draw together with fingertips, turn out onto a lightly floured work surface and knead quickly until smooth and crack free.

16) Once the filling ready (meat soft) pour the mixture into a pie dish.

17) Roll out the pastry so that is big enough to cover the pie dish.

18) The thinner you roll it out the crispier your pastry will be.

19) Cover the pie dish with the pastry and pierce a few times so that air can escape.

20) Trim any excess pastry from the edges.

21) Beat the egg in a cup and brush it all over the pastry.

22) Cook in the oven at 150c for 40 minutes.

23) Turn the temperature up to 200-220c and cook for a further 20 minutes or until golden brown.

24) Serve.

Tip1 If your frying pan is quite small then you may find it easier to transfer the meat mixture to a bigger saucepan before adding the stock.

Roasted Brisket

Serves 6-8


INGREDIENTS
1 x 3kg piece of rolled brisket about 150cm in diameter with some fat
1 x 50g Beef dripping
1 x Teaspoon ground cumin

1 x Teaspoon of ground coriander

½ x Teaspoon of ground cinnamon
1 x Teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper

4 x cloves of garlic sliced in half longways

6 x Bay leaves


METHOD
1) Remove beef from the fridge at least 1 hour before cooking.

2) Preheat the oven to 150°C about 15 minutes before cooking is to begin.

3) Lay a small piece of aluminium foil in the roasting tin and 3 large sheets of aluminium foil, enough to seal in the beef, on top of the small sheet in the roasting tin.

4) Rinse and dry the beef then place on the foil.

5) Melt the beef dripping then add the cumin, coriander, cinnamon and lots of freshly ground black pepper.

6) Spoon the spiced dripping over the top and sides of the beef.

7) Lay the bay leaves and garlic on top of the meat.

8) Pull up the foil at the sides and ends of the joint, pinching and folding the edges to make a good seal.

9) Put the parcel in a roasting tin and cook for around 6 to 7 hours at 150C until the beef feels very soft to the touch.

10) If you’re in a hurry, it will cook in around half that time at 180°C.

11) When the beef is done, leave it in the turned-off oven for 20 minutes to ‘rest’.

12) Open the parcel and carefully lift the beef onto a platter without piercing it to prevent loss of juices.

13) Pour the liquid into a jug and spoon off of some of the fat (use for gravy).

14) Serve in thick slices with gravy.


Tip1: Serve with horseradish sauce

Tip2: Use the broth as a basis for onion gravy
