Gnocchi in a Pesto Sauce

Serves: 4

Preparation Time: 30 Mins

Cooking Time: 30 Mins


INGREDIENTS

For the gnocchi:

1 x 500g Floury potatoes (large baking potatoes are best or King Edward or Maris Piper)

1 x Salt to taste

2 x Medium eggs, preferably free-range or organic

1 x 200g plain flour, plus extra to dust

1 x Polenta, to dust

For the Pesto:

1 x 50g Fresh basil, leaves picked

2 x Cloves of garlic, peeled and crushed

1 x 50g Pine nuts

1 x 50g Freshly grated Parmesan or pecorino cheese

8 x Tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, plus extra storing (optional)

1 x Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste


METHOD:

1) Bring a large pan of salted water to the boil over a high heat.

2) Add the unpeeled potatoes and cook until tender; this could take anywhere from 30 minutes to 1 hour, depending on the size of your potatoes. They are ready when they fall off the blade of a knife easily. It’s important to cook the potatoes with their skins on, as this helps prevent excess moisture getting in.

3) Drain the potatoes and peel them while they’re still hot – use a tea towel to help hold them.

4) Mash the potatoes while they are still hot, too. Try to make the mash as smooth as possible.

5) Season with a pinch of salt and leave to cool for 10 minutes.

6) Add an egg and 150g flour to the mash and mix to form a malleable dough. It needs to be firm and not too sticky. If it seems too soft, add some extra flour; if it is too stiff, add the second egg.

7) Taste and add more salt if needed.

8) Dust the work surface with flour.

9) Take off a handful of dough and roll it into a long sausage shape – the narrower the better.

10) Using a knife, work your way along the dough cutting it into small, evenly sized squares, about 1cm wide.

11) Place your finished gnocchi on trays dusted with polenta. Be careful not to overcrowd the trays or allow them to touch each other as they will stick together in a clump. (At this point you can freeze the gnocchi and once frozen bag them up to save on storage.)

12) Bring a large pan of salted water to the boil to get ready to cook the gnocchi.

To make your pesto:

13) Put the basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmesan and oil in a food processor, and blitz until smooth.

14) Season, to taste, with salt and pepper.

15) Carefully place the gnocchi in the boiling water and cook for about 2 minutes, until they start floating to the top – when they float that means they will be cooked!

16) Using a spider or a slotted spoon, transfer the gnocchi to a serving dish.

17) Mix in your pesto and serve.

Ratatouille Provençale

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

2 x Aubergines chopped into 2cm cubes

2 x Courgettes chopped into 2cm cubes

2 x Peppers (1 red, 1 green) sliced long ways into thin strips

6 x Large tomatoes chopped into quarters

2 x Onions chopped into thin slices

2 to 3 x Cloves of garlic or more to taste

1 to 2 x Small chilli deseeded and scraped to remove pith (optional)

4 x Tablespoons olive oil

Black Pepper to taste

Salt if using


METHOD

1) The key to a successful ratatouille is the heat; it needs a low to medium heat. It takes a long time to cook such that the veggies melt and do NOT burn.

2) Heat the oil in a large, deep pan.

3) When it's hot, put the onion and cook for 20-30mins at medium heat until they become golden and smell absolutely gorgeous.

4) Add the garlic and chilli and stir for 2 to 3 mins.

5) Add the tomatoes on a medium heat until they start to melt and create a juice.

6) Add the peppers and stir the cover the pan with the lid to avoid evaporation.

7) Let the vegetables cook slowly until the peppers are soft, the onions are NOT browning and the tomatoes have wrinkled skin with bits of flesh still attached.

8) Add the courgettes, stir then replace the pan lid and cook for about 10mins until soft.

9) Keep the vegetables at a medium wetness by removing the pan lid as appropriate.

10) Add the aubergines stir and replace the pan lid.

11) Your ratatouille will be ready when the aubergines are soft and melting after about 10 mins.

12) Add pepper and salt to taste and stir gently before serving.


Tip1: Great with soft poached or fried eggs

Lemon Rice Greek

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

2 x Tablespoons olive oil

1/2 x Medium yellow onion, chopped (red onion will also work)

1 x Cup (250ml) basmati rice, soaked and drained or thoroughly washed

2 x Cloves garlic, minced

2 x Teaspoons lemon zest

1 x Cup chicken broth (or vegetable broth for a vegetarian/vegan version)

1/4 x Cup freshly squeezed lemon juice

1 x Tablespoon fresh dill, or to taste

1 x Tablespoon fresh parsley, or to taste

1/4 x Teaspoon ground cumin

1/4 x Teaspoon dried oregano

1/4 x Teaspoon turmeric, required for the bright yellow colour

1 x Salt and pepper, to taste

1 x Tablespoon unsalted butter (optional, for serving)

1 x Extra lemon juice (optional, for serving)


METHOD

1) Sauté onions and garlic in a large saucepan over medium heat with olive oil until soft and fragrant, about 4-5 minutes.

2) Add the rice and lemon zest, sauté until slightly toasted, about 1 minute, stirring frequently.

3) Pour in the broth, lemon juice, dill, parsley, cumin, oregano, salt, pepper, and turmeric, mix and bring to a boil uncovered.

4) Reduce heat to low, cover and cook until the liquids have evaporated and the rice is tender but not sticky, about 10-15 minutes.

5) Stir, then taste and adjust seasonings, if desired.

6) Let sit covered for 10 minutes off the heat.

7) Uncover and fluff before serving.

8) Stir in butter and/or top with extra lemon juice.


Tip1: Add crumbled feta cheese
