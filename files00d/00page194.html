Lemon Pepper Dal

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x 200g Red lentils

2 x Tablespoon ghee

1 x Medium onion, grated

1 x Tablespoon grated ginger

2 x Garlic cloves, crushed

1 x Large tomato, chopped

½ x Teaspoon ground turmeric

½ x Teaspoon chilli powder

½ x Teaspoon ground coriander

½ x Teaspoon garam masala

1½ x Teaspoon salt

1 x Litre boiling water

1 x Pinch bicarbonate of soda

2 x Teaspoon lemon juice

1 x Tablespoon chopped fresh coriander, to garnish

For the tarka

2 x Tablespoon ghee

1 x Teaspoon cumin seeds

½x  Teaspoon ground black pepper

8–10 x Fresh curry leaves

1 x Tablespoon chopped coriander stalks

1 x Large lemon, zest only


METHOD

1) Wash the lentils in 2–3 changes of water.

2) In a bowl, cover the lentils with enough boiling water to completely submerge and allow to soak for15–20 minutes while you prepare the rest of the ingredients.

3) Melt the ghee in a large pan and add the onions. Sauté for 10–12 minutes until the onions begin to caramelise and turn a light golden brown colour.

4) Add the ginger, garlic, tomato, turmeric, chilli powder, ground coriander, garam masala and salt. Stir well and cook over a medium heat for 2–3 minutes.

5) Drain the lentils of the soaking water and add them to the pan, along with 1 litre boiling water and the bicarbonate of soda (this will help soften the lentils quickly). Give everything a quick stir and cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid.

6) Cook over a medium-low heat for 25 minutes, or until the lentils have completely softened. Stir often to prevent the dal from sticking to the base of the pan.

7) Once the dal is cooked, add the lemon juice and beat with a balloon whisk to give it a creamy texture. If it feels too thick, add boiling water until the dal reaches your preferred consistency.

8) Make the tarka in a separate small pan

9) Heat the ghee over a medium-high heat.

10) Once the ghee is hot (not smoking), in quick succession add: cumin, black pepper, curry leaves (they will crackle so stand back), coriander stalks and lemon zest. After a few seconds, switch the heat off and immediately pour this over the hot daal.

11) Stir well and serve garnished with chopped coriander leaves.

Chicken Karahi No Onions Quick

Serves 3


INGREDIENTS

100-150ml x Ghee (or vegetable oil)

600g x Chicken, boneless thigh skin removed

650g x Best quality vine tomatoes (Cut in half)

1 x Large handful of coriander

4-10 x Green chillies (I used 5)

3 x Tablespoons fresh Garlic/Ginger paste (NOT FROM JARS), alternatively use 3 frozen garlic/ginger cubes.

1 x Teaspoon salt, to taste

2 x Teaspoons fresh black pepper. (peppercorns roughly ground)

2  xTeaspoons Coriander Powder

1 x Teaspoon Cumin Powder

1 x Heaped teaspoon Good quality Kashmiri powder (For deeper red, use double)

1/2 x Teaspoon Garam Masala

2 x Tablespoons natural Greek Yogurt

2 x Tablespoons butter

1 x Large handful fresh coriander

3cm x Ginger cut into thin strips


METHOD

1) Add ghee to wok on high heat

2) When hot add the chicken and fry for 2 to 3 mins

3) Add tomatoes, garlic/ginger paste, coriander leaves, green chillies, salt, black pepper cook slowly for 10 mins with lid

4) Add coriander powder, cumin powder, Kashmiri chili powder, garam masala stir and cook, slowly for 10 mins without lid

5) Cook on a low heat for 10mins until sauce thickens

6) Serve with thin ginger strips and fresh coriander leaf garnish

Quick Beef Curry Pressure Cooker

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x 500g Shin beef chopped into large cubes

6 x Tablespoons of ghee (or oil)

½ x Teaspoon of fresh ground black pepper

2 x Onions medium, peeled and rough chopped

2 x Teaspoons of garam masala

1 x Teaspoon of turmeric

½ x Teaspoon of chilli (optional)

1 x Teaspoon of cumin seeds (whole)

2 x Green (or red) medium chillies, finely chopped

1 x 6cm Fresh root ginger (frozen is good) peeled and finely chopped

6 x Cloves garlic finely chopped

x Cup of fresh coriander rough chopped (stems are good)

½ x Tomatoes (1/2 can juice only)

½ x Cup fresh natural yoghurt

1 x ½ Cup of water

½ x Teaspoon of salt

½ x Teaspoon of sugar


METHOD

1) Melt the ghee in the pressure cooker.

2) Fry the chopped onions on medium heat until soft and starting to brown.

3) Add the garam masala, turmeric, cumin seeds and chilli (if using), cook for 2 to 3mins.

4) Add the beef and brown.

5) Add the garlic, ginger and green chilli, cook for 2 to 3mins.

6) Add the tomatoes/juice and cook for 5 to 6mins.

7) Add the yoghurt and stir well.

8) Add the water and stir well.

9) Add salt and sugar.

10) Pressure cook, on high, for circa 20mins

11) Stir in the coriander before serving.


Tip1 Substitute lamb or other cuts of meat.

Tip2 In a heavy pan cook for circa 45min.

Tip3 Great with parathas (buy frozen and cook in mins).
