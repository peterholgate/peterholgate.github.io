Chana Masaledar - Serves 12



Ingredients


1 x 50ml Ghee (or Oil)

1 x 500g Dried chickpeas soaked overnight in water

1 x Teaspoon cumin Seeds

3 x Teaspoon garam masala

3 x Teaspoon ground Coriander

¾ x Teaspoon cayenne pepper

1 x Teaspoon salt

3 x Medium onions chopped

1 x 5cm Piece of  fresh root ginger grated

6 x Cloves garlic crushed  

3 x Tablespoons tomato puree

1 x Juice of a lemon


Method


1) Boil chickpeas in water for about 60 to 70 mins until tender (remove any froth).

2)Turn off heat cover and leave until required.

3) Add oil to a large frying pan and heat on a medium flame until the oil is hot.

4) Add the cumin seeds and when they turn dark add the onion.

5) Fry the onion for about 10 mins or until golden brown.

6) Turn the heat down and add the garam masala and ground coriander, stir fry for about 3 mins.

7) Add the garlic and ginger and stir fry for 3 mins.

8) Add the tomato puree and stir again for about 1 min.

9) Transfer to a large, heavy pan.

10) Drain the chickpeas, reserving the liquid and add about 400mls of the liquid to the large pan.

11) Add the chickpeas, cayenne and lemon juice.

12) Mix well and cook gently, stirring for about 30 mins.

13) Add additional chickpea liquid as required to avoid drying out.

14) Stir gently to avoid breaking the chickpeas.

15) Eat as an accompaniment to other Indian food or with Indian bread and pickles as a snack.


Tip 1 Good to freeze

Tip 2 Large iron pans recommended

Tip 3 Chana = Chickpeas/Garbazos

Frittata with Spinach - Serves 4


Ingredients


1/2 x Small red onion peeled and finely sliced

1 x Clove of Garlic chopped

2 x Tablespoon olive oil

1 x Cup drained and squeezed chopped defrosted frozen spinach or…

…about 450g (16oz) of fresh spinach

4 x Large eggs

1/2 x Cup of pannacotta (or crumbled feta cheese or grated Cheddar Cheese)

Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Dash of Chilli Sauce (hot tabasco type) or more to taste


Method


1) Preheat a 20cm (8in) frying with enough oil in the pan to very lightly coat the bottom.

2) Add the onion and cook until just starting to brown.

3) Add the spinach and garlic and toss for a 2 to 3 mins to heat through, then remove from the heat and allow the vegetables to cool slightly.

3) Beat the eggs in a large bowl.

4) Add in the cooled spinach and red onion, followed by the feta (or other cheese), a good pinch of pepper and a dash of chilli sauce.

5) Pour the mixture into a deep oven dish.

6) Cook in a preheated oven at 160 C for about 20 mins or until nearly set (shake the dish to check).

7) Turn up the heat to 180 C and cook for a further 5 mins or until lightly brown on top.


Serve hot or cold.


Tip 1: Substitute an equal amount of other cooked vegetables for the spinach.


Tip 2: Really good with roasted mixed veg.
