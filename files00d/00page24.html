Chana Masaledar - Serves 12


Ingredients

1 x 50ml Ghee (or Oil)

1 x 500g Dried chickpeas soaked overnight in water

1 x Teaspoon cumin Seeds

3 x Teaspoon garam masala

3 x Teaspoon ground Coriander

¾ x Teaspoon cayenne pepper

1 x Teaspoon salt

3 x Medium onions chopped

1 x 5cm Piece of  fresh root ginger grated

6 x Cloves garlic crushed  

3 x Tablespoons tomato puree

1 x Juice of a lemon


Method

1) Boil chickpeas in water for about 60 to 70 mins until tender (remove any froth).

2)Turn off heat cover and leave until required.

3) Add oil to a large frying pan and heat on a medium flame until the oil is hot.

4) Add the cumin seeds and when they turn dark add the onion.

5) Fry the onion for about 10 mins or until golden brown.

6) Turn the heat down and add the garam masala and ground coriander, stir fry for about 3 mins.

7) Add the garlic and ginger and stir fry for 3 mins.

8) Add the tomato puree and stir again for about 1 min.

9) Transfer to a large, heavy pan.

10) Drain the chickpeas, reserving the liquid and add about 400mls of the liquid to the large pan.

11) Add the chickpeas, cayenne and lemon juice.

12) Mix well and cook gently, stirring for about 30 mins.

13) Add additional chickpea liquid as to avoid drying out.

14) Stir gently to avoid breaking the chickpeas.

15) Eat as an accompaniment to other Indian food or with Indian bread and pickles as a snack.


2017-VEG-004-Chana Masaledar


Tip 1: Good to freeze

Tip 2: Large iron pans recommended

Tip 3: Chana = Chickpeas/Garbanzos




Frittata with Spinach - Serves 4


Ingredients

1/2 x Small red onion peeled and finely sliced

1 x Clove of Garlic chopped

2 x Tablespoon olive oil

1 x Cup drained and squeezed chopped defrosted frozen spinach or…

…about 450g (16oz) of fresh spinach

4 x Large eggs

1/2 x Cup of pannacotta (or crumbled feta cheese or grated Cheddar Cheese)

Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Dash of Chilli Sauce (hot tabasco type) or more to taste


Method

1) Preheat a 20cm (8in) frying with enough oil in the pan to very lightly coat the bottom.

2) Add the onion and cook until just starting to brown.

3) Add the spinach and garlic and toss for a 2 to 3 mins to heat through, then remove from the heat and allow the vegetables to cool slightly.

3) Beat the eggs in a large bowl.

4) Add in the cooled spinach and red onion, followed by the feta (or other cheese), a good pinch of pepper and a dash of chilli sauce.

5) Pour the mixture into a deep oven dish.

6) Cook in a preheated oven at 160 C for about 20 mins or until nearly set (shake the dish to check).

7) Turn up the heat to 180 C and cook for a further 5 mins or until lightly brown on top.


2014-VEG-001-Spinach Frittata


Tip 1: Substitute an equal amount of other cooked vegetables for the spinach.

Tip 2: Really good with roasted mixed veg.

Tip3: Serve hot or cold.


    

Ratatouille Provençale – Serves 4


Ingredients

2 x Aubergines chopped into 2cm cubes

2 x Courgettes chopped into 2cm cubes

2 x Peppers (1 red, 1 green) sliced long ways into thin strips

6 x Large tomatoes chopped into quarters

2 x Onions chopped into thin slices

2 to 3 x Cloves of garlic or more to taste

1 to 2 x Small chilli deseeded and scraped to remove pith (optional)

4 x Tablespoons olive oil

Black Pepper to taste

Salt if using


Method

1) The key to a successful ratatouille is the heat; it needs a low to medium heat. It takes a long time to cook such that the veggies melt and do NOT burn.

2) Heat the oil in a large, deep pan.

3) When it's hot, put the onion and cook for 20-30mins at medium heat until they become golden and smell absolutely gorgeous.

4) Add the garlic and chilli and stir for 2 to 3 mins.

5) Add the tomatoes on a medium heat until they start to melt and create a juice.

6) Add the peppers and stir the cover the pan with the lid to avoid evaporation.

7) Let the vegetables cook slowly until the peppers are soft, the onions are NOT browning and the tomatoes have wrinkled skin with bits of flesh still attached.

8) Add the courgettes, stir then replace the pan lid and cook for about 10mins until soft.

9) Keep the vegetables at a medium wetness by removing the pan lid as appropriate.

10) Add the aubergines stir and replace the pan lid.

11) Your ratatouille will be ready when the aubergines are soft and melting after about 10 mins.

12) Add pepper and salt to taste and stir gently before serving.


2024-VEG-011-Ratatouille Provençale


Tip1: Great with soft poached or fried eggs




Hummus - Makes 6 to 8 Cups


Ingredients

4 x 240g Tins chickpeas (drained weight) or use 500g of dried chickpeas soaked overnight and boiled in water for about 45min.

1 x 200g Tahini paste

2 x Lemons, quartered

8 x Cloves of garlic crushed

1 x 500ml Cold water (pre-boiled)

2 x Teaspoons of fresh cumin powder (optional)

1 x Teaspoon of salt (optional)


Method

1) Add the Tahini to a large mixing bowl.

2) Add the garlic.

3) Pre-warm and squeeze in the juice of the lemons (the tahini will tighten up).

4) Mix well and add water to make a loose paste.

5) Add the drained chickpeas and mix.

6) Use a handheld blender to blend and mix to a smooth paste.

7) Add more water if the paste is too stiff.

8) Add salt to taste and blend or mix again.

9) Add the cumin if using.

10) Transfer to disposable plastic cups and cover with food film.

11) Serve in a bowl with a topping of olive oil (add cumin, chilli or paprika for extra flavour).


2015-VEG-002-Hummus


Tip 1: Freezes well.

Tip2: Almost impossible to mix in a ‘normal’ blender.
