Salmon Ceviche

Serves 2


INGREDIENTS

250g x Fresh, unsmoked salmon prepared as below

1+1/2 x Lemons juice only

1 x Lime juice only

1/2 x Orange juice only

1/2 x Small red onion, very finely chopped

1/2 x Red bird's eye chili pepper, or 1 red cayenne peppers, very finely chopped

1 x Teaspoon soft brown sugar (this is to balance the flavour of the acid / citrus juices)

1 x Pinch salt

1 x Fresh finely ground black pepper (to taste)

3 x Tablespoons x Fresh coriander, rough chopped

1/2 x Ripe avocado sliced lengthways (optional)

1/2 x Mango cut into small squares (optional)


METHOD

1) Skin the fillets and remove the pin bones.

2) Work over each piece with your fingers, making sure that it is completely boneless, skinless and completely free of the thickish membrane around the belly area.

3) Cut fish fillets into thin strips so that the texture of the fish can be felt while consuming.

4) Sieve the citrus juice into a deep glass or pottery dish (not metal) then add the onion, chili, brown sugar, salt, freshly ground black pepper and coriander.

5) Add the fish and mixed gently making sure that the fish is completely submerged in the liquid.

6) Put in the fridge to marinate for a minimum of 1 hour, maximum of 12 hours; 3 to 4 hours is just about perfect.

7) You'll see the fish flesh become opaque as the citrus juice cooks it.

8) Taste the ceviche and adjust the seasonings; you might feel the need for a little more chili, sugar, or salt.

9) The flavour balancing act of this dish is of great importance.

10) Serve the ceviche in a bowl with juices over 2 to 3 slices of ripe avocado.

11) Optional, sprinkle with very small dices of mango.

12) Garnish with fresh coriander sprig.


Tip1 This dish also works with scallops, bream, bass, sea trout etc.

Chilli Con Carne

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

2 x Medium onions

2 x Cloves garlic

2 x Medium carrots

2 x Sticks celery

2 x Red peppers

1 x 50ml Olive oil

1 x Heaped teaspoon chilli powder

1 x Heaped teaspoon ground cumin

1 x Heaped teaspoon ground cinnamon

1 x Salt to taste

1 x Freshly ground black pepper to taste

400g x Tinned chickpeas

400g x Tinned red kidney beans

2 x 400g x Tinned chopped tomatoes

500g x Minced beef

1 x Small bunch fresh coriander

2 x Tablespoons balsamic vinegar

400g x Basmati rice

230g x Guacamole

1 x 50ml Soured Cream

1 x Lime


METHOD

1) Peel and finely chop the onions, garlic, carrots, red peppers and celery.

2) Place your largest casserole-type pan on a medium high heat.

3) Add 2 lugs of olive oil and all your chopped vegetables.

4) Add the chilli powder, cumin and cinnamon with a good pinch of salt and pepper.

5) Stir every 30 seconds for around 7 minutes until softened and lightly coloured.
6) Add the drained chickpeas, drained kidney beans and the tinned tomatoes.

7) Add the minced beef, breaking any larger chunks up with a wooden spoon.

8) Fill one of the empty tomato tins with water and pour this into the pan.

9) Pick the coriander leaves and place them in the fridge. Finely chop the washed stalks and stir in.

10) Add the balsamic vinegar and season with a good pinch of salt and pepper.

11) Bring to the boil and turn the heat down to a simmer with a lid slightly askew for about an hour, stirring every now and again to stop it catching.
12) Serve with rice, soured cream and fresh coriander garnish.

Empanadas Colombianas

Makes about Twenty, 8cm empanadas


INGREDIENTS

1 x 50ml Olive oil for frying

1 x Lime for serving

1 x Ají picante for serving

For Dough or Masa:

1½ x Cups precooked yellow cornmeal (masarepa)

2 x Cups water

1 x Tablespoon vegetable oil

½ x Tablespoon sazon Goya with azafran

½ x Teaspoon Salt

For Filling:

2 x Cups peeled and diced white potatoes

1 x Chicken or vegetable bouillon tablet

1 x Tablespoon olive oil

¼ x Cup chopped white onions

1 x Cup chopped tomato

½ x Teaspoon salt

¼ x Cup chopped green onions

1 x Chopped garlic clove

2 x Tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro

2 x Tablespoon chopped red bell pepper

¼ x Teaspoon black pepper

½ x Pound ground pork and beef


METHOD

To prepare the dough:

1) Place the masarepa in a large bowl.

2) Add the sazon Goya and salt and stir to mix well.

3) Add the water and oil and mix to form dough.

4) Pat the dough into a ball and knead for 2 minutes or until smooth. 5) Cover with plastic and set aside for 20 minutes.

To make the filling:

6) Cook the potatoes in a pot with water and the bouillon tablet for 20-25 minutes or until tender.

7) Drain, gently mash the potatoes and set aside.

8) Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large, heavy skillet.

9) Add the onion and cook over medium-low heat stirring frequently, for 5 minutes.

10) Add the tomatoes, green onions, garlic, bell pepper, cilantro, salt and black pepper. Cook for about 15 minutes.

11) Add the ground pork and beef. Cook, breaking up the meat with a wooden spoon, for 10 to 15 minutes or until the mixture is fairly dry.

12) Transfer the meat mixture to the mashed potatoes bowl and mix well to combine.

13) Break small portions of the dough, about 1 ½ tablespoons each one, and form each portion into a ball by rolling between the palms of your hands.

14) Place the balls of dough between two pieces of plastic and roll each out very thinly to form a circle.

15) Remove the top plastic and place 1 tablespoon of the filling in the center of each.

16) Then using the plastic underneath, fold the dough over to enclose the filling, forming a half circle.

17) Tightly seal the edges by crimping with the tines of a fork.

18)  Fill a large pot with vegetable oil and heat over medium heat to 360° F.

19) Carefully place 3 or 4 empanadas at the time in the heated oil and fry for about 2 minutes until golden on all sides.

20) Using a slotted spoon transfer the empanadas to a plate lined with paper towels.

21) Serve with ají picante and lime wedges on the side.
