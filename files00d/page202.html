Pakoras (Bhajis) Vegetable LCHF


INGREDIENTS

3 x Tablespoon ghee

1 x Pan with Ghee or other oil/fat for deep frying

1 x 100g Besan (Gram) Flour

1/2 x Teaspoon of Cumin Powder

½ x Teaspoon of Turmeric Powder

¼ x Teaspoon of Cayenne or Chopped hot chili

1/2 x Teaspoon of Black Pepper

½ x Teaspoon of Baking Soda

1 x 250ml of Water

1 x Small bunch of coriander leaf

4 x Medium Onions, finely sliced into rings

or

2 x Medium Courgettes, spiralised or cut into thin strips

or

2 x Bell Peppers, finely sliced


METHOD

1) Add water to the Besan flour in a large bowl to form a thick, smooth batter

2) Add the Cumin, Turmeric, Black Pepper, Cayenne and Baking Soda, mix well

3) Add half a teacup of coriander leaf (optional)

4) Pre-Fry the vegetables, Onions, Courgettes or Peppers, in ghee for a few minutes until soft

5) Cool then stir the vegetables into the batter mixture

6) Pre-heat the pan of cooking fat to a medium heat

7) Add the vegetable and batter mix 1 x heaped teaspoon at a time to form a single layer only

8) Fry for about 5 minutes on each side, until crispy and light brown on the outside

9) Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on kitchen paper ready to serve

10) Serve with riata, chutney fresh chopped coriander or similar

PFH Chana Masaledar (Chickpeas/Garbanzos)

Serves 12


INGREDIENTS

1 x 50ml Ghee (or Oil)

1 x 500g Dried chickpeas soaked overnight in water

1 x Teaspoon cumin Seeds

3 x Teaspoon garam masala

3 x Teaspoon ground Coriander

¾ x Teaspoon cayenne pepper

1 x Teaspoon salt

3 x Medium onions chopped

1 x 5cm Piece of  fresh root ginger grated

6 x Cloves garlic crushed  

3 x Tablespoons tomato puree

1 x Juice of a lemon


METHOD

1) Boil chickpeas in water for about 60 to 70 mins until tender (remove any froth).

2) Turn off heat cover and leave until required.

3) Add oil to a large heavy frying pot and heat on a medium flame until the oil is hot.

4) Add the cumin seeds and when they turn dark add the onion.

5) Fry the onion for about 10 mins or until golden brown.

6) Turn the heat down and add the garam masala and ground coriander, stir fry for about 3 mins.

7) Add the garlic and ginger and stir fry for a further 3 mins.

8) Add the tomato puree and stir again for about 1 min.

9) Drain the chickpeas, reserving the liquid and add about 400mls of the liquid to the pan.

10) Add the chickpeas, cayenne and lemon juice.

11) Mix well and cook gently, stirring occasionally for about 30 mins.

12) Add additional chickpea liquid as required to avoid drying out.

13) Stir gently to avoid breaking the chickpeas.

14) Serve as an accompaniment to other Indian food or with Indian bread and pickles as a snack.


Tip 1 Good to freeze

Tip 2  Large iron pot recommended

Stir Fry Pork with Ginger and Honey


INGREDIENTS

2 x Nests medium egg noodles

2 x Teasoon cornflour

2 x Tablespoons soy sauce

1 x Tablesoon honey

1 x Tablespoon olive oil

250g x Pork tenderloin, cut into bite-sized pieces

1 x Thumb-sized piece ginger, finely chopped

2 x Garlic cloves, finely chopped

1 x Green pepper, deseeded and sliced

100g x Mangetout

1 x Teaspoon sesame seed


METHOD

1) Bring a pan of salted water to the boil and cook the noodles following pack instructions.

2) Meanwhile, mix the cornflour with 1 tbsp water, then stir in the soy sauce and honey, and set aside.

3) Heat the oil in a wok over a high heat. Add the pork and cook for 2 mins until browned all over.

4) Add the ginger, garlic, pepper and mangetout, and cook for a further 2 mins.

5) Reduce the heat, then add the soy and honey mixture, stirring and cooking until the sauce bubbles and thickens.

6) Divide the drained noodles between 2 bowls.

7) Top with the pork and vegetables, and finish with a sprinkling of sesame seeds.

8) Serve.
