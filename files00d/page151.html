Turkey Gravy

Serves 6-8


INGREDIENTS

2 x Celery sticks, trimmed and roughly chopped

2 x Carrots, roughly sliced

2 x Onions, peeled and quartered

5 x Fresh bay leaves

5 x Fresh sage leaves

4 x Sprigs of fresh rosemary

2  x Star anise

2 x Rashers of smoked streaky bacon

8 x Chicken wings

1 x Olive oil

1 x Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

4 x Tablespoons plain flour

1 x 60ml Sherry or port (optional)

2 x Heaped dessertspoons cranberry sauce, for finishing


METHOD

1) Preheat the oven to 200ºC/400ºF/gas 6.

2) Put the veg, herbs and star anise into a roasting tray and scatter the bacon on top.

3) Break the chicken wings open and bash the bones up with the end of a rolling pin.

4) Add them in the pan, drizzle with olive oil, season, then toss everything together.

5) Put the tray in the oven to cook for 1 hour, or until the meat is falling off the bone.

6) Take the pan out of the oven, and put it on a hob over a low heat and use a potato masher to really grind and mash everything up.

7) Gradually mix in the flour to thicken the mixture.

8) The longer you let everything fry, the darker your gravy will be.

9) When the flour is combined pour in 2 litres of hot water, turn the heat up and bring to the boil for 10 minutes, till thickened.

10) Turn down the heat and simmer for about 25 minutes, stirring occasionally.

11) Add 60ml sherry or port for flavour.

12) Push and mash everything through so you get as much flavour as possible.

13) Discard anything left behind.

14) Add to the juices in the turkey baking tray after turkey is removed d bring it to the boil over the hob and scrape all those flavourful bits and pieces from the bottom of the pan.

15) Add the cranberry sauce. It won't taste sweet but it will add a wicked background flavour.

16) When gravy is piping hot, strain it through a sieve and into a pan.

17) Simmer until ready to serve.

18) Skim off fat and add any extra resting juices from the turkey before serving.

Caper Sauce for Fish


INGREDIENTS

1 x Tablespoon of capers

4 x Tablespoons of butter

1 ½ x Fresh lemon juice

1 x Teaspoon of chopped parsley

Pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Melt butter in a pan and add capers, lemon juice, parsley and pepper.

2) Heat until sizzling hot and pour over steamed or baked fish.

Serve.

Cheese Sauce for Pasta

Serves 4-6


INGREDIENTS

200ml x Double Cream

200ml x Whole milk

1 x Tablespoon of plain, white flour

100g x Butter

200g x Strong cheddar cheese, finely sliced or grated

1 x Clove garlic, sliced in 2, longways

Black pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Melt the butter in a saucepan over a low heat.

2) Add the flour stirring until it forms a smooth paste.

3) Add the milk slowly, whisking as the sauce thickens.

4) Add the cream slowly with whisking.

5) When thickened add the cheese with stirring.

6) When the cheese has melted add more milk if a thinner sauce is wanted.

7) Add the garlic and the black pepper, stir and keep until needed.

8) Remove the garlic and heat before serving.
