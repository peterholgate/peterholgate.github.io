Classic Tomato Bruschetta

Serves: 6

Preparation Time: 5 Mins

Cooking Time: 5 Mins (plus 20mins Chilling Time)


INGREDIENTS

300g x Fresh tomatoes (approximately 3 large beef tomatoes)

2 x Garlic cloves, peeled

8 x Fresh basil leaves, plus extra to garnish

3 x Tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to drizzle if needed

1 x Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

6 x Thick slices of ciabatta or baguette


METHOD

1) The bread should be crispy and only starting to soften where the olive oil and flavours ooze into the cracks.

2) So slice your ciabatta or baguette slices into diagonal slices approximately 1.5cm width.

3) Then toast them until golden and crunchy.

4) You can do this using a toaster or drizzle the bread with a little olive oil and brown in a frying pan over a medium heat.

5) Chop the tomatoes into small chunks and place in a bowl with all their juices.

6) Crush your garlic and add to the bowl with the tomato flesh. Tear the 8 basil leaves into small pieces and add to the bowl along with the oil.

7) Stir together and season with salt and pepper

8) Marinate in the fridge for at least 20 minutes, or longer, if possible.

9) Cut a whole garlic clove in half and scrape it lightly over each piece of toasted bread.  Then drizzle the bread with some extra virgin olive oil

10) Spread each slice of bread with a tablespoon of the tomato mixture, garnish with a few basil leaves and serve.


TIP:  It’s great if you prepare your toasted bread a few days in advance and then leave them in an open biscuit tin to air dry and get a little harder.  Once you’re happy with how crunchy they are, seal them in an airtight container until you’re ready to use.

Sweet Potato Soup with Coriander

Makes 2 litres


INGREDIENTS

2-3 x Tablespoons olive oil

1 x Red onion, small dice

2 x Cloves of garlic, minced

1 x Teaspoon ground coriander

1/2 x Teaspoon chili flakes

1 x Juice of a blood orange

2 x Sweet potatoes, peeled + diced

5 x Cups vegetable stock

1 x Salt and black pepper to taste


Method

1) Heat the oil in a large soup pot over medium heat.

2) Add the onions, garlic, coriander, and chili flakes to the pot.

3) Lower the heat until the sizzling sounds a bit lighter.

4) Stir and sauté this mixture until the onions are stew-y and soft, but not browned, about 8-10 minutes.

5) Add more oil if necessary.

6) Add the blood orange juice to the pot and stir. Add the sweet potatoes and stir again.

7) Season everything with lots of salt and pepper. Add the stock to the pot and increase the heat.

8) Once everything’s boiling, bring it down to a simmer.

9) Cook the soup until the sweet potatoes are really tender, about 12-15 minutes.

9) Purée the soup in batches in a blender or food processor until completely smooth.

10) Adjust the seasoning if necessary.

11) Serve the soup hot with little sweet potato chips and a sprinkle of sesame seeds if you like.


Tip1 Juice from a regular orange or a splash or sherry vinegar would be just as nice as the blood orange.

Tip2 Garnish this with some little quickie sweet potato chips: just sauté some thin slices in olive oil over medium heat, remove when lightly browned, and then dust them with a bit of salt or spice (I used Old Bay seasoning).

Parsnip Soup

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

1 x Medium onion chopped

1 x 75g Butter

3/4 x Garlic cloves crushed

1 x Medium carrot chopped

1 x Stick of celery chopped

1 x Red pepper chopped

4 x Medium parsnips thinly sliced and chopped

1 x 1 litre Chicken stock (2 x cubes)

1 1/2 x Teaspoons of medium curry powder

1 x 200ml Double cream

1 x Chopped coriander leaves

1 x Teaspoon dry fried cumin seeds for garnish (optional)

1 x Ground black pepper to taste


METHOD

1) In a large, heavy pan fry the onions in butter over a medium heat for about 10 mins until transparent

2) Add garlic, red pepper, carrots and celery, mix well and fry for a further 10mins.

3) Add the curry powder and stir for 2 to 3 mins.

4) Add the chicken stock

5) Add black pepper to taste

6) Stir well and cook on the hob for about 30mins, stirring every five minutes or so.

7) Add more black pepper if required (if using)

8) Cool and blend it at this stage but use CARE HOT!

9) Serve and/or or keep in fridge for a few days and serve.


Tip1 Add 1/4 teaspoon of chilli powder for a spicy kick
