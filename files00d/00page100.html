Bloody Mary Sardines (River Cottage)

Serves 1

Prep time 5 minutes

Cook Time 0


INGREDIENTS

1 x Tin (about 120g) of sardines in oil, drained

1 x Teaspoon tomato ketchup

2-3 x Drops Tabasco sauce

4-5 x Shakes Worcestershire sauce

1 x Squeeze of lemon juice

1 x Pinch of celery salt

1 x Freshly ground black pepper to taste

1 x Slice toast

1 x Butter for toast

1 x Dash of vodka


METHOD

1) In a small bowl roughly mash the sardines with the ketchup, Tabasco, Worcestershire, lemon juice, celery salt and black pepper.

2) Spread hot toast with butter, then pile on sardine mixture.

3) Finish off with a sprinkling of vodka and serve straight away.

Oven Roasted Trout with Thyme

Serves 2


INGREDIENTS

1 x Whole rainbow trout, scaled and gutted
2 x Sprigs of fresh thyme
3 x Tablespoons of lemon infused olive oil
1 x Lemon
4 x Bay leaves
1 x Clove garlic crushed

1 x Black pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Preheat oven to 200C.

2) Give the trout a good rinse and pat dry with paper towels.

3) Mix the crushed garlic, black pepper and the olive oil until it becomes a fragrant paste.

4) Rub this mixture all over the trout, covering the skin and inside the belly.

5) Put the thyme inside the fish.

6) Cut lemons in half and remove the ends so they have a flat edge.

7) With the point of a knife, make an incision into the flesh of each lemon half and stick a bay leaf into it.

8) Place trout and lemons on a roasting tray and bake, approximately 10 minutes at 200C.

9) The trout is cooked properly when the meat is easily pulled away from the bone at the thickest part of the fish.

10) The skin should be crispy and the roasted lemons should take on a marmalade characteristic.

11) The flesh should flake away easily but remain pink and moist.

Serve the trout with the lemon, which you can squeeze on to the fish.


Tip1 Good with roasted potatoes and a fresh watercress salad.

Tip2 Also good filleted and the flesh combined with the salad.

Smoked Haddock and Spinach LCHF

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x 500g Leaf spinach

1 x Knob butter, for greasing

2 x 250g Smoked haddock fillets, skinned and cut into 4 portions

12 x Cherry tomatoes

For the topping…

1 x 200ml Double cream

½ x Lemon, juice only

1 x 100g Cheddar cheese, grated

2 x Spring onions, sliced

1 x 100g Coconut flour

1 x Black pepper to taste

1/4 x Nutmeg grated

1 x Cayenne pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Preheat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4.

2) Place the spinach in a large colander in a sink and carefully and slowly pour over a kettleful of hot water to wilt it.

3)Cool again under the cold tap then squeeze out as much liquid from the spinach as possible.

4) Roughly chop the spinach and scatter evenly over the base of a buttered, medium gratin dish.

5) Season the spinach lightly with salt and pepper and lay the haddock fillets, skinned side down on top.

6) Nestle the tomatoes among the haddock fillets.

7) Mix the double cream, lemon juice, cheese and spring onions in a small bowl.

8) Add black pepper to taste.

9) Spread the mix over the fish and spinach.

10) Sprinkle the coconut flour on top.

11) Grate the nutmeg and sprinkle cayenne on top.

12) Bake for 30mins.

13) Crisp the top under a grill (optional) and serve.
