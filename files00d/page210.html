Linguine alle Vongole (Clam)

Serves 4

Preparation Time: 10 Mins - Cooking Time: 10 Mins


INGREDIENTS

1 x 400g Dried linguine or tagliatelle or use fresh pasta dough (400g flour, 4 eggs and salt)

1 x 1kg Small clams, scrubbed clean

4 x Tablespoons olive oil, plus extra to drizzle

4 x Cloves of garlic, peeled and finely sliced

1 x Large bunch of fresh flat- leaf parsley, leaves and stalks, finely chopped separately

1 x 300g Cherry tomatoes, halved (or large red tomatoes diced into cubes)

1 x Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

1 x 200ml White wine


METHOD

1) First, sort through your cleaned clams: if there are any that aren’t tightly closed, tap them on the work surface and if they still don’t close, throw them away.

2) Bring a large pan of salted water to the boil, ready to cook the pasta.

3) Heat the oil in a large pan over a medium heat. Add the garlic, parsley stalks and tomatoes, stir together and season with a little salt and pepper. Cook for 3 to 4 minutes, until the tomatoes start to soften – be careful not to burn the garlic.

4) If using dried pasta, cook it in the boiling water according to packet instructions.

5) Meanwhile, turn up the heat under the oil, add the clams and white wine, and stir to coat.

6) Cook with the lid on for 3 to 4 minutes, until all the clams have opened, giving the pan a quick shake every so often. If any clams haven’t opened, discard them.

7) If using fresh pasta cook it in the boiling water until al dente.

8) Drain and add the pasta to the clams with a handful of the chopped parsley leaves and a generous drizzle of oil.

9) Stir together and serve sprinkled with the rest of the chopped parsley.

Beef Mince Lasagne

Serves 4-6

Preparation Time 90 Mins

Cooking Time 90 Mins


INGREDIENTS

1 x Store bought packet of pre-cooked lasagne sheets or use home made green pasta dough (see separate recipe)

50g x Smoked pancetta, roughly chopped

1  x Red onion, peeled and finely chopped

1 x Carrot, peeled and finely chopped

1 x Stick of celery, finely chopped

1 x Clove of garlic, peeled and crushed

1 x Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

500g x Beef mince

1⁄2 x Glass of red wine

2 x Tablespoons tomato purée

400g x Tin good-quality chopped tomatoes

1 x Beef stock cube

1 x Dried bay leaf

500ml x Home made white sauce

1 x Splash of milk, if needed

75g x Freshly grated Parmesan cheese, plus extra to serve


METHOD

1) Make your white sauce

2) Make your fresh pasta, then roll it into long pasta sheets and cut it into strips to fit a 30cm x 20cm lasagne dish.

3) Heat a large pan over a medium heat.

4) When hot, lower the heat and fry the pancetta for 2 to 3 minutes, until it starts to crisp up.

5) Stir in the onion, carrot and celery and leave to sweat with the lid on for 10 minutes, until soft and translucent.

6) Add the garlic to the pan and stir for 1 minute.

7) Add the beef to the pan, stirring occasionally until browned all over.

8) Pour in the wine and keep stirring for 2 minutes.

9) The aim is to burn off the alcohol, leaving just a hint of the lovely wine flavour.

10) Stir in the tomato purée and chopped tomatoes and then add 200ml of boiling water.

11) Crumble in the stock cube, add the bay leaf and stir together.

12) Leave the ragù to simmer with the lid on for 15 minutes

13) Remove the lid and continue to simmer for a further 15 minutes. 14) Season with salt and pepper (keep in mind that the Parmesan used in the lasagne is quite salty).

15) Preheat the oven to 180 ̊C/350 ̊F/gas 4.

16) If you are using fresh pasta, bring a large pan of salted water to the boil and blanch the pasta sheets for about 2-3 mins, until al dente.

17) Lay them on a clean tea towel to absorb most of the water.

18) Reheat the white sauce, loosening the sauce with a little milk, if needed.

19) Start to assemble the lasagne by covering the bottom of a 30cm x 20cm ovenproof dish with a thin layer of white sauce.

20) This will stop the pasta sticking to the base and burning.

21) Follow this with a layer of pasta. Slightly overlap the strips so that there are no gaps, but don’t let the pasta curl up the sides of the dish; cut the pieces to fit.

22) Top with a layer of white sauce and then a layer of ragù. 23) Continue in this order until you have 4 or 5 layers of pasta and finish with the white sauce.

23) Don’t use too much ragù or Béchamel at each stage; you only need a thin layer of each.

24) Sprinkle with the Parmesan and cook in the oven for 30 to 40 minutes until golden brown and bubbling.

Green Pasta Dough for Lasagne

Preparation Time: 20 Mins plus 30 Mins Resting Time

Serves: 4-6


INGREDIENTS

235g x Spinach leaves, washed and patted dry

400g x Fine flour (tipo ‘00’), plus extra to dust

2–3 x Medium eggs, preferably free-range

1 x Salt to taste


METHOD

1) Put the spinach in a large saucepan and cover with 250ml boiling water.

2) Cook with the lid on for about 5 minutes, until the spinach has wilted.

3) Remove from the heat, drain and leave to cool (you can speed up the cooling process by running cold water over the spinach).

4) When the spinach is cool enough to handle, squeeze to remove the water.

5) This is a really important step.

6) If there’s any excess water in the filling it may cause the pasta to disintegrate, so make sure the spinach is as dry as possible.

7) Finely chop the spinach, running the knife over the top from time to time to create a paste. (To speed up this process blitz the spinach in a food processor.)

8) Put the flour, 2 eggs, a pinch of salt and the spinach in a large bowl. Mix well to combine.

9) If the dough is too dry, add the final egg, yolk first and then the white, mixing between additions to ensure it is well combined. You may not need to add the egg white.

10) Once the dough has come together, transfer to a lightly floured surface and knead until it has the consistency of Play-Doh.

11) Cover with cling film and rest for 30 minutes at room temperature.

12) Serve.
