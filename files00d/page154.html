Chinese Lemon Sauce

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

3 x Medium lemons

1 x 125ml Water

2 x Tablespoons brown sugar (or honey)

2 x Heaped teaspoons cornflour

1 x Tablespoon sweet chilli sauce

1 x Tablespoon light soy sauce (optional)


METHOD

1) Using a vegetable peeler, peel off the rind of 2 of the lemons, avoiding the white pith. Slice into fine strips.

2) Cut the 2 lemons in half, juice into a medium pan and add the lemon rind, water and sugar.

3) Bring to the boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook till the rind has softened, about 10 minutes.

5) Combine flour with a tablespoon or more of cold water to make a slurry.

6) Add the sweet chilli and soy sauces to the lemon sauce and whisk in while drizzling in the cornflour slurry, making sure there are no lumps.

7) Simmer for about 5 minutes or until thickened.

8) Thinly slice ½ (or more to taste) of the remaining lemon and add to the pan for about 2 minutes.

9) Serve with Battered (tempura is best) Chicken or Fish

Bolognese Sauce No Herbs or Spices

Cooking Time 4 Hours+


INGREDIENTS

1 x Red onion Brown. Chopped

3 x Stalks Celery Chopped

3 x Carrots Chopped

500g x Pork 17.6oz. Mince

300g x Beef 10.5oz. Mince

200g x Veal 7.05oz. Mince

1Litre x Italian tomato passata 4.2 cups

400g x Tomatoes 14.1oz. Peeled

150g x Tomato paste 5.29oz

1 x 50ml Extra virgin olive oil

1 x Glass Red wine

1 x Mug Full cream milk

1 x Salt and pepper, to taste

1-2Litres x Water to be boiled using the kettle


INSTRUCTIONS

1) Authentic Bolognese sauce starts with a soffrito, so place a large pot on your stove (medium heat), add a generous amount of extra virgin olive oil and once warm, add onion.

2) When the onion begins to look glossy and golden, add carrots, celery and then stir through, leaving to cook for 4-5 minutes.

3) Next add ½ glass red wine (118ml) to the authentic Bolognese sauce and leave to simmer on low for a few minutes or until the wine evaporates.

4) Add the mince and break it down using a wooden spoon and/or fork before seasoning with only salt and pepper.

5) Mix every so often to ensure the mince is all cooked through, leaving to brown.

6) Once the water has evaporated, mix in the other half glass of red wine.

7) In total, authentic Bolognese sauce should cook for up to 4-5hr and this will make the meat more tender.

8) After allowing the wine to evaporate again, mix through the passata, paste and peeled tomatoes.

9) Crush tomatoes using the wooden spoon as you stir through.

10) After 1.5hr, add 1L/4.2cups boiled water to the pot and mix through.

11) At this point, taste for salt – this is a preference of taste, add only if you want more as no other seasoning is necessary for an authentic Bolognese sauce!

12) Then after another hour or so, add more – the trick is when the sauce looks dry, add water, this will thicken the mixture and allow the mince to become more tender.

13) Allow the sauce to bubble only a small amount and not spurt everywhere.

14) If it is bubbling too much, turn the heat down.

15) When the sauce is 1/2 hr away from being ready, boil 5L water in a second pot then add a tablespoon of rock salt, leaving it to dissolve.

16) Once the sauce has been cooking for up to 5hr, remove it from the heat and leave to rest.

17) Add your choice of pasta to the water (ours is Pappardelle) and cook according to the packet instructions.

18) While the pasta is boiling, prepare a large fry pan for mixing!

19) Next add a cup of full cream milk to your sauce and stir through then put it back on the stove for 5 minutes.

20) Place a frypan on the stove at a low heat, and add a generous spoonful (or two!) of Bolognese sauce, covering the base.

21) Next, add the strained pasta into the pan along with a cup of pasta water.

22) Mix this through until the pasta is completely lathered before adding another generous amount of authentic Bolognese sauce and mix again.

Pork Barbecue Sauce PFH


INGREDIENTS

1 x Tablespoon of tomato puree

1 x Tablespoon of water

1 x Tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce

1 x Tablespoon of wine vinegar

1 x Tablespoon of clear honey

1 x Tablespoon of ground ginger

1 x Teaspoon of English mustard

2 x Cloves of garlic, crushed

1 x Salt and black pepper, to taste


METHOD

1) Put the pork (or whatever) in a roasting tin.

2) Mix all the other ingredients together.

3) Pour over the pork, coating both sides.

4) Cover the tin with foil.

5) Bake for 40mins at 200-210C.

6) Remove foil and bake for a further 20mins.

7) Serve.


Tip1 This sauce works for other barbecue meats or poultry, just adjust the baking or grilling time.
