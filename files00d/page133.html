Slow Cooker Savoury Pork Casserole LCHF

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

700g x Pork

50ml x Olive oil

2 x Large onions sliced

½ x Glass white wine

150ml x Chicken stock

60ml x Tomato puree

1 x Green pepper chopped

225g x Button mushrooms

4 x Large tomatoes Sliced

1 x Teaspoon of chilli sauce

5ml x Dried sage (or 10ml fresh finely chopped)

2 x Cloves garlic

¼ x Teaspoon black pepper


METHOD

1) In a large pan, heat the oil and fry the pork until light brown.

2) Add the onions and cook for a few minutes to soften.

3) Stir in the wine, stock, puree and cream.

4) Add the green pepper and mushrooms.

5) Add the garlic and black pepper, bring to a boil, and cook for about 2 minutes.

6) Add the tomatoes, chilli sauce and sage.

7) Transfer to the slow cooker.

8) On auto, cook for 6 -10 hours.

Slow Roast Belly Pork

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

2 x Tablespoons fennel seeds

1 x Teaspoons black peppercorns

1 x Small bunch thyme, leaves only

3 x Garlic cloves

3 x Tablespoons olive oil

1½ -2kg x Boneless pork belly, skin scored

2 x Lemons


METHOD

1) Toast the fennel seeds and peppercorns in a dry frying pan for a couple of minutes.

2) Pound them together in a pestle and mortar with some flaked sea salt, the thyme and garlic to make a paste.

3) Mix with 2 tablespoons olive oil and rub all over the flesh of the pork.

4) Cover and chill, leaving to marinate for a few hours or overnight.

5) When ready to cook, rub the skin of the joint with plenty of salt and 1 tablespoon more olive oil.

6) Sit on a wire rack in a roasting tin and roast at 200C/180C fan/gas 6 for 30 minutes.

7) After this time, squeeze the lemons over the skin and turn the heat down to 180C/ 160C fan/gas 4.

8) Roast for a further 2 hrs.

9) Finally, turn the heat back up to 220C/ 200C fan/gas 7 and give it a final blast for another 30 minutes or so, to finish the crackling.

10) Allow to rest somewhere warm for 20 minutes. Carve up into chunks or slices and serve.


Tip1 Good with the braised cabbage.

Slow Cooker Beef Casserole

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

800g x Braising steak cubed (or leave as steaks)

60g x Beef dripping

2 x Medium onions, roughly chopped

2 x Celery sticks, finely chopped

4 x Medium carrots, medium sliced

1 x Tablespoon garlic paste

1 x Heaped tablespoon tomato puree

1 x Tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce

1 x Tablespoon of balsamic vinegar

1 x Fresh black pepper to taste

2 x Beef stock cubes

4 to 5 Sprigs of Thyme

2 x Tablespoons of fresh, chopped parsley

 1 x Large bay leaf

1 x Litre of boiling water

1 x Tablespoon of cornflour

1 x Teaspoon of mustard (powder or ready to use)


METHOD

1) Set the slow cooker to high.

2) Melt the beef dripping in a heavy frying pan and add black pepper to taste.

3) Trim any excess fat etc off the meat then sear until brown and crusty

4) Add to the slow cooker leaving the fat in the pan.

5) Fry the onion and celery in the fat until soft and starting to brown.

6) Add the carrots and cook for 2 to 3 mins.

7) Add the beef cubes, Worcestershire sauce, balsamic vinegar, tomato puree and garlic paste to half of the boiling water.

8) Mix well and add to the pan,

9) Add the thyme and bay leaf and cook for about 5mins.

10) Add to the slow cooker.

11) Add the remaining water to the pan, bring to the boil and add to the slow cooker.

12) Cook for about 4 hours (or 6 to 7 hours if not using the high setting).

13) To thicken, mix the cornflour and mustard in a about 40ml of cold water and add with stirring.

14) Once the gravy has thickened, sprinkle with parsley and serve.


Tip1: Add a small punnet of whole button mushrooms for extra flavour

Tip2: Good with chips!

Tip3: Add a little fresh rosemary for a more herby, rich flavour
