Shami Kebabs

Serves 6-8


INGREDIENTS

1 x 500g Mince Lamb meat (or beef or chicken)

1 x 125g Chickpeas (chana dal)

2 x Medium  Onions roughly chopped

3-4 x Cloves of garlic, peeled

1 x 3cm Piece of Ginger, peeled and roughly chopped

1 x Teaspoon of Cumin seeds

4 x Cloves*

6 x Peppercorns*

3 x Green Cardamoms*

2 x Large Black Cardamoms*

1 x 3cm stick of Cinnamon*

2 x Bay leaves*

*or substitute 1 x teaspoon of Garam Masala and mix well before frying

2 x Teaspoons of coriander powder

2-3 x Dried red chillies (or 1/4 teaspoon powder)

1 x Salt to taste

1 x Black pepper to taste

1 x 1/2 Cup water

Ghee for pan frying

2 x Eggs lightly beaten

1 x Small bunch of coriander leaves finely chopped

1 x Small bunch of mint leaves finely chopped

2 x Tablespoons of Besan (Gram) flour (if needed)



METHOD

1. Wash and soak the dal for 30 minutes then drain

2. Place all ingredients in a pan, except the last three (egg, coriander and mint leaves) and cook, for about 10 minutes, until meat and dal are soft and all the water is absorbed

3. To finish off, stir fry on high until all the liquid is dried off and the meat looks quite dry

4. Pick out and discard large cardamoms, cinnamon stick and bay leaves and allow to cool.

5. Grind in a food processor until smooth

6. Add 2 lightly beaten eggs, coriander and mint leaves and mix well

7. Adjust seasoning. Leave covered in the fridge for an hour or two or even overnight for the mix to set a little

8. Divide into 12-15 portions and shape like a hamburger (or whatever size or shape you want)

9. Heat a little ghee in a non-stick frying pan

10. It is a good idea to test fry one kebab first and doesn’t hold together, add 1-2 tablespoons gram flour or besan to the mix before continuing with the rest

11. Shallow fry all kebabs until brown and crisp on both sides

12. Serve hot with Green Mango Chutney, riata etc

Chicken Curry in Pressure Cooker

Serves 8


INGREDIENTS

6 x Large chicken breasts (or large thighs) chopped into large cubes

12 x Tablespoon ghee (or oil)

6 x Dried red chillies

12 x Cloves

6 x Black cardamoms

12 x Black peppercorns

1 x 6cm Cinnamon stick

3 x Bay leaves medium

6 x Onion medium/large, peeled and cut into rings

6 x Onion medium/large, peeled and rough chopped

1 x 3cm Fresh root ginger (or use Frozen) peeled and rough chopped

12 x Cloves garlic rough chopped

1 x ½ Mug of water

1 x Teaspoon ground turmeric

1 x Teaspoon ground paprika

3 x Teaspoon ground coriander

6 x Teaspoon cumin seeds (dry fry until dark then grind)

1 x teaspoon Chilli Powder (or fresh hot chillies)

1 x Mug fresh natural yoghurt

1 x Small cauliflower chopped into medium florets (optional)

3 x Teaspoon garam masala

1 x Bunch fresh coriander leaf rough chopped


METHOD

1) Add ½ cup of water, chopped onions, garlic and ginger to liquidiser and blend to a paste.

3) Pour ghee into a large pressure cooker on the hob.

4) Add the dried red chillies, cloves, black cardamoms, black peppercorns, cinnamon stick and the bay leaves and cook at medium heat until spices are brown.

5) Add onion rings and cook at medium heat for about 20 min until golden brown and dry and take off heat.

8) Add the ground turmeric, paprika, coriander, cumin, chili.

9) Stir well and cook at medium heat for 3 min.

10) Add chicken pieces and mix well with spices etc and cook at medium heat for 3 min.

12) Add liquidised onions, garlic and ginger to pressure cooker and cook at medium heat for 5 min.

14) Take off heat and add ½ cup of water and yogurt whilst stirring.

16) Put lid on pressure cooker and cook at high for 15 min.

17) Cool with water, remove lid, remove the chicken and boil off excess water if necessary.

19) Replace chicken and stir should be a fairly thick sauce.

20) Decant into large oven dish and add chopped cauliflower and mix in if using then cook at 200C for 5 min.

23) Sprinkle with Garam Masala then cook at 220C for 5 min.

25) Garnish with chopped fresh coriander and serve.

Slow Cooker Massaman Curry

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x 500g New potatoes, halved

1 x 800g Boned lamb shoulder or beef shin, cut into matchbox-size pieces

3 x Tablespoons massaman curry paste

1 x Small bunch fresh coriander, stalks finely chopped

1 x 5cm Piece fresh root ginger, finely grated

1 x 400ml Can coconut milk

1 x Teaspoon light muscovado sugar

1 x Lime, zest and juice

1 x Tablespoon fish sauce, plus extra to taste

6 x Freeze-dried kaffir lime leaves (optional)

1 x Handful roasted unsalted peanuts, roughly chopped, to serve


METHOD

1) Place the potatoes in the slow cooker.

2) Heat a large non-stick dry frying pan and brown the meat in batches until golden brown, then transferring to the slow cooker.

3) Add the massaman paste, coriander stalks and ginger to the frying pan, then fry for a few minutes until fragrant.

4) Tip in the coconut milk and bring to the boil. Season with the sugar, lime zest and fish sauce, add the lime leaves if using, then pour the sauce over the meat and potatoes.

5) Cover with the lid and cook on low for 8 hours until very tender.

6) Spoon off any excess fat from the top of the curry, add the juice from half the zested lime and add more fish sauce if needed.

7) The curry should have a balance of sour, salty, hot and sweet without one flavour dominating. Scatter with the coriander leaves and peanuts, then serve with steamed fragrant rice and the remaining lime in wedges, for squeezing.
