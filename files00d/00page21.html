Thai Style Fishcakes – Serves 4 to 6


Ingredients

1 x 1/2 kg Cod fillets (any white fish will work fine)

6 x Kaffir lime leaves, snipped into thin strips with scissors

3 x Tablespoon. coconut milk

2 x Tablespoon fish sauce

1/2 xTeaspoon shrimp paste (or 1 extra tablespoon fish sauce)

1/2 x Tablespoon chilli powder

1/3 x Teaspoon ground cumin

1/4 x Teaspoon ground coriander

1/2 x Teaspoon brown sugar

3 x Spring onions sliced (scallions)

1 x 4cm Piece galangal grated (or use gingerroot)

3 x Cloves garlic

1 x Red chilli, sliced (or 1/2 teaspoon dried crushed chilli)

1/3 x Cucumber

Coconut oil

Thai sweet chilli sauce

Fresh coriander leaf

Fresh lime juice


Method

1) Rinse fresh or defrosted fish and pat thoroughly dry.

2) Cut into chunks and place in food processor.

3) In a cup, combine the coconut milk, fish sauce, shrimp paste, chili powder, cumin, ground coriander,  and brown sugar.

4) Stir with a fork to combine, then pour into the processor over the fish.

5) Add remaining ingredients (kaffir lime leaf strips, green onion, galangal/ginger, garlic, and chili).

6) Pulse to create a thick fish paste.

7) Picking up a small amount in your hand (about the  size of a golf ball) pat the paste into a small cake and set on a clean plate.

8) Set plate of cakes in the refrigerator for 10 minutes to firm up.

9) Pour coconut oil into a small frying pan or wok at least 3 cm deep.

10) Heat oil to high medium temperature and gently place cakes in oil.

11) Allow to fry 30 seconds to 1 minute.

12)Turn gently lifting cakes from the bottom of the pan (they may stick a little).

13) Fry until golden-brown then drain on paper towel.

14)Serve with Thai sweet chili sauce, chopped cucumber, fresh coriander, and a squeeze of lime juice.


2013-FIS-001-Fishcakes Thai Style


Tips1: If your fish paste is too wet to easily form into cakes, add a little flour or breadcrumbs to the mix.

Tip2: You can make the fish paste up to 24 hours in advance. Cover and set in the refrigerator, then form into cakes and fry.

Easy Paella - Serves 4


Ingredients

2 to 3 x Tablespoons of olive oil

1 x 50g Knob of butter (optional)

1 x Black pepper to taste

1 x Medium leek, finely sliced

½ x Red pepper, finely chopped

1 x 120g Spicy chorizo, skinned, halved and sliced

1 x Teaspoon turmeric

1 x Teaspoon Hot Smoked Paprika (or hot, smoked paprika)

¼ x Teaspoon of Cayenne Pepper (omit if using hot, smoked paprika)

1 x 250g Long grain rice (Basmati is good)

1 x 900ml Chicken Stock (or use 2 ‘Knorr’ cubes)

1 x 200g Frozen petit pois (or peas)

1 x 400g Seafood mix, defrost, if frozen (or other fish, chicken etc)

1 x Lemon cut into 8 wedges



Method

1) Cook the leeks and black pepper in the olive oil and butter for about 5 minutes to soften without browning.

2) Add the chorizo and fry until it releases its oils.

3) Add the red pepper and fry for about 3 mins.

3) Add the turmeric, smoked paprika, cayenne and rice, then stir until coated with oil.

4) Add the chicken stock, bring to boil and simmer for 15 minutes.

5) Add the peas and cook for 5 minutes.

6) Add the seafood and stir in for 2-3 minutes or until the rice is cooked.

7) Serve immediately with the lemon wedges.


2019-FIS-003-Easy Paella


Tip 1: Substitute fish stock for chicken stock.

Tip 2: Substitute cubed chicken for seafood or part thereof.

Tip 3: Serve with Fried Eggs, sunny side up cooked in butter.
