Sausage Meatballs in Tomato Sauce Italian

Serves: 4-6

Preparation Time: 20 Mins

Cooking Time: 40 Mins


INGREDIENTS

For the meatballs:

500g x Sausage meat

100g x Freshly grated Parmesan cheese

1 x Clove garlic, peeled and finely chopped

100g x Breadcrumbs

2 x Medium eggs, preferably free-range or organic

1 x Small handful of chopped parsley

1 x Teaspoon ground fennel seeds

1 x Teaspoon fine salt

1⁄2 x Teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

1 x Tablespoon olive oil

For the polenta:

200g x Fast cooking polenta

1 x Knob of butter

800mls x Cold water

2 x Handfuls of grated parmesan cheese

1 x Salt to taste

For the Sauce:

2 x Red peppers, deseeded and roughly chopped

4 x Medium vine tomatoes, halved

1  x Whole garlic bulb, halved

1 x Red chilli, halved lengthways

2 to 3 x Sprigs of thyme

1 x Salt to taste

1 x Black pepper

1 x 50ml Olive oil


METHOD

1) Preheat oven to 180C

2) In a roasting tray place all the veggies for your sauce, season with salt and pepper and drizzle with oil.

3) Roast for 15-20mins or until roasted and lovely and golden.

4) Then add to a  mini blitzer, whiz up and taste, adding more seasoning if needed.

5) For the meatballs, put all the ingredients, apart from the oil, in a large bowl and mix together with your hands.

6) Shape into walnut-sized balls.

7) Heat the oil in a frying pan over a medium heat and cook the meatballs for about 10 minutes, turning regularly until browned all over and cooked through.

8) Put polenta, butter and cold water into a pan, bring to boil, constantly whisking/stirring to ensure no lumps form.

9) Turn down to simmer and keep cooking for another minute, adding the parmesan and seasoning  (or follow the instructions as directed on the packet).

10) To serve, place a few large spoons of polenta in a large serving bowl, add a few tablespoons of the sauce and top with your meatballs.

11) Drizzle with some extra virgin olive oil and parmesan shavings.

12) Serve

Chicken Kebabs Spanish

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

4 x Chicken breasts (cubed into 2.5cm pieces)

2 x Tablespoons of butter

2 x Teaspoon turmeric

1/2 x Teaspoon paprika

1/2 x Teaspoon smoked paprika

1 x Teaspoon ground coriander

1/2 x Teaspoon ground cumin

4 x Cloves garlic (crushed)

3 x Sprigs fresh thyme

2 x Tablespoons sherry vinegar

1 x Chilli sauce to taste

1 x Black pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Mix together all the ingredients for the marinade.

2) Add the leaves from the thyme by running your fingers down the stalk starting at the top.

3) Put the chicken in the marinade and stir it around so all the pieces are coated.

4) Refrigerate overnight or for a minimum of 2 hours.

5) Thread the chicken on to skewers.

6) If using wooden ones, soak in water first.

7) Cook the chicken in a hot oiled griddle pan.

8) Cook for about 5 minutes on each side.

9) Alternatively bake for 15 minutes in the oven or cook on the bbq.

10) Serve.

Falafel with Tahini Sauce and Tabbouleh


INGREDIENTS

Falafels:

250g x Dried fava beans, peeled and soaked overnight

250g x Dried chickpeas, soaked overnight

1 x Handful of parsley, chopped

1 x Onion, coarsely chopped

2-3 x Garlic cloves, coarsely chopped

1 x Egg yolk  

3 x Tablespoons chickpea flour

2 x Tablespoons fine breadcrumbs

1 x Salt and pepper to taste

1/4 x Teaspoon ground cumin  

1 x Tablespoon of sesame seeds

1 x Teaspoon sumac spice

1litre x Olive oil to fry

Yogurt Tahini Sauce:

200g x Tahini paste (sesame paste)

1 x Garlic clove, mashed

150g x Greek yogurt

2  x Teaspoons lime juice

1 x Tablespoon good olive oil

1 x Salt to taste

1/2 x Teaspoon ground coriander powder

1 x Teaspoon sumac spice

Parsley & Mint Tabouleh:

150g x Bulgur, cooked according to instructions on the packet

1x Salt and black pepper to taste

4 x Tomatoes, finely chopped

1 x Large bunch parsley, finely chopped

1/2  x Bunch mint, finely chopped

1 x Juice and zest of a lemon

1/2 x Ground cumin powder  

2 x Tablespoons good olive oil  

1 x Sumac to taste


METHOD

Falafels:

1) Drain water from both, fava beans and chickpeas.

2) Put both legumes in a food processor, along with the onions, garlic, parsley and mint and process until smooth.

3) Add the egg yolk, breadcrumbs and chickpea flour and process further.

4) You should have a thick paste-like consistency.

5) Season with 1 teaspoon salt, pepper, cumin powder and sumac.

6) Place in the refrigerator for 30 minutes.

7) With the falafel mixture make walnut sized balls and lightly roll in some sesame seeds.

8) If the mixture begins to stick to your hands, moist them in cold water.

9) Heat the oil in a deep fat fryer or a large pot to approx. 180 degrees C.

10) Add a few of the falafel balls into the hot oil and fry for 2-3 minutes, turning the balls so that they evenly brown on all sides.

11) Allow to drain on kitchen paper towels.

Yogurt Tahini Sauce:

12) Add tahini paste, yogurt, garlic, lime juice and olive oil in a bowl and using a pureeing machine mix the mixture well.

13) Season with salt, ground coriander powder and sumac.

Parsley & Mint Tabbouleh:

14) In a small mixing bowl whisk together lemon juice and zest, salt, pepper and cumin powder and oil into a thick dressing.

15) Using a fork loosen the bulgur. Place in a large salad bowl and add tomatoes, mint and parsley.

16) Pour the vinaigrette over the bulgur and toss well. Allow for the flavours to infuse – approx. 1 hour.

17) Sprinkle with sumac before serving.


Tip 1: To see if the oil has reached the right temperature place the handle of a wooden spoon in the oil and if small bubbles form around the handle your oil is ready.

Tip 2: While frying the falafels only add a few balls to the pot at one time so that they do not stick to each other while frying.
