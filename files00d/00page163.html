Braised Red Cabbage with Apple

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

3 x Onion sliced into half-moons

4 x Peeled and roughly diced Bramley apples

1 x Shredded large red cabbage

2 x Tablespoons brown sugar

100ml x Red wine


METHOD

1) Sweat the onions in 2 tablespoons olive oil until soft.

2) Add the apples.

3) Add the red cabbage to the pan. Season, stir and cover with a lid. Turn the heat down and stew for 1 hr, or until cabbage is tender.

4) Check every now and then, adding some water if the pan is looking a bit dry.

5) Remove the lid and stir in 100g soft brown sugar.

6) Add 100ml red wine vinegar, cook for 10 minutes until evaporated, season to taste and serve.

Carrots Xmas


INGREDIENTS

1kg x Small carrots

50g x Unsalted butter

1 x Tablespoon dripping or goose fat

6 x Cloves of garlic

½ x Bunch of fresh thyme

2 x Cementines

2 x Tablespoons runny honey or soft brown sugar


METHOD

1) Trim most of the leafy green stalks off the carrots, then peel them.

2) Melt the butter and dripping or goose fat (if using) in a large frying pan over a medium heat.

3) Crush the unpeeled garlic with the flat side of your knife, then add to the pan turning after 1 minute.

4) Pick and sprinkle in the thyme sprigs, squeeze over the clementine juice, then add the honey or sugar and a splash of water.

5) Add the carrots in a single layer, season with sea salt and black pepper, the jiggle the pan to coat the carrots.

6) Cover, reduce the heat to medium-low and cook for 15 to 20 minutes, or until tender.

7) Remove the lid, then cook for a further 5 minutes, or until the glaze has reduced, and the carrots are sticky and caramelised, turning often.

8) Serve straightaway, or reheat when needed.

Best Roast Potatoes


INGREDIENTS

1 x 2.5 kg Medium Maris Piper potatoes, peeled about 8cm

4 x Tablespoons goose fat , or unsalted butter

1 x Olive oil

1 x Bulb of garlic

½ x Bunch of fresh sage (15g)


METHOD

1) Preheat the oven to 180ºC/350ºF/gas 4.

2) Parboil potatoes in a pan of boiling salted water for 15 minute.

3) Drain in a colander and leave to steam dry for 2 minutes.

4) Give the colander a few light shakes to chuff up the edges of the potatoes.

5) Place the goose fat or butter and 1 tablespoon of oil in your largest roasting tray.

6) Tip in the potatoes, add a good pinch of sea salt and black pepper, then toss to coat, and spread out with small gaps between them.

7) Squash the garlic bulb, then lightly crush each unpeeled clove and add to the tray.

8) Roast for 1 hour, or until the potatoes are crisp and golden all over.

9) Remove the tray from the oven.

10) Gently half-squash each potato with a fish slice or masher so they kind of push into each other and fill the tray.

11) Pick the sage leaves and toss with a little oil.

12) Sprinkle the sage over the potatoes and roast for a further 20 to 25 minutes, or until golden.

13) Serve.


Tip1 Parboil your potatoes and dress them with the fat, garlic and seasoning a day in advance. Simply cover and keep in a cool place until you’re ready to roast.
