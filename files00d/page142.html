Chicken Croquettes

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x 400g Chicken breast shopped into 1x1 cm strips

3 x Eggs

1 x 50g Spring onion chopped

1 x Clove of garlic finely crushed (optional)

1 x Tablespoon Flour

1 x Tablespoon double cream

1 x Bl;ack pepper to taste

1 x Chilli or Cayenne to taste (optional)

1 x 50g Coconut oil to fry


METHOD

1) Add chicken to a large bowl

2) Add the eggs, spring onions, garlic, flour, cream

3) Add pepper (or chilli, herbs etc) to taste and mix well.

4) Form by hand into flat patties about 10cm diameter

5) Add the coconut oil to a non-stick frying pan

6) Fry the patties for about 5 minutes on each side

Serve

Butterflied Baked Chicken in Herbs and Spice

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x Juice of a Lime

1/2 x Juice of a Lemon

1 x 50ml Olive Oil

1 x Tablespoons of Dried Thyme

1 x Tablespoon of Oregano

2 x Tablespoons of Herbes de Province

1 x Teaspoon of Cumin

1 x Tablespoons of Onion Powder

1 1/2 x Tablespoons of Garlic Powder

1 x Tablespoon of Paprika

½ x Teaspoon of Cayenne to taste

1 x Black pepper to taste

1 x Salt to taste


METHOD

1) Mix the Lime/Lemon and Olive Oil in a small bowl

2) Mix all the dried herbs and spices in a bowl.

3) Ensure the chicken is clean

4) Use strong scissors to cut the chicken along the bottom to one side of the spine

5) Open out the chicken, push flat and dry with paper towels

6) Rub in the Oil and Juices mix until thoroughly coated all over

7) Sprinkle half the herbs all over the bottom of the chicken and press in

8) Sprinkle half the herbs all over the top of the chicken and press in

9) Bake, skin side up, under foil, for 50 min at 220C

10) Remove foil and bake for a further 10 to 15mins until the skin is crisp

Tuscan Chicken in Sundried Tomato Sauce

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

3 x Tablespoons olive oil

4 x Chicken breasts

1 x Large egg

3 x Tablespoons plain flour

½ x Teaspoon salt

½ x Teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

1 x Teaspoon dried oregano

½ x Teaspoon dried thyme

1½ x Teaspoon paprika

¼ x Teaspoon garlic salt

1 x Onion peeled and sliced

2 x Cloves garlic peeled and minced

1 x Cup (160g) sun dried tomatoes

1 x Red pepper de-seeded and sliced

1 x Tablespoon tomato puree

⅓ x Cup (90ml) white wine

1 x Cup (240ml) chicken stock

⅓ cup (90ml) double (heavy) cream

½ packed cup (50g) parmesan cheese grated

3 x Packed cups (90g) fresh baby spinach

1 x Tablespoon chopped parsley


METHOD

1) Preheat the oven to 180C.

2) Whisk the egg lightly in a shallow bowl.

3) In a separate shallow bowl, mix together 3 tablespoons of flour, ¼ teaspoon salt, ½ teaspoons of pepper, ½ teaspoon of oregano, ½ teaspoons of thyme, ½ teaspoons of paprika and ¼ teaspoons of garlic salt.

4) Dip the chicken breasts in the egg, then dredge in the flour mixture.

5) Pan fry the chicken, in oil, on both sides until golden

6) Move the chicken to an oven tray and oven cook for about 10 minutes, whilst you make the sauce.

7) Add more oil to the pan and heat on a medium heat.

8) Add the onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until it starts to soften.

9) Add 2 cloves garlic, ½ teaspoon of oregano, 1 teaspoon of paprika, 1 cup of sundried tomatoes, 1 red pepper and 1 tablespoon of tomato puree.

10) Cook for 2 minutes, stirring continuously.

11) Add ⅓ cup of wine and allow to bubble for 2 minutes,

12) Add 1 cup of chicken stock, salt and pepper then bring the boil and simmer for 5 minutes.

13) Add the ⅓ cup of cream, ½ cup of parmesan and the cup of spinach to the sauce and stir and cook for a couple of minutes until the spinach wilts.

14) Remove the chicken from the oven, ensuring that it is cooked through.

15) Add to the pan with the sauce and cook for another couple of minutes.

16) Serve topped with a sprinkling of fresh parsley.

Tip 1: Tastes great with pasta, courgetti (zoodles), rice or sautéed potatoes.
