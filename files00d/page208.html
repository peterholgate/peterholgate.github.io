Cauliflower, Chickpea & Spinach Masala

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

2 x Tablespoon olive oil or ghee

1 x Large onion, finely diced

4 x Garlic cloves, finely chopped

4 cm x Piece fresh ginger, peeled and grated

1 x Green chilli, finely chopped (optional)

2 x Teaspoon ground cumin

2 x Teaspoon ground coriander

1½ x Teaspoon ground turmeric

2 x Teaspoon garam masala

1 x Teaspoon mild chilli powder (adjust to taste)

1 x Medium cauliflower (about 800g), broken into small florets

1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes

250ml x Vegetable stock

1 × 400g tin chickpeas, drained and rinsed

200ml x Coconut cream

200g x Fresh spinach leaves, washed

1 x Juice of ½ lemon

1 x Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

1 x Fresh coriander leaves, to garnish


METHOD

1) Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat.

2) Fry the onion for 8–10 minutes until softened, then add garlic, ginger and chilli for 1–2 minutes.

3) Stir in cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala and chilli powder, cooking for 30–60 seconds until fragrant.

4) Add cauliflower and stir to coat in spices.

5) Pour in tomatoes and stock, bring to the boil, then reduce heat and simmer covered for 12–15 minutes until tender.

6) Stir in chickpeas and coconut cream, cooking for 3–4 minutes.

7) Add spinach in handfuls until wilted.

8) Season with salt, pepper and lemon juice.

9) Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with basmati rice or chapatis.

Dhaba Style Chicken Curry

Serves 2-3


INGREDIENTS

350g x Chicken with/without bones

3 x Medium tomatoes rough chopped (of ½ canned)

20 x Sprigs of fresh coriander

2 x Mild green chillies

50g x Peeled ginger

50g x Peeled x garlic

3 x Tablespoons water

3 x Tablespoons ghee

2 x Teaspoons cumin seeds

4 x Cloves

6 x Black peppercorns (optional)

4 x Green cardamoms

2 x Black cardamoms

3 x Pieces of cinnamon sticks

2 x Onions chopped into small pieces

3 x Tablespoons ginger and garlic paste

2 x Teaspoons turmeric powder

2 x Teaspoons Kashmiri red chili powder

3 x Tablespoons coriander powder

2 x Teaspoons cracked black pepper

1 x Tablespoons cumin powder

1 x Tablespoons garam masala

50g x Plain yogurt

1 x Tablespoons toasted fried fenugreek leaves (Kasuri Methi)

1 x Teaspoons garam masala

1 x ½ Lemon

Warm water as required

Salt to taste


METHOD

1) Puree the tomatoes, coriander leaves, green chillies, ginger, garlic with a little water.

2) Heat the ghee, add the cumin seeds and fry with whole garam masala (cinnamon stick, green cardamoms, black cardamoms, peppercorns and cloves.

3) Add onions and fry until golden brown.

4) Add the puree and salt and fry 6-7min.

5) Reduce heat and add turmeric, Kashmiri chilli, coriander powder, ground black pepper, cumin powder, garam masala and fry for 30 secs or so.

6) On a low flame add yoghurt stirring for 5 to 6 mins.

7) Add chicken fry on medium heat for 3 or 4 mins.

8) Add water simmer 5 or 6 mins until the chicken is cooked.

9) Remove the whole spices is you want.

10) Garnish with Juliennes of ginger, fresh coriander leaves, garam masala powder, and Kasuri Methi leaves.

Sticky Soy and Ginger Pork

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

Marinade:

4cm x Fresh root ginger, peeled and grated

2 x Garlic cloves, crushed

4 x Tablespoons soy sauce

2 x Tablespoons sweet chilli sauce

3 x Tablespoons runny honey

Pork:

1 x Large pork fillet (about 600g), fully trimmed

4 x Spring onions, finely shredded into long, thin strips

1 x Red chilli, seeds removed and thinly sliced

2 x Tablespoons chopped fresh coriander

1 x Salt and freshly ground black pepper

1 x Freshly steamed rice or cooked noodles, to serve


METHOD

1) To make the marinade, place all of the ingredients in a bowl and mix well.

2) Place the fillet in the marinade.

3) Season with salt and pepper and turn until the fillet is fully coated with marinade.

4) Leave to marinate for a few hours in the fridge.

5) Preheat the oven to 220C/200C Fan/Gas 7 and line a small roasting tin with baking paper.

6) Removed the fillet from the marinade (reserving the marinade to use later) and place in the roasting tin.

7) Roast for 25–30 minutes, until golden brown and cooked through.

8) Remove the fillet from the roasting tin and set aside on a board to rest.

9) Cover with foil and leave for 5 minutes before carving.

10) Place the reserved marinade in a small saucepan and place over a medium heat.

11) Bring to a simmer, stirring occasionally.

12) Carve the pork into slices and arrange on a serving platter.

13) Pour the hot marinade over the top and scatter with the spring onions, chilli and coriander.

14) Serve with rice or noodles.

Tip1: Good serves with new potatoes.
