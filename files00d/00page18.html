Recipe FIS-001

Thai Style Fishcakes – Serves 4-6


Ingredients

1 x 1/2 kg Cod fillets (any white fish will work fine)

6 x Kaffir lime leaves, snipped into thin strips with scissors

3 x Tablespoon. coconut milk

2 x Tablespoon fish sauce

1/2 xTeaspoon shrimp paste (or 1 extra tablespoon fish sauce)

1/2 x Tablespoon chilli powder

1/3 x Teaspoon ground cumin

1/4 x Teaspoon ground coriander

1/2 x Teaspoon brown sugar

3 x Spring onions sliced (scallions)

1 x 4cm Piece galangal grated (or use gingerroot)

3 x Cloves garlic

1 x Red chilli, sliced (or 1/2 teaspoon dried crushed chilli)

1/3 x Cucumber

Coconut oil

Thai sweet chilli sauce

Fresh coriander leaf

Fresh lime juice


Method

1) Rinse fresh or defrosted fish and pat thoroughly dry.

2) Cut into chunks and place in food processor.

3) In a cup, combine the coconut milk, fish sauce, shrimp paste, chili powder, cumin, ground coriander,  and brown sugar.

4) Stir with a fork to combine, then pour into the processor over the fish.

5) Add remaining ingredients (kaffir lime leaf strips, green onion, galangal/ginger, garlic, and chili).

6) Pulse to create a thick fish paste.

7) Picking up a small amount in your hand (about the  size of a golf ball) pat the paste into a small cake and set on a clean plate.

8) Set plate of cakes in the refrigerator for 10 minutes to firm up.

9) Pour coconut oil into a small frying pan or wok at least 3 cm deep.

10) Heat oil to high medium temperature and gently place cakes in oil.

11) Allow to fry 30 seconds to 1 minute.

12)Turn gently lifting cakes from the bottom of the pan (they may stick a little).

13) Fry until golden-brown then drain on paper towel.

14)Serve with Thai sweet chili sauce, chopped cucumber, fresh coriander, and a squeeze of lime juice.


Tips1 If your fish paste is too wet to easily form into cakes, add a little flour or breadcrumbs to the mix.


Tip2 You can make the fish paste up to 24 hours in advance. Cover and set in the refrigerator, then form into cakes and fry.

Recipe HUM-001

Hummus - Makes 6 to 8 Cups


Ingredients

4 x 240g Tins chickpeas (drained weight) or use 500g of dried chickpeas soaked overnight and boiled in water for about 45min.

1 x 200g Tahini paste

2 x Lemons, quartered

8 x Cloves of garlic crushed

1 x 500ml Cold water (pre-boiled)

2 x Teaspoons of fresh cumin powder (optional)

1 x Teaspoon of salt (optional)


Method

1) Add the Tahini to a large mixing bowl.

2) Add the garlic.

3) Pre-warm and squeeze in the juice of the lemons (the tahini will tighten up).

4) Mix well and add water to make a loose paste.

5) Add the drained chickpeas and mix.

6) Use a handheld blender to blend and mix to a smooth paste.

7) Add more water if the paste is too stiff.

8) Add salt to taste and blend or mix again.

9) Add the cumin if using.

10) Transfer to disposable plastic cups and cover with food film.

11) Serve in a bowl with a topping of olive oil (add cumin, chilli or paprika for extra flavour).


Tip 1 Freezes well.

Tip2 Almost impossible to mix in a ‘normal’ blender.

Recipe CHI-002

Chicken Espagnole - Slow Cooker -  Serves 4


Ingredients

1 x 100g Knob butter

4 x Chicken joints

1 x 100g Chorizo, halved and sliced (or substitute smoked bacon)

2 x Medium onions, finely sliced

2 x Peppers (capsicums), chopped into 2cm squares

1 x 150g Whole button mushrooms

1 x 500ml Hot chicken stock

1 x Large carrot, diced small

1 x 150g Tomato puree

2 x Medium bay leaves

1 x Bouquet Garni (or rosemary, rhyme and sage sprigs)

1 x Black pepper to taste

1 x Small glass of sherry


Method

1) Brown the chicken joints in a large pan with butter then add to slow cooker.

2) Fry the chorizo until it darkens then add to the slow cooker.

3) Fry the onions to soften about 5 minutes.

4) Fry the peppers to soften about 5 minutes.

5) Fry the carrot for about 2 minutes.

6) Add the chicken stock to the vegetables and bring to a boil.

7) Add the tomato puree, bouquet garni, bay leaves and mushrooms.

8) Season with black pepper, bring back to the boil then transfer everything to the slow cooker.

9) Cook for 6-8 hours on a low setting (or less time on high).

10) Add the sherry about 15 minutes before serving.


Tip 1 Garnish with chopped parsley before serving
