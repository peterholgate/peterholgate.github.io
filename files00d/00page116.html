Pork Chop in Lemon Caper Sauce

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

4 x Bone-in pork chops (about 250geach)

1 x Salt and freshly cracked black pepper, to taste

½ x Teaspoon dried thyme leaves

2 x Tablespoons olive oil

4 x Tablespoons unsalted butter

1 x Tablespoon of minced shallot

2 x Garlic cloves, minced

2 x Teaspoons all-purpose flour

1 x Cup dry white wine

1½ x Cups chicken stock, (Knorr)

2 x Tablespoons drained capers

2 x Tablespoons minced fresh parsley, plus more for garnish

1 x Teaspoon freshly grated lemon zest, plus 2 tablespoons of juice

1 x Splash hot chilli sauce (optional)


METHOD

1) Dry the chops with paper towels, and season aggressively with salt, pepper and the thyme.

2) Swirl the olive oil into a large skillet, and heat over medium until the oil begins to shimmer.

3) Add chops, and cook until well browned on each side and cooked through, about 5 minutes per side.

4) Transfer chops to a plate, and cover to keep warm.

5) Drain the fat from the skillet, then melt 2 tablespoons of butter in it over medium heat until sizzling.

6) Add the shallot and garlic, and sauté until the aromatics soften, reducing the heat if necessary, about 1 minute.

7) Sprinkle in the flour, and cook, stirring, for 2 minutes.

8) Whisk in the wine and chicken stock, raise heat to high and bring the liquid to a boil, scraping up the browned bits on the bottom of the pan.

9) Reduce heat to medium-high and cook, uncovered, until the liquid is reduced by half, 7 to 10 minutes.

10) Stir in the capers, parsley, lemon zest and juice and hot sauce to taste (if you’re using it), and simmer for 1 to 2 minutes.

11) Stir in the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter until it’s melted and the sauce looks smooth.

12)  Nestle the pork chops into the sauce, and allow them to warm up for a couple of minutes, then serve, pouring sauce over each pork chop to taste.

13) Garnish with more fresh parsley.

Slow Cooker Brisket

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x kg Beef brisket

2 x Red onions (sliced)

1 x Beef stock (stock cube)

1 x 400 ml Boiling water

2 x Tablespoons Worcestershire sauce

1 x Salt

1 x Black pepper

2 x Teaspoons dried sage (or fresh)

1 x Teaspoon dried rosemary (or fresh)

1 x Tablespoon cornflour (to thicken, if needed)


METHOD

1) Brown the meat in a dry pan on a high heat.

2) Place the red onions in the base of the slow cooker and put the beef on top of the onions.

3) Make 400 ml/2 cups beef stock/broth using boiling water and pour over the beef.

4) Add Worcestershire sauce.

5) Add salt and pepper, dried sage and dried rosemary. (or use fresh herbs)

6) Cook on low for 8-9 hours

7) Thicken the juices with a paste made from cornflour and a little cold water.

8) Serve

Best Meat Stuffing


INGREDIENTS

2 x Onions, peeled and finely chopped

2 x Leeks, trimmed and finely chopped (use white part only)

1 x Bunch of fresh sage (30g)

1 x Olive oil

1 x 50g Unsalted butter

4 x Rashers of smoked streaky bacon

1 x Whole nutmeg, for grating

1 x 400g Stale bread

1 x 200g Vac-packed chestnuts

1 x 1kg Minced pork shoulder

1 x 400g Tin of peaches, in juice

1 x Clementine, halved


METHOD

1) Pick the sage leaves, keep 2 nice big ones aside, then finely slice the rest.

2) Place a large frying pan on a medium heat with 1 tablespoon of oil and the butter.

3) Finely slice and add the bacon, fry until lightly golden.

4) Stir in the sliced sage, followed by the onions and leeks.

5) Finely grate in half the nutmeg.

6) Add a good pinch of sea salt and black pepper.

7) Cook for 15 minutes, or until soft, stirring occasionally. Leave to cool.

8) Toast the bread, then whiz it to crumbs in a food processor with the chestnuts and tip into a large bowl.

9) Add the cooled onion mixture, the minced pork shoulder and drained peaches.

10) Using your hands, really squash and squidge everything until well mixed.

11) Put 250g of the stuffing mixture aside to cook with your turkey.

12)  Pack the rest into an appropriately sized oiled baking dish.

13) Flatten it down and use your hands to almost tuck the stuffing into the dish, so it mounds up in the middle.

14) Push the clementine halves into the top of the stuffing, cut side up.

15) Place 1 reserved sage leaf on each half.

16) Drizzle with 1 more tablespoon of oil.

17) Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for 50 minutes, or until golden, gnarly and cooked through.

18) Serve.
