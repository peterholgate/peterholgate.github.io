Pork with Lentils - Serves 8


Ingredients

1.2kg  x Lean pork cut into 3 x 3 cm  cubes

1 x 300g Red lentils (split), washed and drained

4 x Onion medium, Spanish sliced coarsely

4 x Carrots medium, sliced into large chunks

12 x Tablespoon of melted beef dripping (or olive oil)

1 x Teaspoon ground black pepper

1/8 x Teaspoon cayenne pepper

1½ x Teaspoon Herbes de Provence to taste.

2 x Bay leaves (Med)

4 x Cloves garlic, rough chopped

1.25 x Litres of chicken stock or use cubes

1.25 x Litres of boiling water (if not using chicken stock)

 1 x Tablespoon of fresh parsley, chopped


Method

1) Add chicken stock cubes to boiling water and mix well or use stock.

2) Pour dripping or oil into large heavy pot on the hob.

3) Add the pork, cayenne and half of the black pepper.

4) Cook on a hob at high heat until outside is seared light brown.

5) Add the onion.

6) Cook at medium heat until the onion is transparent.

7) Add carrots and cook for 2 min.

8) Add chicken stock and and washed lentils, mix.

9) Place bay leaves on top and put heavy lid on.

10) Transfer to an oven and cook for 90 mins at 180C.

11) Test at 90 mins lentils and pork must be soft and well cooked.

12) Cook for a further 30 mins if necessary.

13) Mix well and sprinkle on the remaining black pepper.

14) Sprinkle the Herbes de Provence on top.

15) Remove lid and cook at 200C for a further 5 mins.

16) Garnish with chopped parsley and serve.


2017-MEA-007-Pork and Lentils


Tip 1: Add 2 x rough chopped green peppers (Capsicums) as a variation

Tip 2: Good served with new potatoes in butter and garnished with parsley

Tip 3: Use a heavy pot that you can also use in an oven





Lamb Spinach Karahi (Karachi) - Serves 6


Ingredients

1 x 200g Ghee/Clarified Butter

4 x Medium Onions Roughly Chopped

1 x 400g Can Chopped Toms

1 x 120ml Water

1 x 65g (1.5 Pods) Garlic crushed

1 x 50g (10cm of a thick root) Fresh Ginger, peeled and finely chopped

1 x 900g Boneless leg or shoulder of lamb cut into 3-4cm cubes

1 x Tablespoon Salt

1 x Tablespoon Ground Turmeric

1 x Tablespoon Red Chilli Powder

1 x Tablespoon Paprika

1 x Tablespoon Ground Coriander

1 x Tablespoon Ground Cumin (or add as seeds when cooking onion)

1 x 350g Frozen Spinach

3 x Tablespoons Fresh Coriander (chopped)

2 x Medium sized Green Chillies with stalks removed

½ x Tablespoon Garam Masala

A pinch of ground cumin and freshly ground black pepper (to serve)

3 x Tablespoons Fresh Coriander (chopped) (to serve)


Method

1) Heat the ghee in a large, heavy based pan. Add the onions and cook over a medium heat, stirring now and then, for 20 minutes or until they are soft and a light brown.

2) Remove the fried onions with a slotted spoon, leaving the ghee, and put them in a liquidiser.

3) Add the tomatoes, water, ginger and garlic to the liquidizer and blend to a smooth puree.

4) Add the puree to the ghee left in the pan.

5) Add the lamb and salt then simmer for 30 minutes, until the lamb is half cooked and the sauce is well reduced.

5) Stir in the ground turmeric, chilli powder, cumin, paprika and coriander.

6) Simmer for 30-45 minutes for shoulder or 45-60min for leg, until the lamb is tender, adding a little water now and then if the sauce starts to stick.

6) Put half (175g) of the defrosted spinach leaves into a liquidiser and blend to a smooth puree. Set aside.

7) Rinse out the liquidiser and add the green chillies and 2-3 tablespoons of water and blend until smooth. Set aside.

8) When the lamb is cooked, there should be a layer of ghee floating on the top of the curry.

9) Stir in the spinach puree and the remaining spinach leaves and cook for 2 minutes.

10) Add green chilli puree (hot) to taste.

11) Simmer for 2 minutes more.

12) Stir in the Garam Masala.

13) Sprinkle with ground cumin, freshly ground black pepper and fresh Coriander just before you take it to the table.


2024-MEA-015-Lamb Spinach Karahi


Tip1: Serve with rice, naan breads or parathas etc.

Tip2: Even better on day 2!



Roasted brisket – Serves 6-8


Ingredients
1 x 3kg piece of rolled brisket about 150cm in diameter with some fat
1 x 50g Beef dripping
1 x Teaspoon ground cumin

1 x Teaspoon of ground coriander

½ x Teaspoon of ground cinnamon
1 x Teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper

4 x cloves of garlic sliced in half longways

6 x Bay leaves


Method
1) Remove beef from the fridge at least 1 hour before cooking.

2) Preheat the oven to 150°C about 15 minutes before cooking is to begin.

3) Lay a small piece of aluminium foil in the roasting tin and 3 large sheets of aluminium foil, enough to seal in the beef, on top of the small sheet in the roasting tin.

4) Rinse and dry the beef then place on the foil.

5) Melt the beef dripping then add the cumin, coriander, cinnamon and lots of freshly ground black pepper.

6) Spoon the spiced dripping over the top and sides of the beef.

7) Lay the bay leaves and garlic on top of the meat.

8) Pull up the foil at the sides and ends of the joint, pinching and folding the edges to make a good seal.

9) Put the parcel in a roasting tin and cook for around 6 to 7 hours at 150C until the beef feels very soft to the touch.

10) If you’re in a hurry, it will cook in around half that time at 180°C.

11) When the beef is done, leave it in the turned-off oven for 20 minutes to ‘rest’.

12) Open the parcel and carefully lift the beef onto a platter without piercing it to prevent loss of juices.

13) Pour the liquid into a jug and spoon off of some of the fat (use for gravy).

14) Serve in thick slices with gravy.


2024-MEA-Roasted Brisket


Tip1: Serve with horseradish sauce

Tip2: Use the broth as a basis for onion gravy
