Colombian Hot Sauce (Ají Piqué or Ají Picante)


INGREDIENTS

1 x Seeded small hot pepper (or 1 red habanero pepper)

½ x Cup white vinegar

¼ x Cup water

¼ x Teaspoon salt

1 x Teaspoon sugar

1 x Tablespoon lime juice

2 x Tablespoons vegetable oil

½ x Cup chopped fresh cilantro

¼ x Cup chopped fresh parsley

½ x Cup chopped scallions

½ x Cup chopped tomato


METHOD

1) Put the vinegar, water and habanero pepper in a blender for 2 minutes.

2) Place the remaining ingredients in a bowl and stir to mix.

3) Add the vinegar and habanero mix to the bowl and mix well.

4) Pour in a glass jar and cover.

5) Refrigerate up to 10 days.


Tip1 Ají picante or Ají Piqué is a spicy sauce from Antioquia. It is an absolute must for Colombian Empanadas and it is the perfect sauce to flavour soups, stews and the perfect dipping sauce for Patacones.

White Sauce with Cream (ie Bechamel)


INGREDIENTS

2 x Tablespoons of butter

2 x Tablespoons of Flour

2 x Cups heavy whipping cream

1 x Clove garlic crushed

Black Pepper

Salt


METHOD

1. Melt 2 tablespoons of butter (the REAL stuff).  Melt on medium-high heat in a sauce pan.

2. Add 2 tablespoons of flour.  And start whisking!

3. Add about 1 to 2 teaspoons of salt and some pepper.  If you’re using ‘salted butter’ you might not need that much to start off.  Remember, you can always add a little more later…you just can’t take it back out.

4. Add the garlic.

5. Whisk the butter-flour mixture (also known as a ‘Roux’) for about 2 minutes to cook the taste of flour out.

6. Add 1 cup of heavy whipping cream, keep stirring.  You’ll start seeing it thicken within about 1 minute.

7. Add the other cup of cream and keep stirring.  The sauce will then start reheating back up and be thicker within 3-5 minutes.  (Depends upon how your pan and burner kicks out the heat.)

Add cheddar cheese for a cheese sauce.

Deliziosa Italian Tomato Sauce (LCHF Friendly)


INGREDIENTS

20ml x Coconut oil (or olive oil)

20g x Butter

200g x Onions, finely chopped

2 x Celery stalks, finely chopped

60ml x Parsley, finely chopped

5ml x Demerara sugar

1kg x Jam tomatoes, cored and halved (or 2 cans)

100g x Young carrots, finely chopped

1 x Sprigs fresh thyme

1ml x Dried oregano

1ml x Dried basil

1 x Sprig of fresh basil for garnish (optional)

50ml x Italian tomato paste

2 x Bay leaves

4 x Cloves garlic crushed

 50ml x Red wine

1 x Salt to and black pepper taste


METHOD

1. Place the oil & butter in a large pot, on medium heat.

2. Add the chopped onions and fry gently, until transparent, without browning, for 5mins.

3. Add red wine and reduce for 5 mins.

4. Stir in the remaining ingredients and simmer for 1.5 hours.

5. Puree in a food processor or use blitz stick (optional if vegetables finely chopped).

6. Cool and pack in containers and freeze until required.
