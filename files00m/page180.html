Spicy Beets and Carrots


INGREDIENTS

1 x 500g Beetroot, cooked and peeled

1 x 500g Carrots, peeled

2  x Tablespoons grated fresh ginger

1/2 x Cup brown sugar

1/2 x Cup orange juice

1/4 x Cup cider vinegar

1 x Grated zest of 1 orange

1/2 x Teaspoon ground cinnamon

1/2 x Teaspoon mace


METHOD

1) Cut the beetroot and carrots into slices or chunks.

2) Steam or boil the carrots in lightly salted water about 5 minutes or until tender but not soft.

3) Drain and combine the ginger, sugar, orange juice, vinegar, orange zest and spices in a saucepan.

4) Bring the mixture to a simmer.

5) Cook until thickened.

6) Simmer the cooked beets and carrots in this sauce for about 5 minutes, then serve.

Traditional Braised Red Cabbage with Apples

Serves 10-12


INGREDIENTS

1 x 1 kg Red cabbage

1 x 450g Cooking apples, peeled, cored and chopped small

1 x 450g Onions, chopped small

1 clove garlic, chopped very small

¼ x Whole nutmeg, freshly grated

¼ x Level teaspoon ground cinnamon

¼ x Level teaspoon ground cloves

3 x Level tablespoons brown sugar

3 x Lablespoons wine vinegar

1 x 15g Butter

1 x Salt and freshly milled black pepper, to taste


METHOD

1) Pre-heat the oven to gas mark 2, 300°F (150°C).

2) First discard the tough outer leaves of the cabbage, cut it into quarters and remove the hard stalk.

3) Then shred the rest of the cabbage finely, using your sharpest knife (although you can shred it in a food processor).
4) Next, in a fairly large casserole, arrange a layer of shredded cabbage seasoned with salt and pepper.

5) Next add a layer of chopped onions and apples with a sprinkling of garlic, spices and sugar.

6) Continue with these alternate layers until everything is in.

7) Now pour in the wine vinegar, lastly add dots of butter on the top.

8) Put a tight lid on the casserole and let it cook very slowly in the oven for 2-2½ hours, stirring everything around once or twice during the cooking.

9) Red cabbage, once cooked, will keep warm without coming to any harm, and it will also re-heat very successfully.

10) Serve.


Tip1: Feezes well

Perfect Hummus

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

200g x Dried chickpeas
1½ x Teaspoon bicarbonate of soda
6 x Tablespoons tahini
1 x Juice of a lemon, or more to taste 
3 x Cloves garlic, crushed, or according to taste
1 x Pinch of cumin
1 x Salt, to taste
1 x 50ml Olive oil, to top
1 x Pinch of paprika or za'tar, to top (optional)


METHOD

1) Put the chickpeas in a bowl and cover with twice the volume of cold water.

2) Stir in 1 tsp of bicarbonate of soda and leave to soak for 24 hours.

3)  Drain the chickpeas, rinse well and put in a large pan. Cover with cold water and add the rest of the bicarb.

4) Bring to the boil, then turn down the heat and simmer gently until they're tender – they need to be easy to mush, and almost falling apart, which will take between 1 and 4 hours depending on your chickpeas.

5) Add more hot water if they seem to be boiling dry.

6) Leave them to cool in the water, and then drain well, reserving the cooking liquid, and setting aside a spoonful of chickpeas as a garnish.

7) Mix the tahini with half the lemon juice and half the crushed garlic – it should tighten up – then stir in enough cooled cooking liquid to make a loose paste.

8) Add this, and the chickpeas, to a food processor and whizz to make a purée.

9) Add the cumin and a generous pinch of salt, then gradually tip in enough cooking water to give a soft paste – it should just hold its shape, but not be claggy.

10) Taste, and add more lemon juice, garlic or salt according to taste.

11) Tip into a bowl, and when ready to serve, drizzle with olive oil, garnish with the reserved chickpeas and sprinkle with paprika or za'tar if using.

12) Serve or freeze.
