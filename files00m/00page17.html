Recipe POR-001

Pork with Lentils - Serves 8

 

Ingredients

1.2kg  x Lean pork cut into 3 x 3 cm  cubes

1 x 300g Red lentils (split), washed and drained

4 x Onion medium, Spanish sliced coarsely

4 x Carrots medium, sliced into large chunks

12 x Tablespoon of melted beef dripping (or olive oil)

1 x Teaspoon ground black pepper

1/8 x Teaspoon cayenne pepper

1½ x Teaspoon Herbes de Provence to taste.

2 x Bay leaves (Med)

4 x Cloves garlic, rough chopped

1.25 x Litres of chicken stock or use cubes

 1.25 x Litres of boiling water (if not using chicken stock)

 1 x Tablespoon of fresh parsley, chopped


Method

1) Add chicken stock cubes to boiling water and mix well or use stock.

2) Pour dripping or oil into large heavy pot on the hob.

3) Add the pork, cayenne and half of the black pepper.

4) Cook on a hob at high heat until outside is seared light brown.

5) Add the onion.

6) Cook at medium heat until the onion is transparent.

7) Add carrots and cook for 2 min.

8) Add chicken stock and and washed lentils, mix.

9) Place bay leaves on top and put heavy lid on.

10) Transfer to an oven and cook for 90 mins at 180C.

11) Test at 90 mins lentils and pork must be soft and well cooked.

12) Cook for a further 30 mins if necessary.

13) Mix well and sprinkle on the remaining black pepper.

14) Sprinkle the Herbes de Provence on top.

15) Remove lid and cook at 200C for a further 5 mins.

16) Garnish with chopped parsley and serve.


Tip 1 Add 2 x rough chopped green peppers (Capsicums) as a variation

Tip 2 Good served with new potatoes in butter and garnished with parsley

Tip 3 Use a heavy pot that you can also use in an oven

Recipe CHA-001

PFH Chana Masaledar - Serves 12


Ingredients

1 x 50ml Ghee (or Oil)

1 x 500g Dried chickpeas soaked overnight in water

1 x Teaspoon cumin Seeds

3 x Teaspoon garam masala

3 x Teaspoon ground Coriander

¾ x Teaspoon cayenne pepper

1 x Teaspoon salt

3 x Medium onions chopped

1 x 5cm Piece of  fresh root ginger grated

6 x Cloves garlic crushed  

3 x Tablespoons tomato puree

1 x Juice of a lemon


Method

1) Boil chickpeas in water for about 60 to 70 mins until tender (remove any froth).

2)Turn off heat cover and leave until required.

3) Add oil to a large frying pan and heat on a medium flame until the oil is hot.

4) Add the cumin seeds and when they turn dark add the onion.

5) Fry the onion for about 10 mins or until golden brown.

6) Turn the heat down and add the garam masala and ground coriander, stir fry for about 3 mins.

7) Add the garlic and ginger and stir fry for 3 mins.

8) Add the tomato puree and stir again for about 1 min.

9) Transfer to a large, heavy pan.

10) Drain the chickpeas, reserving the liquid and add about 400mls of the liquid to the large pan.

11) Add the chickpeas, cayenne and lemon juice.

12) Mix well and cook gently, stirring for about 30 mins.

13) Add additional chickpea liquid as required to avoid drying out.

14) Stir gently to avoid breaking the chickpeas.

15) Eat as an accompaniment to other Indian food or with Indian bread and pickles as a snack.


Tip 1 Good to freeze

Tip 2 Large iron pans recommended

Tip 3 Chana = Chickpeas/Garbazos

Recipe CHI-001

Chicken with Parma Ham - Serves 2


Ingredients

4 x 125g Chicken breasts, skinned

3 x Tablespoons of pesto (red or green)

4 x Sun-dried tomatoes, drained of oil and sliced

8 x Thin slices Parma ham (or similar cured ham)

1 x Tablespoon olive oil


Method

1) First preheat the oven to 200 C.

2) Spread the chicken breasts out on a board and with a sharp knife, make a small slit in the top of each one to create a 'pocket'.

3) Spread the inside of the pocket with the classic pesto.

4) Layer the sun-dried tomato slices onto each chicken breast.

5 Wrap each chicken breast with two slices of Parma ham and put them on a baking sheet.

6) Finally drizzle each one with oil and bake them in the oven at 200C for 20-25 mins or until the chicken is cooked through.


Tip1 Use sun-dried tomatoes in oil for best results
