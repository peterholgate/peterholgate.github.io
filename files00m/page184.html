Potatoes and Onions Lyonnaise

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

6 x Medium, brown (Spanish) onions sliced into rings

1 x 200-250g (3-4oz) Salted Butter

8 x Medium Potatoes cut into whole slices

Olive Oil (or other vegetable oil)

4 Sticks of Thyme (Optional)

4 x Bay Leaves (Optional)

1 x Sprinkle Ground Cayenne Pepper (Optional)

2 x Cloves of crushed garlic (Optional)

Salt to taste

Ground black pepper to taste (optional)


METHOD

1) Melt 2/3 of the butter in a large oven dish or baking tray.

2) Add the onions only and stir into the butter and spread out in the dish.

3) Sprinkle with black pepper and salt.

4) Bake in the oven at 180C* (about 360f) for 35 to 40 mins to soften without browning; stir to ensure even cooking.

5) Taste; should be soft and sweet; if not bake a bit longer.

6) Remove from oven and stir to spread out evenly.

7) Add the potato slices overlapping slightly in lines; reverse the direction of each line.

8) Ensure that all the onions are covered by the potatoes.

9) Melt the remaining butter and pour, as evenly as possible, to coat the tops of the potatoes.

10) Bake in the oven at 180C (about 360f) for about 35 to 40 mins or until lightly browned.

11) Sprinkle lightly with olive (or other) oil to ensure all the slices are oily.

12) Sprinkle with black pepper and salt to taste.

13) Bake at 220C (about 420f) for about 10 mins to lightly crisp.

14) Serve hot!


Tip1 Sprinkle with ground cayenne pepper to the layered potatoes for a spicy kick

Tip2 Add bay leaves and thyme, near the end of cooking, for a herby flavour

Tip3 Add crushed garlic to the melted butter, if you like garlic!


*Turn the oven down if they start browning

Squashed Brussels Sprouts


INGREDIENTS

1 x 1 kg Brussels sprouts, washed and trimmed

1 x 150g Raw quality chorizo

1 x Olive oil

1 x 100g Vac-packed chestnuts

2 x Sprigs of fresh rosemary, stripped

1 x Tablespoon sherry vinegar


METHOD

1) Preheat the oven to 180°C/350°F/gas 4.

2) Wash and trim the Brussels sprouts.

3) Cook for 8 minutes in a pan of boiling salted water, then drain well.

4) Squeeze the chorizo out of its skin, crumbling it into a roasting tray with 1 tablespoon of olive oil.

5) On the hob, fry over a medium heat for 6 minutes, until you have ruby oil.

6) Crumble in the vac-packed chestnuts.

7) Add the rosemary leaves and shake about.

8) Tip in the sprouts and toss in the sherry vinegar.

9) Squash and flatten the sprouts with a potato masher so they suck up more flavour.

10) Roast for 25 minutes, or until starting to colour.

11) Serve.

Spicy Bean Soup

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

1 x Medium onion finely chopped

3-4 x Garlic cloves crushed

1 x Medium carrot cut into small cubes

1 x Medium sweet potato cut into small cubes (or use more carrot)

1 x Red pepper cut into small squares

½ x Vegetable stock cube

2 x 300g Can of mixed Beans (or kidney beans) drained and rinsed

1 x 400g Can chopped tomatoes

1 x Teaspoon vegetable oil

1 x Teaspoon chilli powder

1 x Teaspoon ground cumin (optional)

1 x Teaspoon dried oregano (optional)

Ground black pepper to taste (optional)


METHOD

1) In a large, heavy pan fry the onions over a medium heat for about 5 mins until transparent

2) Add garlic, red pepper, carrots and sweet potatoes, mix well and fry for about 5mins.

3) Add the chilli, cumin and oregano, stir for a further 5 mins.

4) Measure 300ml of boiling water, crumble in the stock cube and stir to dissolve.

5) Stir in the beans, tomatoes, stock and add black pepper.

6) Stir well and cook on the hob for about 30mins, stirring every five minutes or so.

7) Add more black pepper if required (if using).

If you want smooth soup, rather than chunky soup, you can blend it at this stage but use CARE HOT!

8) Serve and/or or keep in fridge for a few days and serve.


Tip1 Add extra chilli powder for a spicy kick
