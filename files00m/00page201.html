Pizza Recipe-Italian

Serves: 4

Preparation Time: 30 Mins (plus 1 Hour Resting Time)

Cooking Time: 15mins


INGREDIENTS

For the dough:

1 x Sachet (7g) quick-acting yeast or 30g fresh yeast

2 x Teaspoons caster sugar

1 x 500g Extra fine ‘00’ pizza flour, plus extra to dust

1 x Teaspoon fine salt

2 x Tablespoons olive oil, plus extra to grease for the topping

1 x 400g tin good-quality plum tomatoes

1 x Tablespoon olive oil

1 x Tablespoon dried oregano, plus extra to serve

1 x Teaspoon fine salt

1⁄2 x Teaspoon cayenne pepper

1 x 200g Freshly grated mozzarella cheese

Plus your choice from the following:

1 x Onion (finely sliced and tossed in olive oil and salt)

1 x Sliced mushrooms

1 x Pieces of ham

1 x Chopped peppers

1 x Olives

1 x Pesto, etc.


METHOD:

To make the dough:

1) Dissolve the yeast and sugar in 250ml warm water and allow to rest for 5 to 10 minutes. (The yeast should make the water froth and bubble, but don’t worry if it doesn’t happen.)

2) Mix the flour and salt together in a bowl.

3) Using a fork, mix in the yeasty water until it starts to form a dough.

4) Add the oil and bring together into a smooth dough.

5) Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead until it has an elastic, smooth texture – time to give your arms a workout! If the dough sticks to your hands, add a little extra flour.

6) Place the dough back in the bowl and cover with a clean damp tea towel.

7) Leave for 30 minutes to 1 hour in a warm place, until it has doubled in size.

8) Preheat the oven to its maximum temperature or 250 ̊C.

To prepare your toppings:

9) In a food processor, blend the tomatoes with the oil, oregano, salt and cayenne pepper.

10) Get your choice of toppings ready and grease 4 baking trays (approx 20cm x 28cm) with a little olive oil (it’s important not to overload the pizzas, so stick to a maximum of 3 toppings per pizza).

11) When your dough is ready, divide into 4 tennis-ball-sized amounts and mould until they form smooth balls – cover them with your damp clean tea towel so that they don’t form a crust.

12) On a floured surface, roll each of the balls of dough to a think rectangle shape to fit the baking trays; the dough should be quite thin – about the thickness of cardboard.

13) Transfer the pizza bases to the trays.

14) Place a ladleful of the tomato sauce in the centre of each base and spread towards the edges, leaving a 1cm edge for the crust to form.

15) Sprinkle each pizza with grated mozzarella and your choice of toppings.

16) Place in the hot oven and cook for 7 to 10 minutes until the base is cooked and the top golden.

17) If the base isn’t very crispy, place it on the base of your oven for an extra 3-5 mins.

18) Serve, sprinkled with a little oregano.

Melanzane Parmigiana Italian

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

3 x Aubergine (1kg), sliced thinly lengthways

2 to 3 x Teaspoons of fine salt for salting the Aubergine

8 x Tablespoons olive oil

1 x 400g Mozzarella cheese chopped into 0.5cm squares

1 x 100g Grated Parmesan or Pecorino cheese

1 x Medium onion, peeled rough chopped

1 x Medium carrot rough chopped

3 x Cloves of garlic, peeled and chopped

1 x Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

1⁄2 x Glass of red or white wine (optional)

2 x 400g Tins chopped tomatoes

1 x Tablespoon tomato purée

1 x Bunch of fresh basil

1 x Teaspoon sugar

5 x Tablespoons of plain flour

1⁄4 x Teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)

1 x Vegetable stock cube (optional)


METHOD

1) Place a layer of aubergine slices on a plate then sprinkle with salt and cover with kitchen paper.

2) Add more layers until you have used all of the aubergine then finish with kitchen paper.

3) Weigh the aubergines down using something heavy, like a saucepan filled with water, and leave for 30 minutes so that the aubergine releases wate

4) To a small blender add the onion, carrot, garlic, half of the olive oil then blend to a smoot paste adding a little water if necessary to make the sofrito.

5) Add the sofrito (without oil) to a heavy pan and fry gently for 10mins.

5) Pour in the wine (if using) and keep stirring for 2 or 3 minutes to burn off the alcohol.

6) Blend the tomatoes to make a smooth paste (passata).

7) Add the tomatoes, tomato purée, sugar, cayenne pepper and stock cube to the pan and simmer for 20 to 30 minutes, until the sauce has thickened.

8) Add fresh basil leaves then salt and pepper to season to taste.

9) Coat the dried aubergines in flour then fry in olive oil until golden brown and crispy (deep frying is best).

10) When ready layer the aubergines on kitchen paper to soak up the oil.

11) Preheat the oven to 180 ̊C.

12) Add a thin layer of sauce to the bottom of the dish.

13) Add a layer of cooked aubergine slices to a deep oven dish (circa 30 x 20cm).

14) Add a thin layer of the sauce to cover the aubergine.

15) Sprinkle on about a quarter of the mozzarella, some parmesan.

16) Repeat from 13 to 15 until all the aubergine and cheeses used NB no cheese or basil on the top layer of sauce.

17) Cover the top with foil and cook in the oven for 40 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through.

18) When cooked sprinkle with parmesan and return to the oven for 5mins to melt the cheese.

19) Serve with parmesan, fresh basil and black pepper.


TIP1: Prepare our aubergines and the tomato sauce a day early so that when it comes to cook the dish, all you have to do is layer it up and cook.

Mushroom Rissotto

Preparation Time: 5 Mins

Cooking Time: 15 Mins

Serves: 3 - 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x White onion, peeled and finely chopped

500g x Mixed wild mushrooms, cleaned and roughly torn (if you don’t have wild mushrooms, use sliced chestnut mushrooms)

350g x Arborio rice

1 x Splash of white wine

1 litre x Stock

1 x Mushroom stock cube

20g x Dried porcini, rehydrated

80g x Grated parmesan (plus extra for serving)

1/4  x Teaspoon Pinch of pepperoncino powder (or cayenne pepper)

1 x Knob of butter

1 x Tablespoon of double cream

1 x Salt and black pepper, to taste

1 x Truffle oil


METHOD

1) Put 1000ml of boiling water in a jug then use a little of this water (approx. 200ml) to cover the porcini in a separate cup.

2) Then dissolve the stock cube in the jug of boiling water.

3) Stir to dissolve and then put to one side.

4) Heat some olive oil in a large pan on hob then add the onion.

5) Cook off for a few minutes until softened . Next add the wild mushrooms and keep cooking until lovely and soft.

6) Next add the rice and stir around keeping it moving so that it doesn’t stick.

7) After about 1 minute (when the rice has begun to go translucent but not burnt) add the wine and let the wine evaporate stirring constantly.

8) Start adding a ladel of stock, and keep stirring until the liquid is absorbed, continue this until you have used half the stock.

9) Carefully take the porcini out of the water and squeeze out the excess water saving the juices.

10) Roughly chop the porcini and then stir them through the rice along with the porcini juices as well.

11) Add the parmesan, pepperoncino and then finish adding the remaining stock until all the liquid has been absorbed and the rice is al dente.

12) Then add the butter and the cream, stir through and then put a lid on for 1 minute.

13) Season to taste and serve up with another grating of parmesan and a little drizzle of truffle oil.


Tip1 Once your risotto is ready to serve (and al dente) eat immediately otherwise it will overcook.
