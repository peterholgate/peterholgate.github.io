Thai Style Fishcakes

Serves 4-6


INGREDIENTS

1 x 1/2 kg White-fleshed fish fillets (I like cod, but any will work)

6 x Kaffir lime leaves, snipped into thin strips with scissors

3x Tablespoon. coconut milk

2 x Tablespoon fish sauce

1/2 xTeaspoon shrimp paste (or 1 extra tablespoon fish sauce)

1/2 x Tablespoon chilli powder

1/3 x Teaspoon ground cumin

1/4 x Teaspoon ground coriander

1/2 x Teaspoon brown sugar

3 x Spring onions sliced (scallions)

1 x 4cm Piece galangal grated (or use gingerroot)

3 x Cloves garlic

1 x Red chilli, sliced (or 1/2 teaspoon dried crushed chilli)

1/3 x Cucumber

Thai sweet chilli sauce

Fresh coriander leaf

Fresh lime juice


METHOD

1) Rinse fresh or defrosted fish and pat thoroughly dry.

2) Cut into chunks and place in food processor.

3) In a cup, combine the coconut milk, fish sauce, shrimp paste, chili powder, cumin, ground coriander, and brown sugar.

4) Stir with a fork to combine, then pour into the processor over the fish.

5) Add remaining ingredients (kaffir lime leaf strips, green onion, galangal/ginger, garlic, and chili).

6) Pulse to create a thick fish paste.

7) Picking up a small amount in your hand (about the size of a golf ball) pat the paste into a small cake and set on a clean plate.

8) Set plate of cakes in the refrigerator for 10 minutes to firm up.

9) Pour coconut oil into a small frying pan or wok at least 1 inch deep.

10) Heat oil to high medium temperature and gently place cakes in oil.

11) Allow to fry 30 seconds to 1 minute.

12) Turn gently lifting cakes from the bottom of the pan (they may stick a little).

13) Fry until golden-brown then drain on paper towel.

14) Serve with Thai sweet chili sauce, chopped cucumber, fresh coriander, and a squeeze of lime juice.


2013-FIS-001-Fishcakes Thai Style


Tip1: If your fish paste is too wet to easily form into cakes, add a little flour or breadcrumbs to the mix.

Tip2: You can make the fish paste up to 24 hours in advance. Cover and set in the refrigerator, then form into cakes and fry.


PFH Lamb Rogan Josh from Paste

Serves 4 - 6


INGREDIENTS

50ml x Oil or ghee

2 x Bay leaves

6 x Cloves

1 x Cinnamon sticks

12 x Black peppercorns

3 x Black cardamoms

2 x Bried red chillies

3 x Medium onions sliced

5cm x Fresh root ginger grated

6 x Cloves garlic crushed  

1/2 x Jar Patak's lamb rogan josh paste

800g x Lean lamb cut into medium cubes  

1 x 400g Tin chopped tomatoes

800ml x Chicken stock

2 x Tablespoons of balsamic vinegar

1 x Fresh green hot chillies whole (or more)

100g x Red lentils

1 x Water as required

200ml x Yoghurt

1 x Bunch fresh coriander

2 x Lemons

1 x Jar of Lime Pickle

1 x Jar of Mango Chutney


METHOD

1) Fry the spices whole ie: bay leaves, cloves, cinnamon sticks,  black peppercorns, black cardamoms and dried red chillies in the oil about 5 mins in a large heavy pot until dark brown.

2) Add and fry Onions for about 20mins until golden brown.

3) Add the ginger, garlic, coriander stalks and paste and fry for about 3 minutes.

4) Add the lamb and fry for about 5 mins, stirring to cover with spices etc.

5) Add tomatoes, chicken stock, lentils and chillies, bring to boil then simmer for about 60 mins adding water to avoid drying out.

6) Garnish with coriander and lemon (or lime) wedges.

7) Serve with riata, chutney, pickle and fresh coriander.


Tip 1 Large iron pot recommended

Chicken Tikka Masala from Paste

Serves 4 – 6


INGREDIENTS

1 x 50 ml of ghee or oil

2x Bay leaves

6 x Cloves

1 x Cinnamon sticks

12 x Black peppercorns

3 x Black cardamoms

2 x Dried red chillies

3 x Medium onions sliced

1 x 5cm piece of fresh root ginger grated

6 x Cloves garlic crushed  

1 x 1/2 Jar Patak's Tikka Masala paste

6 x Medium chicken breasts cut into large cubes  

1 x 400g Tin chopped tomatoes

1 x 400ml Coconut milk (or 250ml cream)

1 x 200g Natural yoghurt

1 x Fresh green hot chillies whole (or more)

3 x Teaspoons of garam masala

Water as required

1 x Bunch fresh coriander

2 x Lemons

1 x Jar of Lime Pickle

1 x Jar of Mango Chutney


METHOD

1) Fry the whole spices ie: bay leaves, cloves, cinnamon sticks, black peppercorns, black cardamoms and dried red chillies in the oil about 5 mins until dark brown.

2) Add and fry Onions for 20mins or more until golden brown (Important).

3) Add the ginger, garlic and masala paste and fry for about 3 minutes.

4) Add the chicken and fry for about 5 mins, stirring to cover with spices etc.

5) Add tomatoes, coconut milk, yoghurt and chillies, bring to boil then simmer for about 25 mins adding water to avoid drying out.

6) About 5 mins before serving sprinkle top with fresh garam masala and continue to simmer.

7) Garnish with coriander and lemon (or lime) wedges.

8) Serve with riata, chutney, pickle and fresh coriander.


Tip 1: Large iron pot recommended
