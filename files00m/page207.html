Vietnamese Chicken Salad

(With Nuoc Cham salad dressing)


Serves 3-4

Preparation 15mins or so.


INGREDIENTS

Salad:

500g x Ready cooked chicken, cut into thin strips (or 2 large cooked breasts)

6 x Heaped cups (250ml) wombok cabbage (Napa cabbage), finely shredded

1/2 x Red onion, very finely sliced (so it's floppy)

1 x Red capsicum/bell pepper, finely sliced into thin strips

2 x Mini cucumbers, remove seeds, cut in half, lengthwise, then finely sliced into half moons

1 x Large carrot, peeled then julienned (I use a shredder)

1 x Large chilli, deseeded then julienned, optional (Note 3)

Dressing (Nuoc Cham):

2 x Tablespoon lime juice

2 x Tablespoon rice vinegar

1/4 x Cup fish sauce

1/4 x Cup Olive or Avocado oil

1 x Tablespoon white or brown sugar

1 x Large garlic clove, very finely minced

2 x Teaspoons red chilli, deseeded then very finely minced (birds eye or similar)

Garnish:

1 x Cup (tightly packed) mint leaves, large leaves roughly torn by hand

1 x Cup (tightly packed) coriander/cilantro leaves

1/2 x Cup peanuts, roasted unsalted or salted , finely chopped


METHOD

Shake dressing ingredients in a jar.

Set aside 10 minutes to let flavours meld.

Place all the salad ingredients in a very large bowl.

Pour over half the Dressing and toss well.

Set aside 5 minutes (veg will soften slightly making it more "slaw-like")

Add the chicken.

Sprinkle with the coriander and mint leaves.

Sprinkle with the peanuts.

Add most of the remaining dressing to coat the chicken etc.

Taste then add more dressing if you want.


Tip1 Use Nuoc Cham salad dressing as a dip

Tip2 Serve without chicken as a side salad

Sweet and Sour Pork


Prep Time 15 min

Cook Time 20 min

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1/2 x Cup brown sugar

1/2 x Cup cider vinegar

1/2 x Cup ketchup

3 x Teaspoons soy sauce

500g x Boneless pork loin, cut into 1-inch cubes

1 x Tablespoon cooking oil

1 x Medium onion, chopped

2 x Medium carrots, sliced

1 x Medium green pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces

1/2 x Teaspoon minced garlic

1/4 x Teaspoon ground ginger (or use fresh)

1 x Can pineapple chunks, drained (or use fresh

4 x Portions of Hot cooked rice

1 x Thinly sliced green onions

1 x Sprinkle of sesame seeds

1 x De-seeded, chopped ‘birds eye’ chilli (optional)


METHOD

1) In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, vinegar, ketchup and soy sauce.

2) Pour half into a large resealable plastic bag; add pork.

3) Seal bag and turn to coat; refrigerate for 30 minutes.

4) Set remaining marinade aside.

5) Separate the marinade from the pork.

6) In a large skillet, cook pork in oil for 3 minutes.

7) Add the onion, carrots, green pepper, garlic and ginger; sauté until pork is tender.

8) Add chilli, if using.

9) Add reserved marinade plus the ‘used’ marinade.

10) Bring to a boil; cook for 1 minute.

11) Stir in the pineapple.

12) Serve with rice and if desired, garnished with green onions and sesame seeds.


Tip1 Use cornflour slurry to thicken

Tip2 Add chopped celery for more crunch

Homemade Punjabi Style Chicken Curry


INGREDIENTS

Chicken marinade:

250g x Diced chicken breast

1 x Teaspoon turmeric powder  

1 x Teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder

1/2 x Teaspoon salt

½ x Juice of lemon

1 x Tablespoon garlic paste

Masala sauce:

4-5 x Tablespoon ghee

1 x Teaspoon cumin seeds

2 x Bay leaves

2 x Red onions (finely chopped)

2 x Tablespoon fresh ginger and garlic paste

2 x Teaspoon turmeric powder

1 x Teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder

2 x Tablespoon coriander powder

2 x Green chillies (sliced and seedless)

1 x Tablespoon of water

2 Tablespoon plain yoghurt

3 x Tomatoes pureed or half a can

1 x Salt to taste

200ml x Hot water


METHOD

1) Mix the Chicken with the marinade spices and store in fridge until used (30mins).

2) Heat the ghee in a cooking pot at medium heat.

3) Add the cumin seeds and bay leaves then fry until the seeds are dark brown.

4) Add the onions and fry until golden brown.

5) Add fresh ginger and garlic paste then cook for 3-4mins.

6) Turn the gas to low.

7) Add turmeric, Kashmiri red chilli, coriander powder, green chillies and a tablespoon of water.

8) Cook for 30-40 secs on a low flame.

9) Add yoghurt and cook for 3–4mins on a low flame (optional).

10) When the fat rises to the top add the pureed tomatoes and salt then cook for 3-4mins.

11) Turn the heat to medium and add the seer the chicken for 3–4mins.

12) Add hot water and reduce heat to low.

13) Cook for 5–6mins until the chicken is cooked.

14) Add garam masala, julienned ginger, coriander leaves and Kasuri Methi leaves.

15) Add the juice of half a lemon (optional), stir lightly and serve.
