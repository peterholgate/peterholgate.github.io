Slow Cooked Leg of Lamb

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

5 x Tablespoons Butter or Ghee

1 x 2kg Leg of Lamb

1 x Leaves from 2 largish sprigs of fresh rosemary

12 x Fresh Mint leaves

6 x Cloves of Garlic sliced

2 x Red Onions, roughly chopped

1 x Medium Butternut Squash, peeled, deseeded and roughly chopped

2 x Carrots, roughly chopped

2 x Sticks Celery, trimmed and chopped

2 x Leeks, trimmed and chopped

1 x 400g Cans Chopped Tomatoes

1 x ½ Cup of Wine


METHOD

1) Make incisions all over the lamb with a sharp knife and push in half of the sliced garlic.

2) Heat 2 tablespoons of Ghee in a heavy (or non-stick) frying pan and brown the Lamb all over.

3) Put the Lamb in the slow cooker

4) Brown the Red Onions, Celery and Carrots in the same pan with the Rosemary leaves.

5) Brown the chopped leeks in the same pan.

6) Brown the Butternut squash in the same pan.

7) Add all of the vegetables to the slow cooker.

8) Add the tomatoes, wine and stock to the pan with the remaining garlic and bring to a boil.

9) Pour the tomatoes and stock into the slow cooker and stir to coat the Lamb and Vegetables.

10) Add the fresh mint leaves.

11) Cook for 6-8 hours until the lamb is falling off the bone and the vegetables are tender.

12) Turn the Lamb and stir the vegetables halfway through cooking.

13) Slice or pull the lamb and serve with the vegetables and sauce.

Try also: Substitute tomatoes with a full cup of red or white wine and use Thyme (Lots) instead of Rosemary.


Tip1 To freeze, allow the lamb to cool before removing from the bone and transferring to a freezer-proof container, along with the sauce and veg. Freeze for up to 1 month. Defrost overnight in the fridge.

Roast Leg of Lamb with Garlic and Rosemary


INGREDIENTS

1 x 2kg Leg of lamb, on the bone

3 x Large garlic cloves, finely grated

20g x Butter, softened

4-5 x Fresh rosemary leaves finely chopped

1 x Freshly ground black pepper to taste

125ml x Red wine

1 x Small knob of butter

1 x Heaped teaspoon of plain flour


METHOD

1) Preheat the oven to 200C/Gas 6.

2) Remove the lamb from the fridge and allow it to get to room temperature.

3) Mix the garlic, rosemary and butter with a fork to make a soft paste.

4) Season with Black pepper.

5) With a sharp thin knife or a metal skewer, make between 30 and 50 fingertip sized incisions into the flesh of the lamb.

6) Work the butter and garlic mixture into the lamb by massaging it into the meat, trying to work it into the holes that you've created.

7) Place the lamb in a roasting tin, cover loosely with foil and place in the preheated oven.

8) After 30 minutes, remove the foil and leave to roast for a further 50-60 minutes (for medium) or until cooked to your liking.

9) At the end of cooking time, remove the lamb from the oven and leave it to rest in the roasting tin for 10-15 minutes, with some foil on top to keep it warm.

10) For the gravy, heat a heavy-based frying pan on the hob and pour the pan juices from the roasting tin into the pan (be careful as it may splatter).

11) Add the red wine then urn the heat down and leave the sauce to reduce.

12) To thicken the sauce, add about a teaspoon of beurre manie (mix together the flour and butter to make a paste) to the pan and whisk well, until the sauce has the desired consistency.

13) To serve, slice the lamb and pour over the gravy. Garnish with a couple stalks of fresh rosemary.


Tip1 Add chopped mint to the gravy.

Tip2 Good with potatoes dauphinoise and fresh vegetables.

Lamb Shank for Slow Cooker

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

3 x Lamb Shanks (400-500g each)

3 x Red Onions, finely chopped

6 x Tablespoons of currants or raisins or chopped, dried apricots or a mix

2 x Cloves of garlic, finely grated

60g x Beef Dripping

600ml x Chicken Stock

200ml x Beer (brown)

3 x Tablespoons of Worcestershire sauce

3 x Tablespoons of marmalade

5-6 x Fresh rosemary sprigs, leaves removed and finely chopped

1 x Freshly ground black pepper to taste

5-6 x Stalks of Mint, leaves removed and finely chopped

60ml x Virgin olive oil


METHOD

1) Turn on the slow cooker, auto or medium

2) Melt half the dripping in a large frying pan and sear the lamb shanks, for 10mins or so, until brown all over.

3) Add the lamb to the slow cooker leaving the fat in the frying pan.

4) Add to the frying pan and melt the remaining dripping.

5) Add the red onions and fry for about 20mins to brown without burning.

6) Add the rosemary and garlic.

7) Add the dried fruit and fry for 5 mins.

8) Add the marmalade and stir.

9) Add the Worcestershire sauce and stir.

10) Add the beer and boil for 5 min or more to reduce by half.

11) Add the chicken stock and bring up to boil.

12) Pour the contents into the slow cooker (VERY HOT).

13) Cook for 6-8 hours.

14) Remove the lamb shanks (HOT).

15) Cool then liquidise the sauce (optional).

16) Replace the lamb and cook for a further 30mins to reheat.

17) Add the mint leaves and olive oil to a pestle and motor and crush together until the mint is very finely divided.

18) Serve the lamb shanks and sauce then drizzle with the mint oil.


Tips1 Add hot oven roasted butternut squash, cut into medium size cubes and cooked with garlic, chilli and thyme, after liquidising the sauce (or use other roasted veg).
