Squash & Spinach Pasta Rotolo

INGREDIENTS

1 x Butternut squash (roughly 1.2kg)

1 x Red onion

1 x 50ml Olive oil

1 x Teaspoon dried thyme

500g x Frozen spinach

1 x Whole nutmeg, for grating

4 x Cloves of garlic

700ml  x Jar of passata

6 x Large fresh free-range pasta sheets (roughly 15cm x 20cm each)

50g x Feta cheese

20g x Parmesan cheese

Optional:

1 x Few sprigs of fresh sage


METHOD

1) Preheat the oven to 180ºC/350ºF/gas 4.

2) Cook the squash whole on a roasting tray for around 1 hour 30 minutes, then remove from the oven.

3) Meanwhile, peel and roughly chop the onion, put it into a medium pan on a medium-low heat with a lug of oil, the thyme and a pinch of salt and pepper, and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

4) Stir in the frozen spinach, cover with a lid and allow to slowly cook for another 5 minutes, or until the liquid has evaporated, then remove from the heat.

5) Cut the squash in half, discard the seeds and skin, then mash up with a fork. Keeping them separate, season both the squash and spinach to perfection with salt, pepper and a grating of nutmeg.
6) Peel and finely slice the garlic, then put it into a shallow 28cm casserole pan on a medium heat with a splash of oil and fry for a couple of minutes, or until lightly golden.

7) Pour in the passata, then add a splash of water to the empty jar, swirl it around and pour it into the pan.

8) Bring to the boil, simmer for just 3 minutes, then season to perfection.
9) On a clean work surface, lay out the pasta sheets facing lengthways away from you.

10) Working quickly so your pasta doesn't dry out, brush them with water, then evenly divide and spread the squash over the sheets. 11) Sprinkle over the cooked spinach and crumble over the feta.

12) Roll up the sheets and cut each one into 4 chunks, then place side by side in the tomato sauce.

13) Finely grate over the Parmesan, then pick the sage leaves (if using), toss in a little oil and scatter over the top.#

14) Bake for 35 to 40 minutes at the bottom of the oven until golden and crisp.

15) Delicious served with a fresh green salad.

Hummus Canned

Makes 6 to 8 cups


INGREDIENTS

4 x 240g Tins chickpeas (drained weight) or use 500g of dried chickpeas soaked overnight and boiled in water for about 45min.

1 x 200g Tahini paste

2 x Lemons, quartered

8 x Cloves of garlic crushed

1 x 500ml Cold water (pre-boiled)

2 x Teaspoons of fresh cumin powder (optional)

1 x Teaspoon of salt (optional)


METHOD

1) Add the Tahini to a large mixing bowl.

2) Add the garlic.

3) Pre-warm and squeeze in the juice of the lemons (the tahini will tighten up).

4) Mix well and add water to make a loose paste.

5) Add the drained chickpeas and mix.

6) Use a handheld blender to blend and mix to a smooth paste.

7) Add more water if the paste is too stiff.

8) Add salt to taste and blend/mix again.

9) Add the cumin if using.

10)Transfer to disposable plastic cups and cover with food film.

11) Serve in a bowl with a topping of olive oil (add cumin, chilli or paprika for extra flavour).


Tip 1 Freezes well.

Tip2 Almost impossible to make in a normal blender.

Braised Cabbage

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

2 x Tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil or butter

6 x Slices bacon, cut into 1-inch strips

1 x Small onion, sliced

2 x Bay leaves

1 x Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

1 x Large head cabbage, cored and thinly sliced or shredded

1 x Bottle beer


METHOD

1. In a large pot, add the oil and cook the bacon until browned and crispy.

2. Add the onion and the bay leaves and season with salt and pepper.

3. Add the cabbage, stirring to mix.

4. Add the beer, season and cover.

5. Braise until the cabbage has wilted, stirring occasionally, about 30 minutes.

6. Remove the bay leaves and serve immediately.
