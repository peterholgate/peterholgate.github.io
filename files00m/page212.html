RECIPE

Greek Feast


INGREDIENTS

For the couscous:

300g x Couscous

2 x Mixed-colour peppers

1 x Fresh red chilli

4 x Spring onions

½ x Bunch of fresh dill

200g x Podded raw or frozen peas

1 x Small handful of black olives (stone in)

2 x Tablespoons extra virgin olive oil

40g x Feta cheese

For the chicken:

2 x 200g Skinless chicken breasts

1 x Heaped teaspoon dried oregano

1 x Teaspoon ground allspice

1  x Lemon

1 x 50ml Olive oil

For the tzatziki:

½ x Cucumber

250g x Natural yoghurt

½ x Lemon

½ x Bunch of fresh mint


METHOD

1) Put 1 mug of couscous and 2 mugs of boiling water into a bowl with a pinch of salt and cover.

2) On a large sheet of greaseproof paper, toss the chicken with salt, pepper, the oregano, allspice and finely grated lemon zest.

3) Fold over the paper, then bash and flatten the chicken to 1.5cm thick with a rolling pin.

4) Put into the frying pan with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, turning after 3 or 4 minutes, until golden and cooked through.
5) Using a box grater, coarsely grate the cucumber.

6) Sprinkle it with a good pinch of salt, then squeeze and scrunch with clean hands to get rid of the excess salty water.

7) Pop in a bowl with the yoghurt, juice of ½ a lemon and a pinch of pepper, finely chop and add the top leafy half of the mint, then mix together.

8) Remove the stalk and seeds from the peppers and chilli, then pulse in the processor with the trimmed spring onions and the dill until finely chopped.

9) Scatter over a large tray or platter
10) Add the peas to the veg (if using frozen peas, blanch them in boiling water for a couple of minutes first).

11) Destone and tear up the olives then squeeze over the juice of the zested lemon and add the extra virgin olive oil.

12) Fluff up and scatter over the couscous, toss well and season to taste.

13) Move the cooked chicken to a board, slice it up, then lay it around the couscous.

14) Crumble over the feta and serve with the tzatziki.

Moussaka Lamb or Beef Mince LCHF

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

10 x Tablespoons olive oil

6 x Garlic cloves, chopped

1 x Onion, chopped

2 x Cans of chopped tomatoes

1 x Tablespoon tomato puree

500g x Minced lamb or beef

1 x Glass of red wine

1 x Teaspoon dried oregano

1 x Cinnamon stick, about 10cm long

½ x Teaspoon of cumin seeds

3 x Aubergines, sliced

Salt and ground black pepper to taste

FOR THE WHITE SAUCE

75g x butter

75g x plain flour

500ml x Double cream

100g x Feta cheese, crumbled

1 x Egg yolk

1 x Pinch of nutmeg


METHOD

1) In a large pan, gently cook the garlic and onion in half the olive oil until the onion has softened.

2) In a frying pan, heat olive oil and fry the mince until browned then add the mince to the onion and garlic.

3) Add wine, puree, chopped tomatoes, oregano, cumin and cinnamon stick.

4) Simmer uncovered for 1 and a half hours, stirring occasionally. Remove the cinnamon stick and season to taste.

5) Preheat oven to 190C.

6) Fry the aubergine slices in olive oil until golden on both sides.

7) Season well with salt and black pepper.

8) Make the white sauce. To make, melt the butter in a pan. Add the flour, stirring until mixture forms a smooth paste.

9) Stir in the cream gradually, boil, then simmer for 10 minutes.

10) Take off the heat and stir in the nutmeg, egg yolk, and feta.

11) Layer aubergines into an ovenproof dish (30 x 20cm), followed by half the mince mixture.

12) Add another layer of aubergine, the rest of the mince and finally a final layer of aubergine.

13) Top with the white sauce.

14) Bake the moussaka, uncovered, for about 25-30.

15) Stand for 10-15 minutes and serve.

Greek Style Salad Dressing – LCHF

Makes about 1 Cup


INGREDIENTS

¾ x Cup of Olive Oil

2 x Tablespoons of Balsamic Vinegar

½ x Teaspoon of Thyme dried

½ x Teaspoon of Oregano dried

½ x Teaspoon of Basil dried

½ x Teaspoon of Marjoram dried

¼ x Teaspoon of Black Pepper

1 x Cayenne to taste

1 x Salt to taste

2 x Clove garlic crushed

3 x Tablespoons Water (Cold boiled)


METHOD

1) Add everything to a jar with a lid.

2) Shake well and serve.
