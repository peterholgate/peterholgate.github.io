RECIPE

Best Roast Turkey


INGREDIENTS

1 x 3 kg Turkey Crown

1 x 250g Stuffing

1 x Clementine, cut in half

1 x Fresh red chilli, whole

1 x Bunch of fresh rosemary (30g)

1 x Bunch of fresh sage (30g)

12 x Fresh bay leaves

1 x 250g Unsalted butter at room temperature

1 x Whole nutmeg, for grating

1 x Onions, peeled and rough chopped

1 x Carrots, rough chopped

1 x Sticks of celery, rough chopped

6 x Cloves of garlic, separated but unpeeled


METHOD

1) Add the onions, the carrots and the celery, to the baking tray.

2) Add the garlic cloves.

3) Add the clementine and chilli.

4) Put the turkey crown (room temp) on top of the vegetables with most of the herbs.

5) Place half the stuffing around the edge of the vegetables.

6) Bake the other half of the stuffing in an oven dish.

7) Warm the butter in your hands and spread all over the bird.

8) Generously sprinkle the turkey from all sides with sea salt and black pepper.

9) Pick over the remaining herb leaves and pat them on to the butter.

10) Finely grate over a nice coating of nutmeg.

11) Preheat the oven to 240C.

12) Cover the turkey snugly with tin foil and place it on top of the trivet in the tray.

13) Turn the oven down to 180C.

14) Cook for 34-40mins per kg (2 hours for a 3kg crown).

Chicken Supreme 2

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1L x Milk

1 x Large onion, sliced

4 x Chicken breast fillets, diced

1/2 x Bottle white wine

500g x Button mushrooms, sliced (optional)

100g x Butter

100g x Flour

1 x Tablespoon chopped parsley

1 x Salt and pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Pour the milk into a milk pan and gently heat.

2) Cook the sliced onion in a large frying pan (wok if you have one) until soft.

3) Add the diced chicken and cook until lightly coloured, then drain off excess fat.

4) Add the 1/2 bottle white wine, flame to burn off alcohol (flambé). 5) Add the sliced mushrooms.

6) Reduce the heat and leave to cook whilst you make the béchamel.

To make the bechamel (white sauce):

7) Melt the margarine in a pan.

8) Add the flour and mix in.

9) Cook for a few minutes over a gentle heat without colour (roux). 10) Gradually add the warmed milk, stirring in a little at a time until you get a smooth thick sauce.

11) Add the bechamel to the chicken and stir well.

12) Add the chopped parsley and serve.

13) Serve with new potatoes and seasonal veg or boiled rice.

Chicken with Parma Ham

Serves 2


INGREDIENTS

4 x 125g Chicken breasts, skinned

3x Tablespoons of pesto (red or green)

4 x Sun-dried tomatoes, drained of oil and sliced

8 x Thin slices Parma ham (or similar cured ham)

1 x Tablespoon olive oil


METHOD

1) First preheat the oven to 200ºC.

2) Spread the chicken breasts out on a board and, with a sharp knife, make a small slit in the top of each one to create a 'pocket'.

3) Spread the inside of the pocket with the classic pesto.

4) Layer the sun-dried tomato slices onto each chicken breast.

5) Wrap each chicken breast with two slices of Parma ham and put them on a baking sheet.

6) Drizzle each one with oil and bake them in the oven for 20-25 mins or until the chicken is cooked through.

7) Serve.
