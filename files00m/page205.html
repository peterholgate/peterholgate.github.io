Lamb Spinach Karahi Karachi

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x 200g Ghee/Clarified Butter

4 x Medium Onions Roughly Chopped

1 x 400g Can Chopped Toms

1 x 120ml Water

1 x 65g (1.5 Pods) Garlic crushed

1 x 50g (10cm of a thick root) Fresh Ginger, peeled and finely chopped

1 x 900g Boneless leg or shoulder of lamb cut into 3-4cm cubes

1 x Tablespoon Salt

1 x Tablespoon Ground Turmeric

1 x Tablespoon Red Chilli Powder

1 x Tablespoon Paprika

1 x Tablespoon Ground Coriander

1 x Tablespoon Ground Cumin (or add as seeds when cooking onion)

1 x 350g Frozen Spinach

3 x Tablespoons Fresh Coriander (chopped)

2 x Medium sized Green Chillies with stalks removed

½ x Tablespoon Garam Masala

A pinch of ground cumin and freshly ground black pepper (to serve)

3 x Tablespoons Fresh Coriander (chopped) (to serve)


METHOD

1)  Heat the ghee in a large, heavy based pan. Add the onions and cook over a medium heat, stirring now and then, for 20 minutes or until they are soft and a light brown.

2) Remove the fried onions with a slotted spoon, leaving the ghee, and put them in a liquidiser.

3) Add the tomatoes, water, ginger and garlic to the liquidizer and blend to a smooth puree.

4) Add the puree to the ghee left in the pan.

5) Add the lamb and salt then simmer for 30 minutes, until the lamb is half cooked and the sauce is well reduced.

5) Stir in the ground turmeric, chilli powder, cumin, paprika and coriander.

6) Simmer for 30-45 minutes for shoulder or 45-60min for leg, until the lamb is tender, adding a little water now and then if the sauce starts to stick.

6) Put half (175g) of the defrosted spinach leaves into a liquidiser and blend to a smooth puree. Set aside.

7) Rinse out the liquidiser and add the green chillies and 2-3 tablespoons of water and blend until smooth. Set aside.

8) When the lamb is cooked, there should be a layer of ghee floating on the top of the curry.

9) Stir in the spinach puree and the remaining spinach leaves and cook for 2 minutes.

10) Add green chilli puree (hot) to taste.

11) Simmer for 2 minutes more.

12) Stir in the Garam Masala.

13) Sprinkle with ground cumin, freshly ground black pepper and fresh Coriander just before you take it to the table.


Tip1: Serve with rice, naan breads or parathas etc.

Tip2: Even better on day 2!

Beef Rendang Slow Cooker

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x Onion finely diced

1 x Tablespoon garlic and ginger paste

1 x Heaped tablespoon tomato puree

1 x Heaped tablespoon lemongrass paste

1 x Heaped tablespoon garam masala

1 x Teaspoon of chilli flakes

1/2 x Teaspoon turmeric

1 x Teaspoon cinnamon

1 x Beef stock cube

3 x Tablespoons kecap manis

60g x Desiccated coconut toasted

1 x 400g tin coconut milk

800g x Beef Shin or Cheek cubed (approx. 2 cheeks)

5 or 6 x Lime leaves (Optional)

1 x Juice of lime

1 Tablespoon sugar


METHOD

1) Put all the ingredients except the lime juice and sugar into your slow cooker pot.

2) Cook on the high setting for four hours (or the low setting for eight).

3) When cooked add the lime juice and sugar to taste then serve with a portion of rice.


Tip1: If you like a drier curry then cook on high for a further hour with the lid ajar.

Thai Coconut Chicken Soup Slow Cooker

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x 450g Boneless, skinless chicken breasts

4 x Cups (250ml) chicken broth

1 x Can (400g) coconut milk

2 x Tablespoons red curry paste

1 x Tablespoon fish sauce

1 onion, diced

2 x Garlic cloves, minced

1 x Red, bell pepper, sliced

1 x Lime, juiced

¼ x Bunch of fresh coriander for garnish



METHOD

1) Add chicken, broth, coconut milk, curry paste, fish sauce, onion, garlic, and bell pepper to the Crockpot.

2) Cook on low for 6-7 hours or high for 3-4 hours until the chicken is cooked through.

3) Shred the chicken and stir in the lime juice.

4) Serve garnished with fresh cilantro.


Tip1: Add Thai Basil, lemon grass and galanga for more Thai flavour
