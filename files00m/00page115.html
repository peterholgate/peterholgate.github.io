Pork with Lentils

Serves 8


INGREDIENTS

12 x Tablespoon of melted beef dripping (or olive oil)

1 x Teaspoon ground black pepper

1/8 x Teaspoon cayenne pepper

1 ½ x Teaspoon Herbes de Provence (or dried mixed herbs to taste)

2 x Bay leaves (Med)

4 x Onion medium, Spanish sliced coarsely

4 x Cloves garlic, rough chopped

4 x Carrots medium, sliced into large chunks

1.2kg x Lean pork (leg or similar) cut into 3 x 3 cm cubes

300g x Red lentils (split), washed and drained

1.25 x Litres of chicken stock or use cubes

1.25 x Litres of boiling water (if not using chicken stock)

1 x Tablespoon of fresh parsley, chopped


METHOD

1) Add chicken stock cubes to boiling water and mix well or use.

2) Pour dripping or oil into large heavy pot (Do NOT use pressure cooking).

3) Add the pork cayenne pepper and half of the black pepper.

4) Cook at high heat until outside is seared white/brown.

5) Add the onion.

6) Cook at medium heat until the onion is transparent.

7) Add carrots and cook for 2 min.

8) Add chicken stock and and washed lentils, mix.

9) Place bay leaves on top and put heavy lid on.

10) Cook for 90mins at 180C.

11) Test at 90mins lentils and pork must be soft and well cooked.

12) Cook for a further 30 mins or until pork is soft.

13) Mix well and sprinkle on the remaining black pepper.

14) Sprinkle the Herbes de Provence on top.

15) Remove lid and cook at 200C for a further 5 mins.

16) Garnish with chopped parsley and serve.


Tip1 Add 2 x rough chopped green peppers (Capsicums) as a variation

Tip2 Good served with new potatoes in butter and garnished with parsley

Pork Loin with Apple and Cider Gravy

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

1.8kg piece of rolled and tied pork loin with the skin scored

2 garlic cloves, sliced into thin slivers

1 x Small bunch rosemary, broken into small sprigs

3 x Bay leaves, torn

1 x Onion, roughly chopped

1 x Large carrot, chopped

1 x Bramley apple, peeled, quartered, cored and roughly sliced

1 x Tablespoons of sunflower oil

2 x Tablespoons of plain flour

100ml x Good quality cider

500ml x Vegetable or chicken stock


METHOD

1) If you have time, rub salt in the pork skin 2 hours before cooking and leave it uncovered in the fridge.

2) Heat the oven to 230C/210C fan/gas 8. Turn the pork rind-side down and with a small knife make about 6 deep incisions along the meat.

3) Poke a sliver of garlic, a piece of rosemary and bay in each incision and turn the pork the right way up.

4) If you didn’t salt the pork earlier salt the skin now.

5) Mix the carrot, onion and apple and scatter along the middle of a shallow roasting tray to make a bed for the pork to sit on.

6) Sit the pork on the vegetables and rub the skin with the oil. Place the pork in the oven and leave for 15 mins then turn the heat down to 180C/160C fan/gas 4 and continue to roast for 1hr 30 mins.

7) If after this time you don’t have brilliant crackling turn the heat up again and check every 5 mins until the skin has crackled.

8) Remove the pork from the roasting tray to a board to rest and pour off all but about 2 tbsp of fat from the tray.

9) If your tray is robust enough to go on the heat then do so, if not scrape the contents into a shallow saucepan and place on the heat.

10) Stir the flour in with the veg and cook until you have a mushy, dark amber paste then splash in the cider and bubble down to a thick paste again.

11) Pour in the stock and simmer everything for 8-10 mins until you have thickened gravy.

12) Strain the sauce into another saucepan pushing as much puréed apple as you can through the sieve.

13) Simmer again and season to taste.

14) Carve the pork loin with crackling attached into slices using a serrated knife and serve with your favourite vegetables and the apple and cider gravy.

Slow Cooker Pork Casserole

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

4 x Tablespoons of beef dripping (or use oil)

4 x Pork shoulder steaks (about 750g), cut into large chunks

1 x Large onion, chopped

1 x Large leek, cut into 2cm slices

1 x Large carrot, chopped into 1cm cubes

1 x Bundle of woody herbs (bouquet garni) 2 bay leaves, 3 sage leaves and 4 thyme sprigs

2 x Cloves of garlic, crushed (optional)

1 x Chicken stock cube

2 x Teaspoons of Dijon mustard

1 x Tablespoons of cider vinegar

1 x Tablespoon cornflour

2 x Tablespoons honey

1 x Black pepper to taste

1 x Water to almost cover the slow cooker ingredients


METHOD

1) Heat your slow cooker.

2) Add the dripping (or oil) and black pepper to a wide frying pan over a high heat.

3) Add the pork to the hot pan avoiding overcrowding the meat (cook in batches).

4) Cook until deep brown all over, then transfer to the slow cooker.

5) Add dripping and the onion to the frying pan and cook for a few mins, until they soften then add to slow cooker.

6) Add dripping the leeks and the garlic to the frying pan and cook until the leeks soften then add to the slow cooker.

7) To the slow cooker, add the carrot, herbs, stock cube, mustard and vinegar, season, then add enough water to almost cover the ingredients.

8) Stir, then set your slow cooker on low for 6-8 hrs, or high for 5-6 hrs.

9) In a cup, mix the cornflour with 3-4 tablespoons of water and mix to a smooth paste then add to the slow cooker with stirring.

10) Add the honey and stir.

11) Serve with mash or dumplings, scattered with thyme leaves.
