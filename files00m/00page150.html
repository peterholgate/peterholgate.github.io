Amaretto and Cherry Gelato

Serves: 6 (Makes 1kg of Icecream)

Preparation Time: 10 Mins

Cooking Time: 4-5 Hours Freezing Time


INGREDIENTS

1 x 500ml double cream

1 x 395g tin condensed milk

2 x Teaspoons vanilla extract

1 x 60g Amaretti biscuits, roughly crushed

2–4 x Tablespoons cherry compot or cherry jam

3 x Tablespoons Amaretto Di Saronno  (optional)


METHOD:

1) Whisk all the ingredients together except for the Amaretto di Saronno, amaretti biscuits and cherry compot until the mix forms soft peaks, beat gently.

2) This forms the base of any ice-cream flavour and you can experiment by adding different flavours to this vanilla abase.

3) Next fold in most of the crushed biscuits (keeping some back to decorate the top) and the Amaretto di Saronno (if using).

4) Place in a freezable container to freeze for 1 hour.

5) After 1 hour, remove from freezer and swirl in the cherry compote making a ripple effect.

6) Top with the remaining amaretti biscuits and then return to the freezer for another 5 hours.

7) Serve.

Chocolate and Hazelnut Bacio Gelato

Serves: 6 (Makes 1kg of Icecream)

Preparation Time: 5 Mins

Cooking Time: 4-5 Hours Freezing Time


INGREDIENTS

1 x 500ml double cream

1 x 395g tin condensed milk

2 x Teaspoons vanilla extract

1 x 50g Coco powder

5 x Baci chocolates, 3 roughly chopped and 2 sliced (These are dark Italian chocolates with a hazelnut and truffle centre- Optional).

1 x 100g Whole hazelnuts

2 x Tablespoons icing sugar


METHOD:

1) First place a small frying pan on a medium heat along with the icing sugar.

2) Stir so that the nuts become coated in the icing sugar .

3) Heat for 5-10 minutes (moving the pan around every few minutes to make sure the nuts don’t catch or burn) until the icing sugar coats the hazelnuts and they start to go golden and caramelised.

4) Once golden place in a pestle and mortar, lightly bash into medium pieces and leave to one side.

5) Whisk to soft peaks the double cream, condensed milk and vanilla extract together which forms the base of any icecream flavour you want to make at home -  do not over beat.

6) Then whip in the cocoa powder, and then fold in most of the chopped baci and most of the hazelnuts (saving some for decorating on top)

7) Place in a freezable dish and top with the remaining hazelnuts, chopped and sliced baci and dust with a little cocoa powder to finish.

8) Freeze for at least 5 hours.

9) Serve.

Tiramisu

Serves: 6-8

Preparation Time: 25 Mins

Cooking Time: 1 Hour Chilling Time (or Even Better...overnight)


INGREDIENTS

3 x Medium eggs, preferably free- range or organic, separated

3 x Tablespoons caster sugar

1 x 250g Mascarpone cheese

2–3  x Tablespoons cocoa powder

1 x 250ml Marsala wine, in a shallow dish

1 x 250ml Strong black coffee (ideally espresso), in a shallow dish

1 x 200g Packet of sponge/lady finger biscuits (get savoiardi if possible)


METHOD

1) Prepare your cream mix: separate your egg yolks from your egg whites.

2) Beat the egg yolks with the sugar in a bowl or use a free-standing electric mixer until it goes creamy, light and fluffy.

3) In a large bowl, beat the egg whites until they form stiff peaks. 4) Add the mascarpone to the egg-yolk and mix to combine, and then gently fold in the egg whites – be careful not to lose the lovely light air in the cream mix.

5) Spread a thin, even layer of the mixture over the bottom of a serving dish approx. 22cm in diameter and 10cm deep (ideally a glass one).

6) Using a fine sieve, sprinkle a little cocoa powder over the top.

7) One by one, quickly dip each side of the biscuits first in the Marsala and then in the coffee but don’t let them absorb too much liquid, as they can get rather soggy.

8) Place a neat layer of the soaked biscuits on top of the cocoa powder.

9) Repeat this process, layering the creamy mixture, cocoa powder and soaked sponge biscuits, finishing with a layer of the creamy mixture.

10) Each creamy layer should just cover the biscuits; don’t spread it too thickly.

11) Ideally you want 4 or 5 layers.

12) Lightly dust with more cocoa powder and then chill in the fridge for at least 1 hour, and preferably overnight.


TIP: When you put each layer into the bowl, try to avoid any dribbles on the side of the glass so that your layers end up being pretty and neat.
