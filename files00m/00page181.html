Smoked Haddock Chowder

Serves 2


INGREDIENTS

2 x Knobs of butter

3 x Rashers of streaky bacon, chopped

1 x Onion, finely chopped

1 x Leek, sliced fine (or 5/6 spring onions)

2 x Tablespoons plain flour

600ml x Full cream milk (plus a little extra)

400g x Potatoes cut into small cubes

400g x Smoked skinned haddock fillets (about 2)

140g x Frozen sweetcorn

100g x Frozen peas

100ml x Fish stock (cube is fine)

1 x Freshly ground black pepper

1 x Pinch of cayenne pepper

1 x Chopped parsley, to serve


METHOD

1) Heat the butter in a large saucepan.

2) Tip in the bacon, then cook until starting to brown.

3) Add the onion, cook for 2-3 mins, then the leek and sweat until soft.

4) Sprinkle over the 2tsp flour and mix through and cook for 1 minute or so.

5) Pour over the milk bit by bit, stirring like mad to remove any lumps.

6) Add the stock and potatoes.

7) Bring to the boil, then simmer for 5 mins stirring regularly to avoid it sticking to the bottom of the pan.

8) Add the sweetcorn and peas and cook for 2-3 minutes, bringing back up to heat.

9) Add the haddock, then leave to gently cook for another 5-10 mins. 10) As it cooks, break it into large chunks.

11) Scatter over parsley, if using.

12) Serve with plenty of crusty bread.

Southern Baked Beans

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

2 x 400g Cans of Baked Beans

1 x 500g Ground beef

1 x 1/2 Small sweet onion, diced in small pieces or use 8 small sweet pickled silverskin onions

1 x Medium green bell pepper, diced in small pieces

2 x Slices of smoked bacon, halved

2 x Cloves of garlic

1 x Tablespoon of Olive Oil

1 x Tablespoon Worcestershire sauce

2 x Teaspoons prepared yellow mustard

1/2 x Cup dark brown sugar

¼ x Cup ketchup

1 x Teaspoon of Colombia style, sazon Goya with azafran


METHOD

1 ) Preheat Oven to 160C

2) Cook ground beef in a skillet over medium heat.

3) Chop the ground beef when it is cooked so that the meat is in very small pieces and set meat aside.

4) Cook onion and green bell pepper over medium heat with a tablespoon of olive oil for 4-5 minutes.

5) In a medium baking dish combine the cooked ground beef, onion and green bell pepper with all remaining ingredients.

6) Stir until ingredients are thoroughly combined.

7) Top the beans with the uncooked bacon and place into oven.

8) Bake for 45 minutes – stir the baked beans 1/2 way through the baking time.

9) Serve.

Fricoles de Antioquia

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

Beans:

1 x 500g Dried Pinto beans (or Cranberry beans)

1 x 400g Pork hocks (or unsmoked bacon)

1 x 1.5l Water

2 x Medium carrots, rough chopped (for blending later)

1 x Medium red onion, rough chopped (for blending later)

½ x Teaspoon salt or to your taste

1 x Green plantain, peeled and cut into 8mm slices (add later)

Sauce:

1 x Can diced tomatoes

4 x Chopped scallions (spring onions)

3 x Tablespoons olive oil

¼ x Teaspoon salt

4 x Garlic cloves, minced

½ x Cup chopped cilantro

1 x Bay leaf

2 x Tablespoons of tomato sauce

1 x Teaspoon ground of Sazon Goya (or ½ teaspoon of cumin)


METHOD

1) Wash the beans and soak overnight in cold water.

2) In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat, add the tomatoes, onions, scallions, salt, garlic, cilantro and ground cumin and cook for 10 to 15 minutes.

3) Place all the ingredients in the pressure Cooker, except the plantains.

4) Cook at high pressure for 25 minutes.

5) Remove from heat and let rest for 5 minutes. Release the steam, following the manufacturer's directions.

6) Separate the beans from the sauce and reserve (careful if hot).

7) Blend the sauce and blend to a smooth paste.

7) Add the paste back into the sauce.

8) Add the plantains, simmer them (about 5mins) with the lid off until tender.


Tip1: Make the day before and store in fridge overnight
