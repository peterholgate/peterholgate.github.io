Red Braised Pork Belly Slow Cooker

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

2 x tablespoons coconut oil

3cm x piece root ginger

1 x red onion, chopped

650g pork belly, cubed

3 x tablespoons cream sherry

600ml x chicken stock

3 x tablespoons soft brown sugar

2 x star anise

1 x bay leaf

1 x teaspoon dark soy sauce

1 x Black Pepper to taste


METHOD

1) Preheat the slow cooker to high setting.

2) In a wok over a high heat, warm the oil then add the root ginger and red onion and lower the heat.

3) Cook and stir for 2 minutes.

4) Then add the cubed pork and fry for a few minutes until browned.

5) Add the sherry, stock, sugar, salt, star anise, bay leaf and soy sauce and bring to a simmer.

6) Transfer to the slow cooker.

7) Slow cook on high for 3 to 4 hours topping up with stock if necessary.

8) When the pork is tender, remove from the slow cooker and serve.


Tip1 Also known as Hongshao Rue

Curried Aubergine and Chickpeas LCHF


INGREDIENTS

2 x Aubergines

2 x Tablespoon ghee

1 x Tablespoon brown or black mustard seeds

10-12  x Curry leaves, plus extra to serve, if you like

2 x Onions, finely chopped

2  x Dried chillies, chopped

4 x Teaspoon garam masala

2 x Teaspoon ground coriander

2 x Teaspoon turmeric

400ml x Coconut milk

6 x Tomatoes, quartered

400g x Chickpeas, rinsed and drained


METHOD

1) Halve the aubergines, then cut each half into wedges.

2) Heat ½ ghee in a large pan, preferably non-stick, and brown half the aubergines for 2-3 mins on each side until golden brown and crisp all over.

3) Scoop onto a plate, repeat with the remaining aubergines.

4) Add the remaining ghee to the pan with the mustard seeds and curry leaves and fry for 30 secs until fragrant.

5) Stir in the onions and continue cooking until they are softened and beginning to brown.

6) Add the dried chillies and spices with a spoonful of thick coconut milk, then fry for 1 min more.

7) Add the remaining coconut milk, tomatoes and half a can of water.

8) Simmer for 25-30 mins until thick and saucy.

9) Stir in the chickpeas and aubergines. Continue simmering for 5 mins or so, until everything is hot and the aubergines are tender.

10) Serve with rice or warm naan bread, scattered with a few extra curry leaves sizzled in a splash of oil, if you like.

Coconut Lime Fish Soup

Serves 4


Ingredients

For the Soup Base:

1 X Tablespoon coconut oil or vegetable oil

1 x Medium onion, finely chopped

3 x Garlic cloves, minced

1 x Tablespoon ginger, grated

2 x Stalks lemongrass, bruised (optional, for added fragrance)

2–3 x Cups fish stock or chicken stock

1 x Can (400ml) coconut milk

1 x Medium carrot, thinly sliced

1 x Rd bell pepper, sliced

1 x Small zucchini, sliced into rounds

For the Fish:

500-600g x Firm white fish fillets (cod, tilapia, or snapper), cut into bite-sized pieces

2 x Juice of limes

1 x Salt and pepper, to taste

For Seasoning:

1 x Tablespoon fish sauce (optional, for umami)

1 x Teaspoon red curry paste or chili flakes (adjust to spice preference)

1 x Teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance flavors)

1 x Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish

1 x Lime in wedges, for serving


Method

Marinate the Fish:

1) In a bowl, combine the fish pieces, lime juice, a pinch of salt, and pepper.

2) Toss to coat and set aside while preparing the soup.

Prepare the Soup Base:

3) Heat coconut oil in a large pot over medium heat.

4) Add the onion and sauté until soft and translucent (about 3–5 minutes).

5) Stir in the garlic, ginger, and lemongrass. Cook for another 2 minutes until fragrant.

6) Add the Stock and Coconut Milk

7) Pour in the fish stock and bring to a gentle simmer.

8) Add the coconut milk, stirring to combine.

9) Toss in the carrot, red bell pepper, and zucchini. Let the soup simmer for 8–10 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but still vibrant.

Season the Soup:

10) Stir in the fish sauce, red curry paste (or chili flakes), and sugar (if using).

11) Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

Cook the Fish:

12) Add the marinated fish pieces to the simmering soup. Ensure they are fully submerged.

13) Cover and cook for 5–7 minutes, or until the fish is opaque and cooked through being careful not to overcook.

14) Remove the lemon grass and garnish with fresh coriander leaves

15) Serve with lime wedges and steamed jasmine rice or crusty bread.


Tip1 For a richer soup, add an extra half-can of coconut milk.

Tip2 Add sliced fresh chili or a splash of hot sauce for a spicier kick.

Tip3 If the soup tastes too tangy, add a pinch of sugar to balance the lime’s acidity.

Tip4 Add shrimp, scallops, or mussels for a seafood-packed version.

Tip5 Replace fish with tofu or chickpeas and use vegetable stock.

Tip6 Add fresh Thai basil or mint for a unique flavour profile.

Tip7 Store leftover soup in an airtight container for up to 2 days in a fridge; rewarm gently, don’t boil.
