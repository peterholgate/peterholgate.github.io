Coconut Flour Pie Crust


INGREDIENTS

1/2 x Cup coconut oil or melted butter

2 x Eggs

1/4 x Teaspoon sea salt (optional)

3/4 x Cup coconut flour (or use 50% almond flour)

1-3 x Tablespoons raw honey (if sweet pastry required)


METHOD

1) Preheat oven to 200 deg C.

2) In a medium bowl beat butter, eggs, honey and salt together with a fork.

3) Add coconut flour and stir until dough holds together.

4) Gather the dough into a ball, then pat into a 9″ (22cm) greased pie pan.

5) Prick the dough with a fork.

6) Bake for 8-10 minutes or until lightly brown then cool.

Best Meat Stuffing


INGREDIENTS

2 x Onions, peeled and finely chopped

2 x Leeks, trimmed and finely chopped (use white part only)

1 x Bunch of fresh sage (30g)

1 x Olive oil

1 x 50g Unsalted butter

4 x Rashers of smoked streaky bacon

1 x Whole nutmeg, for grating

1 x 400g Stale bread

1 x 200g Vac-packed chestnuts

1 x 1kg Minced pork shoulder

1 x 400g Tin of peaches, in juice

1 x Clementine, halved


METHOD

1) Pick the sage leaves, keep 2 nice big ones aside, then finely slice the rest.

2) Place a large frying pan on a medium heat with 1 tablespoon of oil and the butter.

3) Finely slice and add the bacon, fry until lightly golden.

4) Stir in the sliced sage, followed by the onions and leeks.

5) Finely grate in half the nutmeg.

6) Add a good pinch of sea salt and black pepper.

7) Cook for 15 minutes, or until soft, stirring occasionally. Leave to cool.

8) Toast the bread, then whiz it to crumbs in a food processor with the chestnuts and tip into a large bowl.

9) Add the cooled onion mixture, the minced pork shoulder and drained peaches.

10) Using your hands, really squash and squidge everything until well mixed.

11) Put 250g of the stuffing mixture aside to cook with your turkey.

12)  Pack the rest into an appropriately sized oiled baking dish.

13) Flatten it down and use your hands to almost tuck the stuffing into the dish, so it mounds up in the middle.

14) Push the clementine halves into the top of the stuffing, cut side up.

15) Place 1 reserved sage leaf on each half.

16) Drizzle with 1 more tablespoon of oil.

17) Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for 50 minutes, or until golden, gnarly and cooked through.

18) Serve.

Stuffing


INGREDIENTS

2 x Large onions, peeled and quartered

50g x Stale bread

200g vx Vac-packed chestnuts

1kg x Shoulder of pork, trimmed and diced

1 x Bunch of fresh sage, leaves picked

3 x Rashers of smoked streaky bacon, roughly chopped

1 x Freshly ground white pepper to taste

1 x Sea salt to taste

1 x Fresh whole nutmeg, for grating

1 x Lemon

1 x Orange or clementine


METHOD

1) Preheat your oven to 190ºC/375ºF/gas 5.

2) Blitz the onions in the food processor until finely chopped, then tip into a large bowl.

3) Tear the stale bread into small chunks and whiz into breadcrumbs. 4) Add these to the bowl, then crush and crumble your chestnuts in there too.

5) Tip your diced pork into the food processor with the sage leaves, bacon, a level teaspoon of white pepper and a good pinch of salt.

6) Finely grate in a quarter of the nutmeg, the zest of half a lemon and just 2 or 3 gratings of orange or clementine zest.

7) Pulse until you've got some chunks and some mush, it won't even take a minute, then tip into the mixing bowl.2.

8) Because the pork is raw, you're committed to seasoning it well so add another pinch of salt and white pepper, then get your clean hands in there and scrunch it all up until well combined.

9) Take just under half of the stuffing out of the bowl to use for your turkey, then transfer the rest to a lovely earthenware-type dish that you can serve from.

10) Use your hands to break it up and push it about, then flatten it all down.

11) Pop it in the oven to cook for 50 minutes to 1 hour until bubbling and crispy.

12) If you're doing it as part of your Christmas lunch, you want to get it on about the same time as your spuds.

13) When done, you can pour away any excess fat before serving if you want to.

14) It will be soft, juicy and succulent on the inside, then gnarly, crispy and chewy on the outside.

15) Serve
