Baked Cod in Bacon LCHF

Serves 2


INGREDIENTS

2 x Sprigs of fresh rosemary

50ml x Olive oil

1 x Sea salt

1 x Freshly ground pepper

2 x Chunky cod fillets, skin on, scaled and boned removed (200g each)

8 x Rashers of smoked streaky bacon


METHOD

1)Preheat your oven to 230 C.

2) Pick the leaves from your rosemary and finely chop them on a chopping board. Drizzle olive oil over the chopped rosemary and sprinkle salt and pepper.

3) Season the cod fillets by rolling them around the chopping board until they’re evenly coated in the herbs and seasoning.

4) Lay half your rashers of bacon on the board next to each other, letting them overlap then place a cod fillet across them, and wrap the rashers around it.

5) Repeat with the second cod fillet and the remaining rashers.

6) Drizzle baking try with some olive oil, rub all over and lay the cod fillets in the tray, skin down.

7) Put a sprig of fresh rosemary on top of each fillet and drizzle with a little olive oil.

8) Place the baking tray in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, until the bacon is golden and the fish is cooked through.

9) If the bacon does not go brown finish under a hot grill.

10) Serve.


Tip1 Serve with some garlicky sauteed cherry tomatoes which I thought went excellently with it.

Tip2 Creamy mashed potatoes would go very well with it too.

Cauliflower with Salmon and Cheese

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x Large cauliflower, cut into florets

50g x Butter, plus extra for greasing

50g x Plain flour

450ml x Milk

2 x Free-range egg yolks

1 Teaspoon x English mustard

1 x Splash of Worcestershire sauce

1 x Splash of Chilli sauce

2 x Cloves of Garlic, crushed

1 x Salt to taste

1 x Black pepper to taste

200g x Fresh salmon, thinly sliced

125g x Cheddar cheese, grated

75g x Emmental cheese, grated


METHOD

1) Preheat oven to 200C.

2) Bring a large pan of salted water to the boil, then add the cauliflower and cook for two minutes (or steam).

3) Drain and place in a buttered ovenproof dish.

4) Place the butter and flour into a saucepan and heat to melt the butter.

5) Cook, stirring for one to two minutes until lightly golden.

6) Remove from the heat and gradually whisk in the milk to form a smooth sauce.

7) Return to the heat and simmer for two to three minutes.

8) Remove from the heat and beat in the egg yolks, mustard and Worcestershire sauce and garlic.

9) Add the salmon into the sauce, stirring carefully.

10) Season with salt and pepper to taste.

11) Pour over the cauliflower and top with the grated cheeses.

12) Place in the oven and cook for 10-15 minutes, until golden brown. Serve immediately.


Tip: Add wholemeal breadcrumbs for a crunchy top
