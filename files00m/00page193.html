Aloo Gobi

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

2 x Medium potatoes, cut into 2cm cubes and store in cold water

1 x 400g cauliflower (gobi), chopped into 4cm florets and store in warm water

2 x Medium onion, chopped finely

2 x Medium tomatoes, finely chopped (or 2 x tablespoons tomato paste)

2 x Tablespoons ginger and garlic minced (or paste)

2 x Green chillies slit or chopped (optional)

5 x Tablespoons coriander leaves or stalks chopped finely

Salt to taste

4 x Tablespoons oil (or ghee for non-veg)

1 x Teaspoon cumin seeds (jeera)

1/2 x Teaspoon turmeric

2 x Teaspoons garam masala

2 x Teaspoons coriander powder

2 x Teaspoon red chili powder (or less!)

2 x Teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) (optional)


METHOD

1) Heat oil in a pan, add the cumin seed and cook until it sizzles.

2) Add the ginger garlic paste & saute for 30 seconds.

3) Add the onions & stir fry until transparent.

4) Add the potatoes and green chilli and stir fry for 2 to 3 mins.

5) Cover and cook on a low to medium heat until the potatoes are half done.

6) Add the cauliflower & stir fry for about 3 mins.

7) Add all the spice powders and mix well.

8) Cook covered until the cauliflower is almost fork tender (not soggy!)

9) Add the salt, tomatoes, more oil (if required) and kasuri methi

10) Fry for 4 minutes on a medium flame until the raw smell of tomatoes goes away.

11) Finally garnish aloo gobi with coriander leaves

12) Serve with rice, roti or any bread.

Roasted Aloo Gobi

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

400g x Potatoes (such as Maris Piper or King Edward), cut into medium-sized chunks

1 x Large cauliflower, cut into florets

1 x Tablespoon of cumin seeds

2 x Teaspoons of coriander seeds

2 x Teaspoons nigella seeds

1 x Teaspoons ground cinnamon

1 x Teaspoons turmeric

1 x Teaspoons chilli powder

4 x Tablespoon vegetable oil or sunflower oil or rapeseed oil

8 x Curry leaves

4 x Garlic cloves, crushed

2 x 400g Cans chopped tomatoes

2 x Small green chillies, pierced a few times

1 x Teaspoons golden caster sugar

1 x Lime, juiced

1 x Small pack coriander, chopped


METHOD

1) Heat oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4.

2) In a large pan, simmer the potatoes for 5-6 mins until starting to soften.

3) Drain well.

4) On a large baking tray, toss the potatoes and cauliflower with the spices and 2 tbsp oil.

5) Season well and roast for 45 mins until veg is soft and brown, stir halfway through cooking.

6) Meanwhile, heat the remaining oil in a large pan.

7) Fry the curry leaves and garlic for 1 min, making sure the garlic doesn’t brown.

8) Add the tomatoes, chillies, sugar, lime juice and some seasoning.

9) Cover with a lid and simmer for 15 mins until the tomatoes have broken down.

10) Add the roasted veg to the tomatoes.

11) Simmer for 5 mins, adding a splash of water if the curry gets too thick.

12) Stir through the coriander and serve with rice, warm naan and yogurt.

Chicken Karahi Karachi

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x 200g Ghee/Clarified Butter

4 x Medium Onions Roughly Chopped

1 x 400g Can Chopped Toms

1 x 120ml Water (add more if needed during cooking)

1 x 65g (1.5 Pods) Garlic rough chopped

1 x 50g (10cm of a thick root) Fresh Ginger, peeled and rough chopped

1 x 900g Boneless chicken breast cut into 3-4cm cubes

1 x Tablespoon Salt

1 x Teaspoon Red Chilli

Powder

1 x Tablespoon Paprika

1 x Tablespoon Ground Coriander

1 x Tablespoon Ground Cumin (or add as seeds when cooking onion)

1 x Tablespoon Ground Turmeric

2 x Medium sized Green Chillies with stalks removed

½ x Tablespoon Garam Masala

1 x Pinch of ground cumin (to serve)

1 x Pinch and freshly ground black pepper (to serve)

1/2 x Bunch of Fresh Coriander (chopped) (to serve)


METHOD

1)  Heat the ghee in a large, heavy based pan (or pressure cooker), add the onions and cook at medium heat, stirring now and then, for 20 to 30minutes or until they are soft and a light brown.

2) Remove the fried onions with a slotted spoon, leaving the ghee and blend to a smooth puree in a liquidiser, with half of the water, then set aside.

3) Add the tomatoes, remaining water, ginger and garlic to the liquidizer and blend to a smooth puree, then set aside.

4) Add the ground turmeric, chilli powder, cumin, paprika, salt and coriander to the ghee and fry for about 2minutes.

5) Add the chicken and cook in the spices, stirring for 3-4mins.

6) Now add the pureed onions and tomatoes etc to the pan.

7) Simmer for 30-40 minutes until the chicken is tender, adding a little water now and then if the sauce starts to stick or cook in a pressure cooker for 15mins.

8) Rinse the liquidiser and add the green chillies and 2-3 tablespoons of water and blend until smooth. Set aside.

9) When the chicken is cooked, there should be a layer of ghee floating on the top of the curry.

10) Optionally add cauliflower (simmer 5 mins) or pre-blanched spinach (cook 2 mins) at this stage.

11) Add green chilli puree (hot) to taste.

12) Simmer for 2 minutes more.

13) Stir in the Garam Masala.

14) Sprinkle with ground cumin, freshly ground black pepper and fresh Coriander just before you take it to the table.


Tip1: Serve with rice, naan breads or parathas etc.

Tip2: Stop at stage 9 and store or freeze; even better on day 2!

Tip3: Use boneless chicken thighs (add 5mins to the cooking time)
