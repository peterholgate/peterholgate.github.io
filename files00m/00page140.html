Antipasto Clarita

Serves 4


INGREDIENTS

1 x 200g Can tuna in oil

1 x 100g of Black pitted olives

1 x 100g of Green pitted olives

1 x 100g of Capers

1 x 200g of Roasted mixed Peppers (Capsicums) in oil

1 x Small red onion

1 x 200g Tomato ketchup

2 x Tablespoons of  Hot chilli sauce

1 x 200g of Artichoke hearts

1 x 100ml of Extra Virgin olive oil


METHOD

1) Mash the Tuna with the oil in the can.

2) Cut olives in half.

3) Cut the peppers into 2cm wide strips.

4) Chop artichokes into 2 cm square pieces.

5) Fine chop the red onions.

6) Drain the capers.

7) Mix the tomato and chilli sauce.

8) Mix all the ingredients together with Olive Oil.

9) Keep cool and serve.

Celery and Lime Bruschetta Italian

Serves: 6

Preparation Time: 5 Mins

Cooking Time: 5 Mins


INGREDIENTS

4 x Large sticks of celery

2 x Garlic cloves, peeled

6 x Tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to drizzle if needed

1 X Juice of 1⁄2 a lime

1 x Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

6 x Thick slices of ciabatta or 1 baguette


METHOD:

1) The most important thing when it comes to a bruschetta is the bread – it should be really hard and crispy and only starts to soften where the olive oil and flavours ooze into the cracks.

2) Slice your ciabatta or baguette slices into diagonal slices approximately 1.5cm width.

3) Then toast them until golden and crunchy using a toaster or drizzle the bread with a little olive oil and brown in a frying pan over a medium heat.

4) Put 3 sticks of celery and the garlic in a food processor and blend to a purée and scrape into a bowl.

5) Finely chop the remaining stick of celery and leave to one side.

6) Add the oil and lime juice to the puréed celery, then season, to taste, with salt and pepper and stir together to combine.

7) Spread each slice of bread with a tablespoon of the celery mixture and garnish with a sprinkling of the finely chopped celery.

8) Serve.


TIP:  It’s great if you prepare your toasted bread a few days in advance and then leave them in an open biscuit tin to air dry and get a little harder.  Once you’re happy with how crunchy they are, seal them in an airtight container until you’re ready to use.

Peas with Buffalo Mozzarella Bruschetta Italian

Serves: 6

Preparation Time: 5 Mins

Cooking Time: 10 Mins


INGREDIENTS

1 x 250g Peas

8 x Fresh mint leaves

1 x Teaspoon extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to drizzle if needed

1 x Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

2 x Tablespoons Salsa Verde, plus extra to drizzle

6 x Thick slices of ciabatta or baguette

1 x 125g Buffalo mozzarella


METHOD

1) The most important thing when it comes to a bruschetta is the bread – it should be really hard and crispy and only starting to soften where the olive oil and flavours ooze into the cracks.

2) So slice your ciabatta or baguette slices into diagonal slices approximately 1.5cm width.

3) Then toast them until golden and crunchy using a toaster or drizzle the bread with a little olive oil and brown in a frying pan over a medium heat.

4) Put half the peas and all the mint in a blender and blend to a purée.

5) Scrape into a bowl then add, to the bowl, the remaining whole peas, the oil, a little salt and pepper, and the salsa verde.

6) Mix well to combine.

7) Spread each slice of bread with a tablespoon of the pea mixture. 8) Tear the mozzarella with your hands and arrange on top.

9) Sprinkle with a little more salt and pepper, to taste.

10) Place the bruschettas on a baking tray and grill for 1 or 2 minutes, until the cheese has melted.

11) Drizzle with a little extra Salsa Verde, Classic Pesto or Balsamic Glaze just before serving.


TIP:  It’s great if you prepare your toasted bread a few days in advance and then leave them in an open biscuit tin to air dry and get a little harder.  Once you’re happy with how crunchy they are, seal them in an airtight container until you’re ready to use.
