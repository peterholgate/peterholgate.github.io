French Onion Soup

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

1 x Tablespoon Extra Virgin Olive Oil

8 x Large onions peeled and cut into 6-7mm slices (core out the top and root)

1 x 125g Chopped shallots

1 x 25g Unsalted butter not salted

1 x 170g Plain flour

2 x Large Leeks halve lengthways and cut into 6-7mm slices (white and light green only)

1 x 60ml Cream sherry

1 x 60ml Ruby port

1 x 750ml Beef stock (3 Knorr type cubes)

1 x 750ml Vegetable or chicken stock (3 Knorr type cubes)

1 x Salt and black pepper to taste

1 x French stick cut into 12mm slices

1 x 64g Swiss cheese (eg Emmental)

1 x 64g Grated parmesan reggiano


METHOD

1) In a large fry pan heat the oil and a tablespoon of butter when hot add the onions and cook at high heat to caramelize, until golden brown.

2) Add the shallots and cook until light golden brown as well. This could take 15 to 20 minutes.

3) In a large Soup Pot, add the butter and when melted add the flour and make a soft roux.

4) Add the caramelized onion and shallots, add the leeks and cook for 2minutes.

5) Add the stocks and salt and pepper then cook slowly for 60 to 90 minutes until onion are soft! 

6) Cut 9 to 12 slices of the French bread and toast them in the oven until light golden brown. 

7) Pour the soup in individual bowls and place 2 or 3 slices of the toasted bread on top.

8) Cover with the cheeses and bake another 5 minutes until cheese is brown.

9) Sprinkle with chives and serve.


Tip1: Make your own Chicken, Vegetable or Beef stocks.

Chicken and Sweetcorn Soup

Serves 6


INGREDIENTS

2 x Chicken stock cubes (or use 1 litre of chicken stock)

1 x 1 litre of water for above (see above)

1 x 198g Can of corn (green giant is the best)

1 x Tablespoon of corn flour (thickener)

1 x 60ml cold water for above

1 x Salt and pepper to taste

2 x Spring onions (or use a 10cm piece of leek, finely chopped)

1 x 250g Cooked chicken finely shredded

1 x Drizzle of sesame oil

2 x Fresh eggs


METHOD

1) To a large, deep pan, add the stock cubes to 1 litre of hot water

2) Liquidise (cream) half of the sweetcorn and add to pot

3) Add the remaining sweetcorn

4) Add cooked chicken (finely shredded) and the tin of creamed corn

5) Add salt pepper and allow to simmer for 10mins

6) Mix 1 table spoon of cornflour with 60mls of water and add to the soup (stirring) to thicken

7) For a thicker soup add more cornflour to your desired thickness

8) Simmer for 5 mins

9) Beat the two eggs and drizzle from a fork into the soup

10) Chop the spring onions and add to the soup

11) Finish with a drizzle of sesame oil and stir
