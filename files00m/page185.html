RECIPE

Sprout and Sweet Potato Salad (Hot)

Serves: 4-6

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes


INGREDIENTS

For the Roasted Vegetables:

1 x Small butternut squash, peeled and cubed

500g x Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved

2 x Medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed

3 x Tablespoons olive oil

1 x Salt and pepper, to taste

1 x Teaspoon dried thyme

For the Cranberry Glaze:

1/2 x Cup cranberry juice

1/4 x Cup dried cranberries

2 x Tablespoons honey or maple syrup

1 x Tablespoon balsamic vinegar

For the Finishing Touch:

120g x Goat cheese, crumbled

1/2 x Cup dried cranberries (for garnish)

1 x Tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (optional)


METHOD

Roast the Vegetables:

1) Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

2) In a large bowl, toss the butternut squash, Brussels sprouts, and sweet potatoes with olive oil, salt, pepper, and thyme until well coated.

3) Spread the vegetables on a large baking sheet in a single layer.

4) Roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until tender and caramelized.

Prepare the Cranberry Glaze:

5) While the vegetables are roasting, prepare the cranberry glaze. In a small saucepan, combine the cranberry juice, dried cranberries, honey (or maple syrup), and balsamic vinegar.

6) Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for about 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens into a glaze.

Assemble the Salad:

7) Once the vegetables are roasted, transfer them to a large serving bowl.

8) Drizzle the cranberry glaze over the roasted vegetables and gently toss to coat.

9) Sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese and extra dried cranberries on top.

10) Serve warm garnished with fresh parsley.

Coconut Flour Pie Crust


INGREDIENTS

1/2 x Cup coconut oil or melted butter

2 x Eggs

1/4 x Teaspoon sea salt (optional)

3/4 x Cup coconut flour (or use 50% almond flour)

1-3 x Tablespoons raw honey (if sweet pastry required)


METHOD

1) Preheat oven to 200 deg C.

2) In a medium bowl beat butter, eggs, honey and salt together with a fork.

3) Add coconut flour and stir until dough holds together.

4) Gather the dough into a ball, then pat into a 9″ (22cm) greased pie pan.

5) Prick the dough with a fork.

6) Bake for 8-10 minutes or until lightly brown then cool.

Simple Seeded Brown Bread


Prep: 10mins

Rise 1: 30mins

Rise 2: 2-4hours

Bake: 30mins


INGREDIENTS

500g x Strong brown bread flour

7g x Quick acting yeast

15g x Olive oil (or 1 tablespoon)

400ml x Warm water

10g x Fine salt

40ml x Warm water

60-120g x Your choice of seeds (try pumpkin, sunflower and poppy)


METHOD

1) Add the flour and yeast together in a bowl and mix well.

2) Add olive oil, 400ml warm water then mix with a spoon.

3) Cover with a close fitting plate and leave to rest in a warm place for 30mins.

4) Dissolve 10g fine salt in 40ml warm water and add to the dough along with your seeds and mix again until combined (optionally, you can your hands in to mix the seeds in well).

5) Cover, with a plate, and leave to rest for 2-4 hours, or until fully risen, in a warm place.

6) Before cooking, pre-heat your oven to 220C (fan) together with the Dutch Oven.

7) Sprinkle the base and sides of the Dutch Oven with plain flour, before adding the dough.

8) Pour the dough to a Dutch Oven (Don’t worry if the dough is very loose and wet).

9) Sprinkle the top with a little more flour.

10) Put in oven for 40mins or until the bread is baked and golden brown.

11) Remove and put on wire rack to cool.


Tip1: Try 120g chopped black and green olives instead of seeds.

Tip2: When cool slice, bag and freeze, makes good toast.

Tip3: If the bottom is not crusty when removed; return to the hot oven for a few more minutes.
